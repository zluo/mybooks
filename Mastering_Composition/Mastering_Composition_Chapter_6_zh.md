# 1 Photography as Art 摄影作为艺术
Throughout this book I have encouraged you to contemplate what you are trying to do through your photography. This may naturally lead you on to the question of photography's relationship to art. In particular, when can we call it art and when does it become "fi ne art photography?" I don't intend to answer these questions conclusively, but simply offer some of my thoughts. I believe that contemplating these issues can also help us put some perspective on what it is we are actually doing.

在本书中，我一直在鼓励你通过摄影来反思自己所要表达的内容。这自然会引导你思考摄影与艺术之间的关系。特别是，我们何时才能称其为艺术，何时才成为“纯艺术摄影”？我并不打算给出最终答案，只是想分享一些我的想法。我认为，反思这些问题也能帮助我们更清楚地认识到自己实际在做什么。

Collectively, dictionary definitions seem to suggest that an essential part of the production of a piece of art is the active, creative, and manipulative involvement of the artist: you can't have art without the artist. There is perhaps a subtle difference between art and design in this regard—art is whatever the artist says it is, with or without purpose or beauty, whereas design generally has an identifi able purpose.

> 总体而言，词典定义似乎表明，创作一件艺术品的一个基本要素是艺术家的主动、创造性和操控性参与：没有艺术家，就无法产生艺术。在这方面，艺术与设计之间或许存在微妙的差异——艺术可以是艺术家所定义的任何事物，无论是否有目的或美感，而设计通常具有明确的目的。

So when do photographers become artists? In purely depictive photography, composing the image is a very rigid process and is concerned with showing the entire subject or a part of it with maximum clarity. The goal is accuracy of depiction; a faithful representation of reality. There is likely to be little active, creative, manipulative involvement from the photographer. So, perhaps here the photographer remains more of a technician than an artist.

> 那么，摄影师何时才能成为艺术家呢？在纯粹的再现摄影中，构图是一个非常严格的过程，关注于以最大清晰度呈现整个主体或其部分。其目标是准确的再现，即忠实呈现现实。在此过程中，摄影师可能较少主动、创造性和操控性的参与。因此，或许在此类摄影中，摄影师更像一位技术员而非艺术家。

#### 1.1.1 Artistic Qualities

> 1.1.1.1 艺术性特质

Despite all this, I think that most of us draw a line somewhere through all the work we have produced. On one side of it are the images we'd view as "art," or that we at least feel have an artistic quality. On the other side are the rest.

> 尽管如此，我认为我们大多数人会在自己所创作的所有作品中划出一条界限。一侧是我们将视为“艺术”的图像，或者至少我们认为它们具有艺术性；另一侧则是其余的作品。

In one or two of the major international photography competitions, there is a category for Fine Art and it can be interesting to view the entries in this category and see if you can identify any differences between them and images entered in other categories, such as Landscape, Nature, and Still Life. I've certainly not been able to see any clear distinction, although the Fine Art category perhaps tends to include simpler imagery with an emphasis on strong shapes and patterns in the compositions. Many of the images, particularly those including people, also appear to be "staged," which suggests that the vision came fi rst with the photographer going on to manifest it.

> 在一些重要的国际摄影比赛中，设有艺术摄影类别。观看该类别中的参赛作品，并尝试辨别它们与其他类别（如风景、自然和静物）作品之间的差异，可能是一件有趣的事情。我确实无法看出任何明显的区别，尽管艺术摄影类别可能更倾向于包含一些更简洁的图像，强调构图中强烈的形状和图案。许多作品，尤其是包含人物的作品，似乎都是“摆拍”的，这表明摄影师的构想先于拍摄，随后通过拍摄将其具象化。

#### 1.1.2 Aesthetic Merit

> 1.1.1.2 美学价值

In my own work, this line is not fi xed and undoubtedly changes over time with certain images fl itting from one side to the other. I've heard it argued that photography is fi ne art when it is undertaken purely for its aesthetic merits. My criteria for defi ning whether one of my images could be regarded as "Fine Art" refl ects this, in that it will depend largely on my intentions for its use. This no doubt also infl uences the creative energy that I put into the capture and processing of the image.

> 在我的作品中，这条线并不固定，毫无疑问会随着时间推移而变化，某些图像会在两侧之间游移。我曾听人争论说，当摄影纯粹以审美价值为目的时，它便属于美术。我定义某张图像是否可以被视为“美术”的标准也反映了这一点，即这在很大程度上取决于我拍摄该图像的用途意图。毫无疑问，这同样也影响着我在拍摄和后期处理过程中投入的创作能量。

For example, fractal patterns (forms or shapes that are repeated in an image in such a way that if you zoom in you will fi nd the same pattern on a smaller scale) are apparently often there to be perceived at some level of our awareness. These are widespread in the natural world, which perhaps explains the satisfaction they provide.

> 此外，正如我们所见，我们似乎在挑战中获得乐趣，尤其是在尝试构思图像时。抽象图像在此方面给我们带来了严峻的挑战。有趣的是，许多抽象图像中往往比艺术家和观众有意识所察觉的要具有更多的结构、秩序、图案和平衡。例如，分形图案（一种在图像中以某种方式重复出现的形态或形状，当你放大图像时，会发现其在更小的尺度上呈现出相同的图案）似乎在我们意识的某个层面中普遍存在。这些分形图案在自然界中非常普遍，或许正因如此，它们才给我们带来满足感。

It is interesting to ponder such fi ndings in relation to our notions of beauty. It is not entirely true to say that something is inherently beautiful because it is

> 就这些发现与我们对美的观念之间的关系进行思考是有趣的。不能完全说某物因其本身而具有自然之美。

necessary for us to judge something fi rst to give it this label. We probably often base this judgment on our recognition of, and identifi cation with, the elegance that has gone into its composition. Much of this elegance corresponds with many of the mathematical sequences and derived ratios (such as the Golden Ratio) that we have discussed in this book.

> 我们判断某事物并为其贴上这种标签，这是必要的。我们通常基于对构图中所体现的优雅的识别与共鸣来做出这种判断。这种优雅在很大程度上与我们在本书中讨论过的许多数学序列和衍生比例（如黄金比例）相呼应。

#### 1.1.5 Natural Sequences

> 自然序列

It is interesting that these also seem to be widespread in the natural world. Growth occurs in biological organisms by cell division. One cell becomes two, the two become four, and so on. However, each cell of the same generation does not necessarily divide at the same time, so one cell becomes two, one of these then divides before the other, producing three. Then the other cell divides, producing fi ve. Then 8, 13, and so on. This is the Fibonacci sequence that we met with early in this book.

> 有趣的是，这些现象似乎在自然界中也很普遍。生物体的生长是通过细胞分裂实现的。一个细胞分裂成两个，两个细胞分裂成四个，依此类推。然而，同一世代的每个细胞并不一定同时分裂，因此一个细胞分裂成两个，其中一个细胞先分裂，产生三个细胞，然后另一个细胞分裂，产生五个细胞。接着是八个、十三个，依此类推。这就是我们在本书早期就遇到的斐波那契数列。

If you count the number of leaves on branches of a plant, they typically follow a certain sequence and repeating patterns or fractals are commonly encountered. Nature has not used these patterns to please human beings, so why do we fi nd such satisfaction in this aspect of design? The answer, perhaps, is that we are all formed by these same biological processes, so have this elegance in us too. So, maybe our appreciation and experience of beauty is related to our perceiving a resonation between a pattern that is outside us and a similar pattern within us? It seems that we are all born with this ability to respond to "beauty," and as photographers we are often trying to create it too.

> 如果你数一数植物枝条上的叶片数量，它们通常遵循一定的序列，重复的图案或分形结构在自然界中很常见。自然并未使用这些图案来取悦人类，那么为什么我们在这个设计方面仍会感到满足呢？或许答案在于，我们自身也由相同的生物过程形成，因此我们体内也蕴含着这种优雅。因此，也许我们对美的欣赏和体验，与我们感知到外部图案与体内相似图案之间产生共鸣有关？看起来，我们生来就具有回应“美”的能力，而作为摄影师，我们常常也试图去创造这种美。

One way or another, these types of fi ndings and debate make us realize how much more there is to discover and understand in relation to what makes some photographs so effective. This type of research may eventually shed more light on this, but sometimes I wonder if it would be a shame if we got too obsessive about controlling our image-making. It's nice that there is still some mystery there and that we can often look at a photograph and conclude that it works, but we're not quite sure why!

> 无论以何种方式，这些发现和讨论都让我们意识到，关于为何某些摄影作品如此具有感染力，还有许多值得探索和理解的内容。这类研究最终可能会为我们揭示更多真相，但有时我也会想，如果我们对图像创作的控制过于执着，是否反而会失去一些东西？正是这种神秘感令人着迷，我们往往能直观感受到一幅作品的效果，却无法确切说出原因！

# 2 Conclusion

> **焦外（Bokeh）** 是摄影和光学中的一个重要概念，指图像中**失焦区域**（如背景或前景）的模糊程度和质感。它描述了镜头如何渲染非主体部分的视觉效果，直接影响照片的观感和艺术性。

---

### 0.1 **核心概念**
1. **定义**  
   - **焦外**（Bokeh）指镜头在对焦时，对焦区域以外的区域（如背景或前景）呈现出的模糊效果。  
   - 这种模糊并非单纯的“模糊”，而是带有特定**质感和层次感**的视觉效果，可能显得柔和、朦胧或带有光斑等。

2. **中文翻译**  
   - “焦外”直译为“焦点之外”，强调的是**失焦区域**的视觉表现。  
   - 在摄影术语中，常被译为“**景深模糊**”或“**虚化效果**”，但“**焦外**”更贴近其技术内涵。

---

### 0.2 **影响焦外效果的因素**
1. **光圈大小（Aperture）**  
   - 大光圈（如 f/1.4、f/1.8）能产生更明显的焦外效果，因为光圈越大，景深越浅，失焦区域越明显。  
   - 光圈形状（如圆形或非圆形）会影响焦外的**光斑形状**，进而改变画面的美感。

2. **镜头设计**  
   - 不同镜头的光学设计会影响焦外的**柔和度**和**层次感**。例如：  
     - **高分辨率镜头**可能在焦外区域产生更细腻的渐变模糊。  
     - **低质量镜头**可能在焦外区域出现“**光斑**”或“**色差**”，影响画面质感。

3. **对焦距离**  
   - 越靠近主体（如人像摄影），焦外区域的模糊程度越强。  
   - 背景与主体的距离越远，焦外的模糊效果越明显。

4. **拍摄距离与构图**  
   - 背景与主体的距离、构图方式（如大景深或浅景深）也会影响焦外的视觉效果。

---

### 0.3 **焦外的审美意义**
1. **艺术表现**  
   - 焦外效果常用于**人像摄影**或**特写摄影**，通过虚化背景突出主体，营造**氛围感**或**故事性**。  
   - 例如：人像摄影中，柔和的焦外可以弱化背景杂乱，增强人物的主体地位。

2. **情感传达**  
   - 焦外的质感（如柔和、朦胧）可以传递**情感**或**情绪**。例如：  
     - 柔和的焦外可能显得温暖、梦幻；  
     - 粗糙的焦外可能显得复古、怀旧。

3. **镜头特性**  
   - 一些镜头因焦外表现独特而被追捧（如“**散景**”镜头），成为摄影爱好者追求的“**镜头性格**”。

---

### 0.4 **焦外的常见误区**
1. **并非越模糊越好**  
   - 过度的焦外可能导致背景完全失焦，失去画面信息，反而影响表达。  
   - 需根据**主题和构图**平衡焦外的强度。

2. **焦外≠背景虚化**  
   - 焦外不仅指背景的模糊，还包括**前景**（如前景虚化）或**主体边缘**的模糊效果，需综合考虑。

3. **焦外与景深的关系**  
   - 焦外是景深的延伸，但两者并非完全等同。焦外的质感和层次感是镜头设计的综合体现。

---

### 0.5 **总结**
- **焦外（Bokeh）** 是摄影中通过镜头设计和拍摄技巧实现的**视觉艺术**，直接影响画面的层次感和氛围。  
- 它不仅是技术参数（如光圈、景深）的结果，更与**镜头光学性能**和**摄影师的审美选择**密切相关。  
- 优秀的焦外效果能提升照片的**情感表达**和**艺术价值**，是摄影创作中不可或缺的元素。

So, what can we conclude about the role of composition in our image-making? Firstly, it is worth acknowledging that some aspects of our composition are performed unconsciously, but many are conscious creative decisions. Secondly, composition is not just about putting a few lines in an image and balancing the weight—you can have a carefully composed shot that tells us virtually nothing other than it is carefully composed. Composition on its own is generally not the purpose of our photographic endeavors. Rather it is a valuable tool to use if we intend to convey something to others, whether it's a story, message, insight, mood, or simply some detail of content.

> 因此，我们可以得出关于构图在我们摄影创作中所扮演角色的哪些结论呢？首先，值得承认的是，我们构图的一些方面是无意识完成的，但很多则是有意识的创作选择。其次，构图不仅仅是将几条线条放入画面中并平衡视觉重量——你甚至可以拍出一幅精心构图的照片，除了表明它被精心构图外，几乎不传达任何其他信息。通常来说，构图本身并不是我们摄影追求的主要目的。相反，如果我们意图向他人传达某些内容，无论是讲述一个故事、传达某种信息、表达深刻见解、营造特定氛围，还是仅仅呈现某些内容细节，构图便是一种有价值的工具。

We've seen that our eyes—whether we are photographer or viewer—do not work like cameras. They are linked to our brain and the signals that reach our consciousness are fi ltered by the processes of perception. To a limited degree this, along with other psychological processes linked to our perception of two-dimensional images, has been studied and we can use the principles that arise from these studies to help us realize our intentions. Whether we intend an image to be perceived easily, giving a sense of harmony and order, or whether we intend to upset the viewer by making things a little more challenging for them, these principles are still at our disposal.

> 我们已经看到，无论是摄影师还是观众，我们的眼睛并不像相机那样工作。它们与我们的大脑相连，传递到意识中的信号会经过感知过程的过滤。在一定程度上，与我们对二维图像的感知相关的其他心理过程也已被研究，我们可以利用这些研究得出的原理来帮助我们实现自己的意图。无论是希望图像被轻松感知，带来和谐与秩序的感觉，还是希望通过增加一些挑战让观众感到困扰，这些原理仍然为我们所用。

Based to some degree on these principles of perception (but also on experience), various "rules" have been espoused. The rules are usually grounded in a desire for harmonious, organized, readily perceived, and pleasing images. If our intention is another, very different type of image and response then we may choose to break the rules, but even here a knowledge of the rules is benefi cial, as it will help you to more consciously and creatively break

> 在一定程度上，这些感知原理（同时也基于经验）催生了各种“规则”。这些规则通常源于对和谐、有条理、容易被感知且令人愉悦的画面的追求。如果我们意图创造一种截然不同、具有不同反应效果的画面，那么我们可以选择打破这些规则。然而，即使在这种情况下，了解这些规则也是有益的，因为这将有助于你更加自觉和创造性地打破规则。

them. The degree to which you break them can provide a whole spectrum of responses in the viewer. They give you, as a photographer, more control over the subtleties of how your images are perceived and received. We can also see why some would argue that, strictly speaking, "rules" do not exist in composition.

> 它们。你打破它们的程度可以为观者带来多样化的反应。作为摄影师，这使你对观众如何感知和接受你的影像拥有更多的控制力。我们也可以理解为何有人会认为，严格来说，"规则"在构图中并不存在。

Sometimes, for an image to have impact, a certain frame of mind is needed on the part of the viewer, so how this type of image will be received is far from predictable. These are often images that don't rely so much on the confi guration of their elements and perhaps, therefore, are not so open to interpretation according to the principles that have been discussed in this book. Or maybe they are, but we have not yet found the more subtle factors and clarifi ed the explanations that would help us understand the responses they engender.

> 有时，为了让一幅图像产生冲击力，观者需要具备一定的心理状态，因此这类图像的接受效果远非可预测。这些图像往往并不太依赖其元素的布局，也许因此，它们根据本书中所讨论的原则而言，并不那么具有解读空间。或者也许它们确实具有，但我们尚未发现更微妙的因素，也未明确解释这些因素，以帮助我们理解它们所引发的反应。

In closing, I'd like to stress that the best tools at our disposal for mastering composition are not bought from camera stores—they are within us. To better express something about our subject matter and ourselves in our photographs, we should take steps to engage more in the process. Before we start we need to take time to establish a relationship with our subject matter; to engage our hearts and emotions. Secondly, we need to engage our imaginations; to let them run wild to form our vision for an image.

> 最后，我想强调的是，我们掌握构图技巧最强大的工具并非从相机店购买而来，而是存在于我们自身之中。为了在照片中更好地表达我们对拍摄对象的理解以及自我感受，我们应该更积极地参与创作过程。在开始之前，我们需要花时间与拍摄对象建立联系，触动我们的情感与心灵。其次，我们需要激发我们的想象力，让想象力自由驰骋，构建图像的愿景。

Then, perhaps, we bring our cameras and our eyes into the process. That is to say, having formulated a vision we now use our eyes objectively to see what is actually in the scene. Then, we try to engineer the scene, which may involve simply waiting for something to move, or adjusting our camera, lens, or shooting position, so that the scene best refl ects our vision. We do this largely by bringing our bodies into play; using our legs to explore the scene and the options different viewpoints offer.

> 接下来，也许，我们将相机和眼睛带入到这个过程中。也就是说，在形成一个愿景后，我们现在用客观的眼睛去观察场景中实际存在的内容。接着，我们尝试对场景进行调整，这可能包括简单地等待某物移动，或调整相机、镜头或拍摄位置，以便场景最能体现我们的愿景。我们主要通过调动身体来完成这一过程；用双腿探索场景以及不同视角所提供的各种可能性。

It's amazing how often you see photographers standing behind tripods with the tripod legs at full extension simply so that they can stand comfortably, without stooping. Tripod legs are adjustable, and so are ours! We may need to lie down and, heavenforbid, get a little dirty or wet! When you see a serious, passionate photographer at work, and they're truly "in the zone," you'll see that they are lost in their creative experience—they won't be aware that they've just knelt in a mud patch. Throughout the process our minds are also at work—as we have seen, the arrangement of elements in our compositions is a considered process.

> 令人惊讶的是，你经常能看到摄影师站在三脚架后，将三脚架腿完全展开，仅仅是为了能舒适地站立，而无需弯腰。三脚架腿可以调节，我们的也可以！我们可能需要躺下，天哪，甚至弄脏或弄湿自己！当你看到一位认真投入的摄影师在工作，他们真正“进入心流状态”时，你会发现他们完全沉浸在创作体验中，不会意识到自己刚刚跪在泥地里。在整个过程中，我们的头脑也在积极运作——正如我们所见，构图中元素的安排是一个经过深思熟虑的过程。

I would like to thank you for joining me on this exploration of a fascinating topic. I hope you will now go on to look at your own photographs—and those of other photographers—with a critical eye and a greater understanding of why some shots work and others don't.

> 感谢您与我一同探索这个引人入胜的主题。我希望您现在能够以批判性眼光和更深入的理解去审视自己的照片——以及他人的作品，明白为什么有些照片成功，而有些则不然。

# 3 Glossary

> 3 术语表

Anomaly When an object or element is emphasized due to its dissimilarity to others surrounding it.

> 异质：当一个物体或元素由于与周围物体的差异而被强调时。

Aspect Ratio A numerical way of describing the shape of the frame by stating the horizontal and vertical measurements together as a ratio.

> 宽高比是一种数值方法，通过将水平和垂直尺寸合并为比例来描述画幅的形状。

Burning This term originates as a description of a darkroom process whereby a portion of the photographic paper is exposed to light from the enlarger for longer, resulting in a darkening of that part of the image. The term is still used when digital images are similarly manipulated to darken specifi ed parts of the image. *See also Dodging*.

> 烧焦：该术语起源于暗房工艺的描述，指将相纸的某部分暴露在放大机的光线中更长时间，从而导致图像的该部分变暗。该术语同样用于描述对数字图像进行类似处理以加深指定部分的情况。参见：Dodging（遮挡）。

Closure In the context of visual perception, this term describes our tendency to provide imaginary "missing" details to complete a potential pattern or shape that is not otherwise completely defi ned.

> 闭合 在视觉感知的背景下，这一术语描述了我们倾向于添加想象的“缺失”细节，以完成一个未被完全界定的潜在图案或形状的倾向。

Color Constancy This is the phenomenon of visual perception whereby we can identify two areas of slightly different color in an image (due to their differing degrees of illumination) as being the same material and thus actually the same color. This is based on similarities they have in other respects and our experience of the world around us.

> 色彩恒常性 这是视觉感知现象，即我们能够识别图像中两个颜色略有不同的区域（由于它们的照明程度不同），并将其视为同一物体，因此实际上颜色相同。这基于它们在其他方面的相似性以及我们对周围世界的体验。

Compositional Frame When objects or shapes within a scene act as a "frame within the frame" to encompass or partially surround other key elements within the scene.

> 构图中的框架：当场景中的物体或形状作为“框中框”来框住或部分包围场景中的其他关键元素时。

Continuance A term used to describe our potential to perceive what are actually separate elements in an image as being the same element captured in or conveying a sequence.

> 连续性：用于描述我们感知图像中实际上独立的元素为同一元素被捕捉到或传达序列的能力的术语。

Contrast This is a description of the extent to which adjacent areas of an image differ in brightness. It

> 对比度：这是对图像中相邻区域亮度差异程度的描述。

refl ects the gradation between the highest and lowest luminance values.

> 表现最高和最低亮度值之间的渐变。

Counter Form Imaginary shapes generated by our minds due to our tendency to strive for Closure (*which see*) or to continue existing lines, shapes, or contours.

> 对抗形式 由我们大脑因追求闭合（参见闭合）或延续现有线条、形状和轮廓而生成的虚幻形状。

Cropping To select and isolate a part of an image with the aim of improving composition or fi tting an image to the available space.

> 裁剪：选取并突出图像的一部分，以改善构图或使图像适应可用空间。

Depth In terms of visual imagery, this is a description of the sense of a recession of elements within the image into the distance. It is thus an impression of a third dimension that is actually lacking in the twodimensional image.

> 景深 在视觉影像方面，这是指图像中元素向纵深方向退缩的感知。因此，它给人一种实际上并不存在于二维图像中的三维空间的立体感。

Depth of Field The distance extending in front of and beyond the point of focus that is also perceived as being acceptably sharp. If an object is placed within this zone it will appear to be in focus.

> 景深 指焦点前后能被感知为清晰的范围。如果物体放置在该范围内，就会显得清晰。

Divine Proportion—*see Golden Mean.*

> 黄金比例—参见黄金分割。

Dodging The opposite of Burning (*which see*). Here a portion of the image is lightened. Software now enables us to selectively apply this to certain tones too.

> dodging（与 dodging 相反，参见 dodging）。此处图像的一部分被提亮。如今软件使我们能够有选择地将此应用到特定的色调上。

Dynamic Section A rectangular picture plane is divided by two lines, one running diagonally cornerto-corner and the other running from one of the other corners to meet the fi rst line at a 90-degree angle. The lines and intersection point are supposedly good places to position elements.

> 动态构图：一个矩形画幅被两条线分割，一条是对角线，从一个角落延伸至另一个角落；另一条线则从另一个角落延伸，与第一条线在直角处相交。这些线条及其交点通常被认为是放置元素的佳处。

Dynamic Tension This term describes the sense of unease, imbalance, and movement that we can gain from an image where elements are placed in an imbalanced or unconventional way.

> 动态张力 这一术语描述了通过图像中元素以不平衡或非常规的方式排列所传达出的不安、不平衡和动感。

Element In the context of this book, this term is used to encompass the various distinct entities that might be identifi able in the scene before us or within a photograph. These act as the component parts of our compositions. Distinct objects, areas of different or contrasting tone or color, lines, shapes, patterns, and even relatively empty or "inactive" spaces can thus be considered as elements.

> 元素 在本书的语境中，这一术语用于涵盖在我们眼前场景或照片中可能被辨识出的各种不同实体。这些元素构成了我们构图的基本组成部分。不同的物体、色调或色彩对比明显的区域、线条、形状、图案，甚至相对空旷或“不活跃”的空间，均可被视为元素。

Emergence This is the process whereby we suddenly perceive and recognize a complex form in an image.

> 浮现 这是指我们在图像中突然感知并识别出复杂形态的过程。

Equivocation When a picture can be seen in two or more ways due to its perceptual ambiguity.

> 歧义：当一张照片由于其感知上的歧义而能够以两种或多种方式被解读时。

Fibonacci Sequence or Ratio Leonardo Fibonacci proposed that there was a ratio often seen in nature that is pleasing to the eye—that ratio is 1:1.618. He also associated this with a mathematical sequence given his name. In this sequence, each subsequent number is the sum of the previous two.

> 斐波那契数列或黄金比例  
列奥纳多·斐波那契提出，在自然界中存在一种常被视作视觉美感的比例，即1:1.618的比例。他还将这一比例与以他名字命名的数学数列联系起来。在该数列中，每个后续数字都是前两个数字之和。这一比例常被应用于摄影构图中，以增强画面的视觉美感。

Figure This term is often used in this book in the context of its meaning as defi ned in the Figure/ Ground principle of the Gestalt Theory. Figure is the element or elements that we perceive as being subject matter within the image at any one time. Ground is the rest of the image. One can become the other as our focus of attention shifts and our interpretation of the image changes.

> 图底关系
本书中常使用“图”（Figure）这一术语，其含义源自格式塔理论中的图底关系原则。图指的是我们在某一时刻感知为图像主体的元素或元素组合。底则是图像的其余部分。当我们的注意力发生变化、对图像的解读也随之改变时，图与底之间可以相互转换。

Form The perception we gain of the threedimensionality of an object in a photograph due to the change in tones across it related to the light direction and the shadows cast.

> 形式：我们通过照片中物体的明暗变化所获得的三维感知，这种变化与光线方向和阴影有关。

Format In the context of picture format this is a term for the proportion of the horizontal-to-vertical dimensions of the image (*see also Aspect Ratio*).

> 格式 在图像格式的语境中，此术语指的是图像水平尺寸与垂直尺寸的比例（另见宽高比）。

Gestalt Theory A theory defi ned by psychologists early in the 20th century in their attempts to understand and defi ne the principles underlying our "visual intelligence" and perceptive processes. It looks at how we acquire and maintain stable perceptions that provide us with some useful meaning out of the complex and potentially chaotic world around us.

> 格式塔理论是由20世纪初的心理学家在试图理解并定义我们“视觉构成能力”和“视觉感知过程”的原理时提出的理论。它探讨了我们如何获取并保持稳定的视觉感知，从而在复杂且可能混乱的环境中赋予我们有意义的解释。

Golden Mean A proportion or "golden" ratio. If used to divide planes and pictorial elements are placed along the resulting lines or points, the compositions are supposedly more aesthetically pleasing. The ratio is derived by dividing a line of length c into two parts, a and b, whereby the ratio of a:b is the same as the ratio of b:c.

> 黄金分割，一种比例或“黄金”比例。若将画面元素按此比例分割，并将画面元素放置在分割线或分割点上，构图将被认为更具审美价值。该比例是将长度为c的线段分成两部分a和b，使得a与b的比例等于b与c的比例。

Golden Ratio—s*ee Golden Mean.*

> 黄金比例—见黄金分割。

Golden Section—*see Golden Mean.*

> 黄金分割—参见黄金比例。

Golden Spiral A logarithmic spiral whose growth factor is related to the Golden Ratio. Elements can be aligned along such a spiral with apparently pleasing results.

> 黄金螺线是一种对数螺线，其增长因子与黄金比例相关。沿此类螺线排列元素似乎会产生令人愉悦的效果。

Ground*—see Figure.*

> 地面—参见图。

Hourglass Effect When, due to our tendency to retain a sense of three-dimensionality in a two-dimensional image, we get a perception of two cones converging to a point of interest where they meet. Our attention is strongly drawn to and focused on this point.

> 沙漏效果 当我们在二维图像中保留三维感的倾向时，会感知到两个锥体汇聚到它们相交的焦点兴趣点上。我们的注意力被强烈吸引并聚焦于这个点上。

Hue The attribute of color that enables it to be classifi ed as red, green, blue, yellow, or purple. This is related to its spectral position.

> 色相 色彩能够被归类为红、绿、蓝、黄或紫色等属性。这与其在光谱中的位置有关。

Invariance A principle of visual perception whereby geometrical objects are recognized and identifi ed regardless of their scale, rotation, translation, certain deformations, different lighting, and so on.

> 不变性 是一种视觉感知原理，无论几何物体的尺度、旋转、平移、某些变形、不同的光照等，都能被识别和辨识。

Law of Prägnanz As one of the fundamental principles of the Gestalt Theory, this law states that our psychological processes attempt to make order, harmony, symmetry, simplicity, and structure out of seemingly disconnected bits of information.

> 完形法则 作为格式塔理论的基本法则，该法则指出我们的心理过程会试图从看似不相关的碎片信息中创造出秩序、和谐、对称、简洁和结构。

Lead Room—*see Rule of Space.*

> 引导空间—参见空间规则

Leading Space—*see Rule of Space.*

> 引导空间—参见 空间规则

Multistability A description of our tendency to switch back and forth between two possible perceptions within an image when there is ambiguity between them.

> 多稳态：描述我们在图像中因两者之间存在歧义而倾向于在两种可能的感知之间来回切换的现象。

Negative Space An area of image that is empty or not containing subject matter and "inactive." It is often used as a relative term—a space containing little compared to the "positive space" containing the subject matter and elements key to the composition of the image.

> 负空间  
图像中空旷或不包含主体的区域，通常被认为是“非活跃区域”。它通常作为相对术语使用，指与包含主体及其构图关键元素的“正空间”相比，所占比例较小的区域。

Nose Room—*see Rule of Space.*

> 鼻距—参见空间法则。

Optical Lines These assumed or implied lines are ones that our eyes tend to follow, such as the direction of gaze of a person in the picture.

> 视觉线条：这些假定或暗示的线条是我们的眼睛倾向于跟随的线条，如画面中人物的视线方向。

Panoramic Format This is when the Format (which *see*) of a picture is particularly elongated, typically exceeding an aspect ratio (*see Aspect Ratio*) of 2:1.

> 全景格式：当图片的格式特别拉长，通常超过2:1的长宽比时。

Perspective The art of suggesting three dimensions on a two-dimensional surface by accurately recreating the spatial relationships that objects receding into the distance in a scene actually present to the eye.

> 透视：通过精确再现场景中远景物体实际呈现给眼睛的空间关系，在二维平面上营造出三维感的艺术。

Phi—*see Golden Mean.*

> 斐波那契数列—参见黄金比例。

Point Used to describe an element in a composition, this term identifi es a shape that is small, simple, and concentrated in form that attracts the eye with great force.

> 点：用于描述构图中的元素，该术语指一种小、简单且形式集中的形状，能够强烈吸引视线。

Positive space—*see Negative Space.*

> 正空间—参见负空间。

Rabatment The imaginary line created if you divide a rectangular picture to form a square with sides equal in length to the shorter side of the rectangle.

> 裁切线 是将矩形画面分割成一个边长等于矩形较短边的正方形时所形成的虚线。

Reifi cation The constructive and generative tendency of our perceptual processes, whereby we perceive shapes and objects that are not there.

> 具象化：我们知觉过程的建构性和生成性倾向，使我们感知到实际上并不存在的形状和物体。

Rule of Space A subject in a photograph should have space to move into in the direction in which they are facing or moving.

> 空间规则：照片中的主体应留有向其面对或移动方向延伸的空间。

Salience This is a pictorial element's level of impact and thus degree of dominance in the image.

> 突出性：这是图像中某一图像元素的影响力程度，因此决定了其在画面中的主导程度。

Saturation In relation to color this is a description of its purity and thus to some degree its intensity, though this also depends on its luminosity or brightness.

> 在色彩方面，饱和度是对色彩纯度的描述，因此在一定程度上也反映了其明度，尽管这还取决于其亮度或明度。

Scale Constancy A phenomenon of visual perception whereby we identify two separate elements of differing sizes in the image (due to their differing distances from us) as being the same type of object and actually the same size. It is based on similarities they have in other respects and our experience of the world around us.

> 大小恒常性
一种视觉感知现象，即我们能够识别图像中两个不同大小的元素（由于它们与我们的距离不同）为同一类型的物体，并实际具有相同的大小。这一现象基于它们在其他方面的相似性以及我们对周围世界的体验。

Tone A description of the lightness or darkness of a part of an image that enables it to be distinguished from other parts that have a different tone. In the context of color it describes the shade of that color, which is the quality of that color that varies according to the amount of white or black mixed with it.

> 色调：描述图像中某一部分的明暗程度，使其能够与其他具有不同明暗度的部分区分开来。在色彩的语境中，它描述该颜色的深浅，即该颜色的明暗程度，根据与之混合的白色或黑色量而变化。

Triptych A display composed of three separate images or panels that are brought together in this way.

> 三联画：由三张独立图像或面板组成的展示。

Vectors Imaginary lines generated by our minds to continue existing ones or to connect otherwise isolated lines or elements.

> 矢量 是由我们的思维产生的假想线，用于延续现有的线条或连接原本孤立的线条或元素。

Visual Attention The process whereby something catches our eye in the real world or in an image.

> 视觉注意力：在现实世界或图像中，某物吸引我们目光的过程。

Visual Weight A term used to describe the tendency an element in a picture has to draw the viewer's attention. It can also be used to describe the impression of actual weight that a pictorial element has.

> 视觉重量 用于描述画面中某一元素吸引观众注意力的倾向性术语。也可用于描述画面元素所呈现的实际重量的印象。
