## 1 <span id="page-46-0"></span>Turn Anatomy 101 转弯结构 101

All turns are not created equal. All share a common structure, but their components can look quite different from one to the next. In this chapter we examine

> 并非所有的转弯都相同。它们都遵循相同的结构，但各个组成部分在不同转弯中可能大相径庭。本章将探讨...

the various species of turns and their similarities and differences, and introduce some terminology we will need later.

> 各种转弯类型及其相似之处和差异，并介绍一些我们之后会用到的专业术语。

### 1.1 Phases of the Turn 转弯的阶段

Different things happen in different parts of every turn, and to give us a framework for their dissection, we need a clear vocabulary to refer to certain phases of the turn, namely *initiation*, *control*, *completion*, and *transition*. A single turn or a turn that begins from a traverse (however short) and ends with another has three phases: initiation, control, and completion. When turns are linked directly to one another, with no intervening traverse, the completion of the first flows seamlessly into the initiation of the next and together form a transition phase, as shown in figure 3.1.

> 在每个转弯的不同阶段，会发生不同的事情。为了给我们一个分析这些阶段的框架，我们需要一个清晰的术语来指代转弯的特定阶段，即起始阶段、控制阶段、完成阶段和过渡阶段。一个单独的转弯，或从横移（无论多短）开始并以另一个转弯结束的转弯，包含三个阶段：起始阶段、控制阶段和完成阶段。当转弯直接相连，没有中间的横移时，第一个转弯的完成阶段自然地衔接至下一个转弯的起始阶段，共同形成过渡阶段，如图3.1所示。

#### 1.1.1 Initiation 起始

During the initiation you establish the initial steering angle needed to start the turn (discussed later in this chapter, p. 45). You do this so the snow, acting through the ski, will make your path bend during the next phase of the turn, the control phase. In the initiation phase, you also align various body segments with each other and with your skis so that you can balance properly against the forces of the turn that will arise in the control phase. Your direction of travel doesn't change significantly in the initiation, which ends when the ski is solidly engaged with the snow.

> 在启动阶段，你需要确定开始转弯所需的初始转向角度（本章后面将详细讨论，第45页）。你这样做是为了让雪通过滑雪板会在转弯的下一阶段，即控制阶段，使你的轨迹弯曲。在起始阶段，你还会协调各个身体部位彼此之间以及与滑雪板的关系，以便正确平衡以对抗控制阶段中产生的转弯力量。在起始阶段，你的行进方向不会发生显著变化，该阶段结束于滑雪板稳固地切入雪中时。
![](_page_46_Picture_8.jpeg)

FIGURE 3.1 Turn phases. The transition encompasses the completion of one turn and the initiation of the next. Skier: François Bourque, Canada.

> 图3.1 转弯阶段。过渡阶段包括完成一个转弯并开始下一个转弯的过程。滑雪者：François Bourque，加拿大。

The initiation phase is when you set the tone of the whole turn. You might float, dive, or ease into the initiation; you might hang back, hurry, or brace. The accuracy, dynamism, and commitment with which different skiers initiate their turns is a strong reflection of their ability levels.

> 进入阶段是你为整个转弯定调的关键时刻。你可能会浮滑、俯冲或从容进入起始阶段；也可能后仰、急躁或准备就绪。不同滑雪者在进入转弯时所表现出的精准度、力度和投入程度，强烈反映了他们的技术水平。

Three things happen during the initiation:
> 在进入转弯时有三件事发生：

- 1. Your balance axis inclines toward the inside of the new turn, relative to your outside ski, in anticipation of the centrifugal force you'll encounter in the upcoming control phase. In other words, your center of mass moves closer to the center of the new turn than your outside foot.
> 你的平衡轴相对于外侧滑雪板，向新转弯的内侧倾斜，以预判即将在控制阶段遇到的离心力。换句话说，你的质心会比外侧脚更靠近新转弯的中心。

- 2. Your skis change edges. Strictly speaking, only one edge has to change. Whichever edge or edges were supporting the skier before the entry into the new turn, a different edge or edges will now do the work of making the turn happen.
>你的滑雪板改变边刃。严格来说，只需改变一个边刃即可。无论之前进入新转弯时是哪条边或哪些边在支撑滑雪者，现在将由不同的边或边组合来完成转弯动作。

- 3. At least one of your skis must establish a steering angle relative to your momentum. This could be as small as the angle provided by the ski's reverse camber when carving, as in figure 3.2, or a pivot of more than 40 degrees, as in figure 3.3.
>至少有一只滑雪板必须相对于你的动量形成转向角度。这个角度可以小至如图3.2所示，由滑雪板反曲（reverse camber）在转弯时提供的角度，也可以如图3.3所示，达到超过40度的旋转角度。

> 图3.2 当转弯半径和速度恰当时，滑雪者只需将滑雪板边缘滚动并让其划出弧线即可开始转弯。滑雪者：阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔，挪威。

#### 1.1.2 *Control* 控制

The control phase is where the snow makes you turn (figure 3.4). Significant lateral force between the ski and the snow makes your momentum's direction change, and the ski, because of its self steering effect, turns itself as it moves forward. To take advantage of the ski's self-steering effect, you control the distribution of pressure fore and aft on the ski, as well as its platform angle and edge angle, to get the shape of turn you want. In short turns, especially on a steep slope, the control phase may be very short.

> 控制阶段是雪板使你转弯的阶段（见图3.4）。雪板与雪之间产生显著的横向力，使你的动量方向发生改变，同时，由于雪板的自我转向效应，雪板在向前移动时会自行转弯。为了利用雪板的自我转向效应，你需要控制雪板上前后压力的分布，以及雪板的平台角度和刃角，从而获得你想要的转弯形状。在短弯道，尤其是在陡坡上，控制阶段可能非常短暂。

![](_page_48_Figure_6.jpeg)

Figure 3.4 During the control phase the force from the snow changes the skier's direction of travel. The control phase of many modern slalom turns, like this one, is short and intense. Skier: Benjamin Raich, Austria.

> 图3.4 在控制阶段，雪的反作用力会改变滑雪者滑行的方向。许多现代回转技术的控制阶段，如本例所示，都短促而激烈。滑雪者：本杰明·赖赫，奥地利。

#### 1.1.3 *Completion* 完成

During the completion phase you want to stop turning and either go into a traverse or start a turn in the other direction. To do this, you must eliminate the lateral force from the snow that was making you turn. You do this primarily by reducing your inclination into the turn and the ski's self-steering effect. These two actions occur together in a coordinated fashion. Reducing your inclination reduces the ski's edge angle, which reduces its self-steering effect. Reducing the self-steering effect reduces the ski's steering angle, which in turn reduces the lateral force from the snow, requiring less inclination to remain balanced.

> 在完成阶段，你希望停止转弯，要么进入横向移动，要么开始向相反方向转弯。要做到这一点，你需要消除使你转弯的雪地横向力。你主要通过减少进入转弯的倾斜度和滑雪板的自动转向效应来实现这一点。这两个动作以协调的方式同时发生。减少倾斜度会降低滑雪板的边角角度，从而减少其自动转向效应。减少自动转向效应会降低滑雪板的转向角度，从而进一步减少雪地的横向力，使你只需更小的倾斜度即可保持平衡。

In some turns, simply reducing the ski's edge angle will allow it to straighten out, putting the skier into a traverse. In many more dynamic turns, the skier may also encourage the ski to stop turning by shifting the distribution of pressure toward the tail, as shown in figure 3.5.

> 在一些转弯中，仅仅减少滑雪板的边角角度就能使其变直，使滑雪者进入横向移动状态。在许多更动态的转弯中，滑雪者也可以通过将压力分布向尾部转移来促使滑雪板停止转动，如图3.5所示。

Another commonly used mechanism for completing the turn, especially when it will be linked closely with the next one, is to make the skis slow down or turn more tightly under the skier. Done properly, this has the effect of eliminating the skier's inclination, and hence ending the turn. Carried further, it can cause the skier to topple across the skis toward the inside of the next turn, beginning that turn's initiation phase.

> 完成转弯的另一种常用机制，尤其是在与下一个转弯紧密衔接时，是让滑雪板在滑雪者下方减速或更紧密地转向。如果正确执行，这将消除滑雪者的倾斜，从而结束转弯。若进一步发展，这会导致滑雪者向下一个转弯的内侧倾倒，开始该转弯的启动阶段。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_49_Picture_6.jpeg)

Figure 3.5 Kathrin Zettel of Austria completes a slalom turn by moving the pressure on her outside ski from under the fall of her foot to the heel, while simultaneously reducing the ski's edge angle. This makes the ski run straight, releasing her from the turn.

> 图3.5 奥地利选手卡特琳·齐特尔在完成一个蛇形转弯时，将脚掌下的压力转移到脚跟，同时减小雪板的边角，使雪板直线滑行，从而脱离转弯。

#### 1.1.4 *Transition* 转换

Until they reach a certain level of skill, skiers execute turns that have distinct initiations and completions, separated by traverses. Advanced skiing starts when the traverse between a skier's parallel turns disappears and the completion of one turn and the initiation of the next merge into a seamless transition phase (figure 3.6). Now, instead of picturing a turn as going from traverse to traverse, it's often useful to view it as going from fall line to fall line, as many of the photomontages in this book are constructed. For an expert skier, the transition is a continuous succession of movements.

> 在达到一定技术水平之前，滑雪者执行的转弯动作具有明确的起始和完成阶段，中间由平移段分隔。当滑雪者平行转弯之间的平移段消失时，高级滑雪技巧便开始显现，此时一个转弯的完成与下一个转弯的起始融为一体，形成无缝衔接的过渡阶段（图3.6）。此时，与其将转弯想象为从平移段到平移段的过程，不如将其视为从落差线到落差线的过程，因为本书中的许多照片拼贴作品都是以此为基础构建的。对于专家级滑雪者来说，这种过渡过程是一个连续不断的动作序列。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_50_Picture_2.jpeg)

Figure 3.6 Aksel Lund Svindal of Norway melds the completion of one turn with the initiation of another in a fluid, seamless transition.
> 图3.6 挪威选手阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔将一个转弯的完成与另一个转弯的开始流畅无缝地衔接。

The transition begins when your body starts to be released from the lateral force of the turn and its momentum carries it in a straighter line than your feet are traveling. This is where the expert feels release and the illusion of acceleration. In the extreme, your skis pop from the snow, your feet fly to the outside of the new turn, and your body shoots down the hill in anticipation of the force that will develop when the skis engage the snow to make the next arc.

> 过渡阶段始于你的身体开始从转弯的横向力中释放，此时惯性使你的身体沿比双脚移动更直的线路滑动。这就是专家感受到释放并产生加速错觉的时刻。在极端情况下，你的滑雪板会从雪面上弹起，双脚飞向新转弯的外侧，身体则顺着山脊疾驰下滑，预感到滑雪板再次接触雪面产生下一段弧线时即将产生的力量。

An important moment occurs during the transition when your center of gravity's path crosses that of your feet (figure 3.7). At this instant the skis are running straight and flat on the snow, making them easy to pivot. So the more accurately you can sense the crossover point, the more easily you can turn the skis without unweighting to disengage them from the snow. For the same reason, this is the moment when your pole plant can have the greatest turning effect (see Torque From the Pole Plant in chapter 7).

> 在重心轨迹与脚部轨迹交叉的过渡阶段（见图3.7）是一个重要时刻。此时滑雪板正直行且平躺在雪面上，便于转动。因此，你越能准确感知交叉点，就越容易在不卸载的情况下转动滑雪板，使其脱离雪面。出于同样的原因，这也是杖插地产生最大转向效果的时刻（参见第7章中的“杖插地产生的扭矩”）。

![](_page_50_Figure_6.jpeg)

Figure 3.7 At the moment in the transition when the path of the center of gravity passes over that of the feet, the skis go flat. This is the moment to start steering them to their initial steering angle for the next turn. Skier: Laure Pequegnot, France.

> 图3.7 在过渡阶段的某一时刻，当重心轨迹越过脚部轨迹时，滑雪板会变平。这时应开始将滑雪板转向下一个转弯的初始转向角度。滑雪者：Laure Pequegnot，法国。

### 1.2 The Virtual Bump 虚拟雪包

Because you make turns on a slope, the total force between you and the snow varies throughout every turn. Even on a perfectly smooth hill, the effect of making turns is just like skiing through bumps. The better the skis hold and the tighter the turn, the greater the effect. In a sharply carved turn made on a smooth slope, for instance, you will feel light at the top of the turn and heavy at the bottom. This is because the gradient the skis are on changes through the course of the turn in the same way it does when you ski over one bump and into another.

> 因为你在坡面上转弯，你与雪之间的总受力在每个转弯过程中都会发生变化。即使是在完全平滑的坡面上，转弯带来的效果也如同在起伏的雪面上滑行一样。雪板的抓地力越强，转弯越紧，这种效果就越明显。例如，在平滑坡面上进行一个锐利的刻滑转弯时，你会在转弯顶部感觉轻盈，而在底部则感到沉重。这是因为雪板在转弯过程中坡度的变化方式，与你在起伏之间滑行时坡度的变化方式是相同的。

On the left of figure 3.8, you can see the track of two idealized turns. The right of that figure shows the gradient on which the skis are running at each instant in those turns. At the beginning, the skis are across the fall line, on a relatively flat grade. As the skier approaches the fall line, the grade becomes steeper and steeper, and as the skier turns out of the fall line, the skis come onto a flatter and flatter grade. The gradient traced by the skis, as shown on the right of figure 3.8, looks just like the profile traced by a skier running through bumps.

> 在图3.8的左侧，你可以看到两个理想化转弯的轨迹。该图的右侧则展示了滑雪者在这些转弯过程中每时每刻所处的坡度。开始时，滑雪者处于下滑线的上方，坡度相对平缓。随着滑雪者接近下滑线，坡度变得越来越陡；而当滑雪者从下滑线转弯离开时，滑雪板又逐渐回到更平缓的坡度上。如图3.8右侧所示，滑雪板所描绘的坡度轨迹看起来就像滑雪者穿越雪包时留下的轮廓。

![](_page_51_Figure_5.jpeg)

Figure 3.8 As a skier moves through turns on a smooth slope, the effective gradient changes as if the skier were skiing through bumps.

> 图3.8 当滑雪者在平缓坡面上通过转弯时，有效坡度会发生变化，仿佛滑雪者正在起伏的雪包上滑雪。

Another way to look at the same phenomenon is shown in figure 3.9. Gravity always points in the same direction. At the start of the turn, what little centrifugal force there is points the other way, making you feel light. At the end of the turn, the two forces are pointing in nearly the same direction, making you feel heavy. In between, the total force you feel builds continuously. Again, this is just like skiing first into the trough between two bumps, and into another bump at the end of the turn.

> 另一种观察同一现象的方式如图3.9所示。重力始终指向同一方向。在转弯开始时，微弱的离心力指向相反方向，使你感觉轻盈。在转弯结束时，重力和离心力几乎指向同一方向，使你感觉沉重。在中间阶段，你所感受到的总受力持续增加。同样，这就像先滑入两个 bumps 之间的凹槽，再滑入转弯结束时的另一个 bumps。

Adding to the effect is the fact that when going from one turn to another, you go from being inclined to one side of your feet to being inclined the other way. This action, shown in figure 3.10 on page 42, tosses you upward just as a bump would. The total effect, which I call the *virtual bump,* plays a fundamental role in all of advanced skiing. Figure 3.11, also on page 42, shows a World Cup slalom skier flexing to absorb the virtual bump between two tightly carved turns on a smooth slope.

> 增强这种效果的是这样一个事实：当你从一个转弯转向另一个转弯时，你的身体会从脚的一侧倾斜变为另一侧倾斜。这种动作，如第3.10图（第42页）所示，会像遇到一个实际的隆起一样将你向上弹起。这种整体效果，我称之为“虚拟隆起”，在所有高级滑雪技巧中都起着根本性的作用。第3.11图（第42页）展示了一名世界杯回转滑雪选手在平滑坡道上，于两个紧密回转之间屈膝以缓冲虚拟隆起。

> 图3.9 重力和离心力在转弯过程中相互作用，使滑雪者在转弯开始时感觉较轻，结束时则较重，就像在雪堆中滑雪一样。滑雪者：宝琳·莱希，奥地利。

Just as if she were skiing in moguls, she is most fully extended in the middle of the turn and most flexed in the transition. The effect of the virtual bump makes it easier to start a turn from the end of the previous turn, rather than from a traverse, and it's one of the tools experienced skiers use to make skiing less work when they are tired or just want to relax and cruise. For the world-class racer, the effect reaches magnitudes that make it formidable and potentially disastrous. How to exploit and control the virtual bump is addressed in chapters 6 and 9.

> 就像她在雪坡上滑雪一样，她在转弯的中间部分身体完全伸展，而在过渡阶段则最为弯曲。这种虚拟的凸起效果使得从上一个转弯的末端开始新的转弯更为容易，而不是从横向移动中开始。这是经验丰富的滑雪者在疲惫或只是想放松滑行时用来减轻体力消耗的一种技巧。对于世界级的滑雪选手来说，这种效果的强度足以变得极具挑战性，甚至可能带来危险。如何利用和控制这种虚拟凸起将在第6章和第9章中进行探讨。

### 1.3 Types of Turns 转弯类型

I'm going to start with an intentional oversimplification and say that there are three types of turns: skidded turns, checked turns, and carved turns. In truth, these are idealizations that seldom, if ever, occur in their pure forms. Still, most turns are dominated by the characteristics of one or another of these three basic types. In practice, very few turns are perfectly carved, and most turns that end with a check have a control phase that involves some skidding and possibly carving. So as we consider carved, skidded, and checked turns, keep in mind that there's some overlap in nearly every turn we make.

> 我将从一种有意的简化开始，说滑雪转弯可以分为三种类型：打滑转弯、制动转弯和刻滑转弯。实际上，这些类型只是理想化的模型，很少，甚至几乎从未以纯粹的形式出现。然而，大多数转弯主要由这三种基本类型中的一种或几种特征主导。在实践中，几乎很少有转弯能完美地完成刻滑，而大多数以制动结束的转弯在控制阶段通常会涉及一定程度的打滑，甚至可能伴随刻滑。因此，在我们考虑刻滑、打滑和制动转弯时，要记住几乎每个转弯都存在这些类型的重叠。

#### 1.3.1 *Skidded Turns* 滑行转弯

A ski making a skidded turn moves sideways as it moves forward—the kind of turn a car racing on a dirt track makes. The skid makes use of the oversteering characteristics of skis, discussed in chapter 2, to make the skis turn themselves as they move forward. The middle of the ski engages the snow at a steering angle to the skier's momentum in the transverse plane, and the snow pushes back to turn or slow the skier down (figure 3.12). The component that acts perpendicular to the skier's momentum is that portion of the snow's force that makes the skier turn.

> 一种侧滑转弯会使滑雪板在向前移动的同时横向滑动，就像一辆在泥地赛道上赛车一样。这种滑行利用了滑雪板的转向过度特性（第2章讨论过），使滑雪板在向前移动时自行转弯。滑雪板的中部在横向平面上以与滑雪者动量呈转向角度的方式切入雪面，雪面则会反作用力使滑雪者转弯或减速（图3.12）。作用力中垂直于滑雪者动量的那部分，正是雪面作用力使滑雪者转弯的那部分。

The more a turn is skidded, the less efficient it is and the more the skier's speed and momentum is scrubbed away. The less smooth the turn is, too, because the ski doesn't absorb irregularities and shocks nearly as well when it hits them sideways as when it encounters them with its tip.

> 转弯越打滑，效率越低，滑雪者的速度和动量损失越多。转弯越不流畅，因为当滑雪板侧面撞击时，它几乎无法像以尖端接触时那样有效地吸收不规则性和冲击。

Along with being easy to execute, skidded turns are an obvious way to control speed, and that's why most skiers rely on these turns, even on modern equipment that's designed to carve. This is not to say that skidding is categorically bad. All skiers, including the best in the world, do it frequently. Skidding with purpose and finesse is every bit as important a skill as carving, and has gotten something of a bad rap since shaped skis have become popular.

> 除了易于操作外，打滑转弯也是控制速度的显而易见的方法，这就是为什么大多数滑雪者仍然依赖这些转弯，即使是在设计用于刻滑的现代滑雪装备上。这并不意味着打滑绝对不好。所有滑雪者，包括世界顶级滑雪者，都会频繁地进行打滑。有技巧地进行打滑是一项与刻滑同样重要的技能，自从造型滑雪板流行以来，打滑便获得了一些负面评价。

#### 1.3.3 *Checked Turns* 刹车转弯

In a checked turn, the skier sets the edges sharply at a large steering angle at the turn's completion. The skis slow dramatically, but the momentum of the skier's body carries it across the feet into the next turn, pulling the skis along with it (figure 3.14). ### 1.4 The Initial Steering Angle 初始转向角

In chapter 2, table 2.1 illustrated the size of turns that can be carved with typical modern skis at different edge angles. An edge angle of 45 degrees is achievable by a very good, technically proficient skier. Experts who really know their stuff are capable in certain circumstances of holding edge angles of 60 degrees or so. Higher angles are, at this time, the domain of high-level competitive skiers. A technically strong skier on an all-mountain ski can probably carve a turn with a radius of about 12 meters, or a bit less than 40 feet. On a full-on carving ski, the radius tightens to about 8.5 meters, or 28 feet.

> 第二章中，表2.1展示了使用典型现代滑雪板在不同边角角度下可刻出的转弯尺寸。技术娴熟的滑雪者能够实现45度的边角角度。真正精通滑雪的专家在特定情况下甚至可以保持约60度的边角角度。更高的边角角度目前属于高水平竞技滑雪选手的领域。一名技术强大的滑雪者使用全山型滑雪板时，大概可以刻出半径约为12米（约40英尺）的转弯。而在专为刻滑设计的滑雪板上，半径则会缩小到约8.5米（约28英尺）。

These are big turns—bigger than many of the turns a skier makes in a day of skiing. They are the sorts of turn depicted in figure 3.15a, using our model of a marble rolling around in a bowl. In this case, we have two bowls of a radius that correspond to the ski's carving radius. The bowls are aligned so they match up perfectly, and the marble never encounters a steering angle larger than the infinitesimally small angle presented by the bowl's curve. #### 1.4.1 **Partially Carved Turns** 部分切线转弯

As I've said, many turns a skier wants to make are quite a bit smaller than the carving radius achievable even with deep-cut carving skis. We can make these turns with little or no skidding through what could be called *partial carving*.

> 正如我之前所说，滑雪者想要做出的许多转弯，其半径往往比即使使用深刃刻滑滑雪板所能达到的刻滑半径要小得多。我们可以通过所谓的部分刻滑，以几乎不打滑的方式完成这些转弯。

Figure 3.15b illustrates turns in which the marble must change direction more quickly than the curve the bowls alone will produce. Each time the marble leaves one bowl, it hits the side of the next at an angle, and there's a component of the reaction force from the bowl that reduces the marble's momentum. But as soon as the marble is rolling along the wall of the next bowl, it's immediately carving again, and no more momentum is lost through the rest of the turn.

> 图3.15b展示了需要比仅靠弯道本身产生的曲线更快改变方向的转弯。每当大理石离开一个弯道，它会以一定角度撞击下一个弯道的侧面，此时碗壁对大理石产生一个反作用力的分量，从而减少其动量。但一旦大理石开始沿着下一个弯道的内壁滚动，它便立即再次进行刻滑，整个转弯过程中不再损失动量。

This is, in fact, how many carved turns are made, both by recreational skiers and World Cup racers.

> 第二种情况提供了一个理想化的描述，说明你如何做出一个比滑雪板自然转弯半径更小的部分刻痕转弯。你首先将滑雪板在横向平面内转动至初始转向角度，从而让滑雪板刻出一个半径在其范围内的弧线。实际上，许多刻痕转弯正是如此完成的，无论是休闲滑雪者还是世界杯滑雪选手都是如此。

If you're traveling in a shallow traverse, the ski can have a significant steering angle well before it reaches the fall line, and so can generate a useful turning force. > 你如何确定初始转向角是每个滑雪教学体系中的关键要素。当你以楔形滑雪时，每条滑雪板始终都有一个转向角（图3.16）。要开始转弯，你只需让其中一条滑雪板主导，可以通过将大部分体重压在它上面，或者通过将其扭转到比另一条滑雪板更大的转向角来实现。

Their purpose is always the same: to get the mid body of the ski to a steering angle that extracts the lateral force from the snow that turns them in the direction they want to go.

#### 1.4.2 *The Size of the Initial Steering Angle* 初始转向角的大小

How big an initial steering angle do you need for a particular turn? That depends on the turn and whether you want to carve as much of it as you can. The sharper the turn, the greater the initial steering angle. Small initial steering angles provide a progressive buildup of force from the snow and a smooth entry into the turn but are inappropriate for short turns, especially on steep terrain. A large initial steering angle makes for a dramatic buildup of reaction force. Recall, too, that when the size of the steering angle gets beyond a certain point, you get more speed control and less direction change.

> 你需要多大的初始转向角度来应对特定的转弯？这取决于转弯的性质以及你是否希望尽可能多地进行刻意转弯。转弯越陡，初始转向角度越大。小的初始转向角度提供从雪面逐步增加力量并流畅地进入转弯，但这种技巧不适用于短弯道，尤其是在陡坡地形上。较大的初始转向角度会导致反作用力显著增强。请记住，当转向角度的大小超过一定限度时，你会获得更多的速度控制，而方向改变则会减少。

Turns in which the ski follows its edge from start to finish—that is, turns with little or no initial steering angle—are fine on broad, groomed runs that don't require you to turn in any particular place. More and more of our skiing terrain seems to be going in that direction, too. But when you seek more challenging terrain, you'll need greater initial steering angles. Large initial steering angles are necessary, for instance, for short turns on steep slopes. In the sort of turn made by extreme skiers in very steep couloirs, the skis are pivoted nearly 180 degrees before they engage the snow.

> 那些滑雪板从开始到结束始终沿着边刃滑行的转弯动作——即初始转向角度很小或几乎没有的转弯——在宽阔、修整过的雪道上表现良好，这些雪道不需要在特定位置进行转弯。越来越多的滑雪地形似乎也朝着这个方向发展。但当你寻求更具挑战性的地形时，就需要更大的初始转向角度。例如，在陡坡上进行短距离转弯时，就需要较大的初始转向角度。在极端滑雪者在非常陡峭的峡谷中进行的此类转弯中，滑雪板在接触雪面之前几乎旋转了180度。

Partially carved turns are the bread and butter of high-performance skiing on hard snow. Current practice among World Cup slalom and giant slalom skiers is to start carving as early as possible in the turn, which in slalom and giant slalom is usually somewhere around the fall line (figures 3.17 and 3.18).

> 部分刻滑是硬雪高速滑雪的核心技术。当前世界杯回转和大回转选手的常见做法是在转弯中尽早开始刻滑，通常在回转和大回转中，刻滑开始的位置大约在落差线附近（图3.17和图3.18）。

So, how do you know what the initial steering angle should be for any given turn? The way an expert at carving judges the initial steering angle is similar to the way an archer aims an arrow. When aiming at a target, an archer doesn't point the arrow directly at the bull's-eye. Knowing that the arrow will follow a curved path from the bow to the target, the archer aligns the arrow with that curve at the point where it meets the bow (figure 3.19).

> 因此，如何判断任何转弯的初始转向角度？滑雪专家在进行刻滑时判断初始转向角度的方式，类似于弓箭手瞄准箭矢的方式。当瞄准目标时，弓箭手不会直接将箭矢对准靶心。知道箭矢从弓到靶会沿曲线飞行，因此弓箭手会在箭矢接触弓的点上将其与该曲线对齐（见图3.19）。

> 图3.18 美国选手泰德·利盖蒂在陡坡上以较大的初始转向角度开始一个大回转转弯。滑雪板直到接近落线才开始接触雪面，此时滑雪板已被重新导向超过50度。有关利盖蒂的更多信息，请参见第134页。

The skier's problem is similar to the archer's, and world-class racers have developed their own version of the archer's solution. Before starting a turn, racers know where they want the turn to end and in what direction they want to be going at that point (figure 3.20*a*). Knowing from experience how sharp an arc they can carve, they in effect see that arc on the snow, beginning at the turn's intended exit point and progressing back up the hill (figure 3.20*b*). They then initiate the turn by pivoting the ski until it is aligned with that arc at the point it intersects their current path, and establishing the initial steering angle. When the ski lines up with the arc, the racer engages it with the snow and carves to the turn's completion (figure 3.20*c*).

> 滑雪者的问题与弓箭手类似，世界级的滑雪选手们也发展出了类似弓箭手解决方案的技巧。在开始转弯之前，选手们知道自己希望转弯结束的位置，以及在该点希望以什么方向继续前进（图3.20a）。凭借经验，他们知道能刻出多大的弧线，因此实际上在雪面上看到这条弧线，从转弯预期的出口点开始，向山上延伸（图3.20b）。然后他们通过转动滑雪板，使滑雪板在与当前路径相交的点上与这条弧线对齐，并确定初始的转向角度。当滑雪板与弧线对齐时，选手便将滑雪板切入雪面，完成转弯（图3.20c）。

Line selection is complicated by the pitch of the hill. Because of the pitch, the ski's edge angle will always be smallest early in the turn and get progressively greater until the turn's completion (figure 3.21). Because the ski's edge angle determines its carving radius, the turn's radius will naturally get tighter as the turn proceeds. The steeper the hill, the greater the effect. It can be mitigated somewhat by carving with the forebody early in the turn and moving back on the ski coming through the bottom, but the fact remains that nice, circular arcs just aren't possible on slopes with much pitch.

> 线路选择因坡度而变得复杂。由于坡度的影响，滑雪板的边角角度在转弯初期总是最小，随后逐渐增大，直到转弯完成（图3.21）。由于滑雪板的边角角度决定了其刻痕半径，随着转弯的进行，转弯半径会自然变小。坡度越陡，这种影响就越大。通过在转弯初期使用前段刻滑并随着通过底部时向后移动重心，可以在一定程度上缓解这种影响，但事实依然如此：在坡度较大的斜坡上，漂亮的圆形轨迹几乎是不可能的。

Becoming deft with skis or arrows requires learning the shapes of the curves they make. In archery, the curves vary with the pull of the bow, the distance to the target, the wind, and so on. In skiing, too, many factors contribute, including the shape, stiffness, and condition of the skis; the size of the turn; the skier's speed; the slope of the hill; the character of the snow; and others.

> 要熟练掌握滑雪技巧或箭术，需要学习它们所形成的曲线形状。在射箭运动中，箭的飞行轨迹会因拉弓力度、目标距离、风力等因素而有所不同。滑雪同样如此，许多因素都会影响滑行，包括滑雪板的形状、硬度和状态；转弯半径；滑雪者的速度；坡度；雪质；以及其他因素。

Many skiers start their turns with excessive steering angles. They fling their skis sideways into the turn, resulting in a barely controlled power slide. By oversteering the ski from the start, they never give it a chance to slice, but instead make it scrape. Unfortunately, most of these skiers never develop an appreciation for fitting their skis to a round, smooth arc. Skiers who insist on skiing on only steeps and moguls are especially prone to learning only to pivot and skid. They need to understand that making many turns of all sizes at all speeds on smooth, moderate terrain is essential to the development of well-rounded ski technique. I often seek out smooth, gentle runs and practice making slow, clean, carved turns. This is a great exercise to work on balance, control, and snow sensitivity.

> 许多滑雪者在转弯开始时使用过大的转向角度。他们将滑雪板横向抛入转弯，导致几乎失控的强力滑行。从一开始过度转向，使滑雪板无法切线，反而造成拖雪。不幸的是，这些滑雪者大多从未体会到将滑雪板贴合圆滑弧线的重要性。那些坚持只在陡坡和雪块上滑雪的人，尤其容易只学会旋转和打滑。他们需要明白，在平缓的中等坡度地形上，以各种速度和各种大小完成大量转弯，对于培养全面的滑雪技巧至关重要。我经常寻找平缓的缓坡，练习做出缓慢、干净、切线式的转弯。这是一项极佳的练习，有助于提高平衡感、控制力和对雪面的敏感度。

Controlled skidding and slipping are required in many turns to achieve a desired line and speed. Still, the extent to which the skidding can be reduced generally makes the turn smoother, more controlled, and more enjoyable. And because it's fair to say that skidding comes far more naturally to skiers, carving skills are the ones requiring deliberate study and practice.

> 受控的侧滑和打滑在许多转弯中都是必要的，以获得理想的路线和速度。 不过，一般来说，能够减少侧滑的程度，就能让转弯变得更加顺畅、可控、也更令人享受。而且，由于可以说侧滑对滑雪者来说要自然得多，因此刻滑（carving）技巧才是那些需要刻意学习与练习的技能。
