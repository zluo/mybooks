# 1 <span id="page-88-0"></span>Fore and Aft

> 1 <span id="page-88-0"></span>前后

Skiers move forward (fore) and backward (aft) in relation to their skis primarily to maintain stability. The first skill beginners learn when making their initial straight runs on those slippery boards is how to keep up with their feet as their skis accelerate, and how to keep upright as their skis slow to a stop. Viewed from the side, in the sagittal plane, the skier is challenged with keeping the balance axis going through the feet. As a skier gains experience and progresses to fresh challenges, variations in terrain and the character of the snow place similar demands on balance and require similar adjustments from the skier (figure 5.1). Moguls, variable loose snow, and some of the most entertaining natural features of a ski slope all require constant fore-aft adjustments (figure 5.2).

> 滑雪者主要通过向前（前部）和向后（尾部）与他们的滑雪板保持相对移动来维持稳定性。初学者在滑那些滑溜的木板时，首先学会的就是如何随着滑雪板加速而跟上脚步，以及如何在滑雪板减速停止时保持站立不倒。从侧面看，在矢状面上，挑战是让平衡轴通过双脚进行调整。当滑雪者获得经验并进入新的挑战中，地形的变化和雪的性质对平衡提出了类似的要求，并需要滑雪者做出相应的调整（图5.1）。巨石、多变的松散雪地以及滑雪坡上的许多最有趣的自然特征都要求不断调整前后方向（图5.2）。

At a finer level of control than is required just to stay in balance, fore and aft movements enable skiers to manipulate the ski's turning behavior: its self-steering effect, described in chapter 2. They do this by shifting the distribution of pressure along the ski's length, which controls the ski's tendency to oversteer and determines whether one part of the ski bends into more reverse camber than another.

> 在保持平衡所需的控制级别更精细的情况下，前后运动允许滑雪者操纵滑雪板的转向行为：如第2章所述的自导向效果。他们通过改变沿滑雪板长度的压力分布来做到这一点，这控制了滑雪板过度转向的趋势，并决定了滑雪板的一部分比另一部分反向拱形更多弯折。

#### 0.1.1 Moving Fore and Aft

> 1.1.1.1 前后移动

Ask most skiers to move from tip to tail on their skis, and they'll lean their whole bodies to and fro (figure 5.3). This is how they've learned to control fore–aft balance over a lifetime of standing on surfaces that give them reliable and significant friction. They move their centers of gravity back and forth over their feet, which don't move.

> 让大多数滑雪者从滑雪板的前端移动到后端，他们会整个身体前后来回倾斜（如图5.3所示）。这是他们一生中在提供可靠和显著摩擦力的表面站立时学会控制前后平衡的方式。他们在脚不动的情况下，在自己的重心在脚上前后移动。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_89_Picture_6.jpeg)

Figure 5.3 In most nonskiing situations, people adjust their fore–aft balance by moving their upper bodies back and forth over their feet.

> 图5.3 在大多数非滑雪情况下，人们通过前后摇动上半身来调整重心，使其在脚上移动。

When standing on something slippery, like skis on snow, there's a better way to make most fore–aft adjustments: by sliding your feet back and forth under your body (figures 5.4 and 5.5). Because your skis, boots, bindings, and lower legs are much less massive than your torso, arms, and head, you can adjust more quickly and precisely this way than by moving your upper body. The primary action happens in the ankles, with a bit of movement at the knee.

> 当站在如滑雪板在雪地上的滑溜表面时，做出前后调整的更好方法是：通过在身体下方前后滑动脚（图5.4和5.5）。由于您的滑雪板、靴子、绑定装置以及小腿的重量远小于躯干、手臂和头部的重量，因此这种方式可以更快更精确地进行调整，与移动上半身相比。主要的动作发生在踝关节，膝盖也会有一些活动。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_90_Picture_3.jpeg)

Figure 5.4 The expert skier controls pressure on the skis fore and aft by moving the feet forward and backward under the body. This is done primarily by flexing and straightening the ankles.

> 图5.4：专家滑雪者通过在身体下方前后移动双脚来控制滑雪板上的压力。这主要是通过弯曲和伸直脚踝来完成的。

![](_page_90_Picture_5.jpeg)

Figure 5.5 2007 World Cup overall champion Nicole Hosp of Austria bends her ankles to move her balance forward as she enters this turn. In the last two frames, she progressively straightens her ankles, moving her feet forward relative to her center of gravity.

> 图5.5 在进入这个弯道时，奥地利的2007年世界杯总冠军Nicole Hosp弯下脚踝以调整重心向前。在最后两帧画面中，她逐渐伸直脚踝，将双脚向前移动至其重心下方。

#### 0.1.2 Bode Miller

> 1.1.1.2 贝德·米勒

Bode Miller is one of the best and most innovative skiers of his time. Bode finished in the top four in the race for the overall World Cup for seven straight years, from 2002 to 2008. He has won more World Cup races than any other American. He is also one of only two men—the other being Marc Girardelli—to have won World Cup races in all five disciplines in a single season. While it took Girardelli most of the season to accomplish this feat, Miller did it in six weeks.

> 鲍德·米勒是他那个时代最出色、最具创新精神的滑雪运动员之一。从2002年到2008年，他在总积分世界巡回赛中的成绩连续七年都排在前四名，这是他的一大成就。他是所有美国选手中获得世界巡回赛胜利次数最多的运动员。他也成为仅有的两名在一个赛季内所有五个项目都赢得过世界巡回赛的男子运动员之一——另一人是马尔科·吉拉德利。然而相比之下，吉拉德利完成这一壮举需要整个赛季的努力，而米勒只花了六周时间就做到了这一点。

While a teenager, Miller realized before anyone else the potential of shaped skis for racing, choosing to compete on recreational shaped skis when they first appeared on the market rather than the best racing skis of the time, which had conventional sidecuts. And he won races on them. As he moved to international competition and began his rise to the highest ranks, coaches and competitors could see that it was only a matter of time before he would be the man to beat in slalom and giant slalom.

> 在青少年时期，米勒在其他人之前意识到剪裁形状的滑雪板在竞赛中的潜力，他选择在市场刚推出休闲型剪裁滑雪板时就在其上参赛，而不是当时最好的竞速滑雪板（这些滑雪板具有传统的侧弯度），尽管这些最新款式的滑雪板还没有采用新型的剪裁设计。但他凭借这些滑雪板赢得了比赛。随着他的国际赛事经验不断增加并逐步向最高水平提升，教练和竞争对手都开始意识到他将成为未来在小回转和大回转比赛中击败所有人的关键人物。

Bode did become that man. It was as if he saw the possibilities in slalom and giant slalom technique and line, and was waiting for the equipment to catch up with him. As it did, and as his technique and tactics developed, he became not just the man to beat, but the man to watch. He consistently tested the limits of early inclination and edge angle, using aggressive knee angulation to get the ski engaged and carving. This enabled him to push his line to the absolute limit. When he pushed it too hard, his innate athleticism and ability to improvise usually kept him in the course.

> 布德确实成为了那样的人。仿佛他看到了回转和大回转技巧及线路的可能性，并在等待装备与之相匹配。当装备跟上，随着他的技术战术发展，他不仅成为了难以战胜的人，更是被关注的对象。他始终测试着早期倾向和刃角的极限，利用激进的膝盖角度让滑雪板参与并进行刻面滑行，这使得他能够将线路推到绝对极限。当他用力过猛时，天生的运动能力以及即兴的能力通常都能使他在比赛中保持在正确的轨道上。

In the 2004-05 season, Miller turned his attention to downhill and super G, and won the overall World Cup title. In addition, he won the super G crown and was second for the season in both giant slalom and downhill. He has continued to be successful in speed events, as many of the great skiers before him—such as Girardelli, Aamodt, and Kjus—were in the latter halves of their careers.

> 在2004-05赛季，米勒将注意力转向了大回转和超级大回转，并赢得了总体积分杯。此外，在巨型回转和大回转项目上他获得了第二名的好成绩，并且在速度类赛事中持续取得了成功，这与很多之前伟大的滑雪者（如吉拉德利、阿莫特和库斯）的职业生涯晚期表现相似。

Bode has left an indelible mark on skiing and ski racing. He envisioned what was possible and went for it, daring everyone else to match him. Time and again he has proven that his vision was true, and that few were capable of matching it.

> 鲍德已经给滑雪和滑雪比赛留下了难以磨灭的印记。他想象到了可能达到的目标，并勇敢地去实现，挑战其他人也跟上他的步伐。一次又一次，他证明了他的愿景是真实的，而且很少有人有能力与之匹敌。

exercise 锻炼

Straightening the ankle, or plantarflexion, pushes the foot forward. Bending the ankle, or dorsiflexion, pulls the foot back. To feel these movements, start from a flexed stance that places your center of gravity over the arches of your feet, with your ankles bent slightly so your shins are lightly touching the tongues of your boots. To shift your balance forward from this neutral position, dorsiflex by pushing your knees forward, drawing your feet back, or pulling your toes up. (These are all essentially the same movement.) As your shins start to press firmly against the tongues of your boots, the boots act like levers to increase the shift in pressure toward the tips of your skis. To move your balance back, straighten your ankles, or plantarflex them. You can think of sliding your feet forward, or pushing your toes down. Move only your feet. Whatever you do, don't rock your upper body backward. Once your calf muscles have started to press against the backs of your boots, the pressure will shift quickly toward the tails of your skis.

> 正直踝关节，或称为跖屈运动，是将脚向前推的动作。弯曲踝关节，或称为背屈运动，则是把脚拉向后方。为了体验这些动作，可以从一种重心位于脚弓之上的折腰姿态开始，让脚踝略微弯曲，这样可以让小腿轻轻地接触靴子的舌头部分。从这个中性位置向前转移平衡时，可以通过推膝盖前倾、收回双脚或抬起脚趾（所有这些都是本质上相同的动作）来执行背屈运动。当小腿开始紧紧地顶在靴子的舌面上时，靴子就像杠杆一样增加压力向滑雪板的前端转移。

为了将重心后移，可以伸直脚踝，或者执行跖屈动作。你可以想象是向前滑动双脚或向下推脚趾。只移动你的双脚。无论你做什么，都不要让上半身向后摇摆。一旦小腿开始挤压靴子的背部时，压力就会迅速转移到滑雪板的尾部。

The key is to control the pressure as much as possible by bending and straightening the ankles only (see figure 5.7, p. 82). Of all the joints in your body, the ankles have the greatest effect on your fore–aft balance. Changing the flex in the ankles by 10 degrees, for example, moves an average person's center of gravity forward by about 6 inches (15 cm), about the span from the ball of the foot to the heel. Once your knees, hips, and shoulders are comfortably flexed in an athletic posture, they should have minimal involvement in fore–aft adjustments.

> 关键是通过仅弯曲和伸直脚踝来尽可能地控制压力（见第5.7节中的图，页82）。在您身体的所有关节中，脚踝对您的前后平衡影响最大。例如，将脚踝的柔韧性改变10度可以将普通人的重心向前移动约6英寸（15厘米），大约是从脚掌到脚跟的距离。一旦膝盖、髋部和肩膀舒适地弯曲成运动姿势，它们在前后调整中的作用就应最小化。

Making turns in a tuck on a flat run or road, as shown in figure 5.6, is a good exercise for developing fore–aft movement in the ankles. As you go into the turn, move forward over your skis by bending your ankles, feeling an increase in pressure under the balls of your feet and against the tongues of your boots. As you finish the turn, slide your feet forward until you feel light pressure from your boot cuffs on the backs of your legs. Keep your chest down and your back flat. This will prevent you from moving your center of gravity by flexing or extending at the waist or dropping your hips.

> 在平坦的滑道上或跑道上，如图5.6所示的用下蹲姿势进行转弯是一个很好的练习来发展踝关节的前后运动。当您进入转弯时，通过弯曲脚踝向前移动，在脚底和靴子舌头下感受到压力增加。在完成转弯后，将双脚向前滑动，直到感觉到从靴筒在大腿后面的压力。保持胸部低垂和背部平坦。这样做可以防止您通过腰部弯曲或伸展或降低臀部来移动身体的重心。

As you make these movements, you should be able to feel the spot under your feet where the pressure is highest. This is the point your center of gravity is directly over, as determined by the balance axis: the line of action of gravity and centrifugal force combined.

> 当你做出这些动作时，你应该能够感觉到脚下的那个受压点。这就是在重力和离心力作用线确定的平衡轴上，你的重心直接位于其正下方的那个点。

Your ski boots aren't shoes. They are your skis' handles, and just as you manipulate a tennis racket or golf club through its handle, you manipulate your skis with your boots. The higher and stiffer they are, the more responsive your skis will be to small movements of your legs and ankles. Pressing sharply against the front of the boot puts a shot of pressure on the ski's forebody. Unless this pressure is accompanied by a movement of your center of gravity toward the tip of the ski, it will be short lived. The back of the boot provides leverage on the tail of the ski and support that enables you to pressure the tail quickly without getting out of balance (figure 5.7).

> 你的滑雪靴不是鞋子，它们是你的雪橇的把手。就像你通过球拍或高尔夫球棒的手柄来操纵网球和高尔夫一样，你也通过靴子来操控你的滑雪板。靴子越高越硬，你就越能响应腿部和脚踝的小动作。在靴子前部用力压下会产生一股压力作用于滑雪板的前端部分。除非这一力量伴随着重心向滑雪板尖端移动，否则这种力量将不会持久。靴子后部为滑雪板尾部提供杠杆力与支撑，使你能在不失去平衡的情况下快速加压于尾部（图5.7）。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_93_Picture_4.jpeg)

Figure 5.7 Pushing against the front or back of the boot cuff causes big shifts in the distribution of pressure fore and aft along the ski. Lindsey Vonn gets strong tip pressure by flexing the front of her boot, and then quickly moves that pressure to the tail by straightening her ankle and pushing on the back of the boot.

> 图5.7：通过推动滑雪靴套脚部前方或后方，压力在滑雪板上前后方向上的分布会发生巨大变化。林赛·沃恩通过弯曲她靴子的前端获得强大的尖端压力，并快速通过伸直她的踝关节并在靴子后方推动来将这种压力转移到尾部。

Because boots are so critical, take care when selecting a pair, considering the type of skiing you do the most. To ski in the stiffest boots requires great finesse and ability, and you should be especially careful not to buy boots that are too stiff in the front. You'll ski better in a boot that's a bit too soft than one that's too stiff. See chapter 10 for more information on boots.

> 由于靴子极为关键，所以在挑选时要特别注意，尤其是要考虑你最常进行的滑雪类型。穿着硬壳靴滑雪需要极高的技巧和能力，因此尤其要注意不要购买前部过紧的靴子。穿略微偏软一些的靴子比穿过紧的靴子滑雪会更舒适。更多关于靴子的信息，请参阅第十章。

#### 0.1.3 Finding the Skis' Neutral Point 找到滑雪板的中性点

Every pair of skis has an optimal fore and aft neutral spot that's dictated by a combination of design factors. Skis designed primarily for medium radius turns (giant slalom skis, for example) tend to favor a neutral point at or just behind the ball of the foot, whereas skis designed for short radius turns (such as slalom skis) tend to favor a stance just a bit farther back than that. More forgiving skis have a wider range over which they respond well, whereas more demanding skis have a narrower range.

> 每对滑雪板都有一个由设计因素决定的最理想的前部和后部中性点。主要为中半径转弯设计的滑雪板（如大回转滑雪板）倾向于在脚掌后面或其正后方找到一个中性点，而专门为了短半径转弯设计的滑雪板（如回转滑雪板）则更倾向于在比脚掌稍远的位置找到这个中性点。更加宽容的滑雪板在其表现良好的范围内更宽泛，而对技术要求更高的滑雪板则有更窄的表现范围。

The following exercise, which instructors call pivot slips, is a good way to find a pair of skis' neutral point. Shown in figure 5.8, pivot slips are also a useful warm-up exercise and a good way to get acquainted with a new pair of skis or boots. I suggest making them the first thing you do when you demo a pair of skis. Start off down the fall line on a gentle slope or road. Once underway, swivel your skis back and forth 180 degrees, flat on the snow, without your body's path deviating from the fall line. Use your feet and knees to turn the skis, keeping them flat on the snow. The skis should brush lightly against the snow, but not grip or catch. You'll likely need to adjust both up and down and fore and aft to find just the right stance. Every pair of boots requires a slightly different amount of bend at the ankles, knees, and waist, and every pair of skis has its own neutral point fore and aft.

> 以下这项练习，教练称之为“旋转滑行”，是一个很好的方法来找到滑雪板的中立点。如图5.8所示，旋转滑行也是一个有用的热身练习，也是熟悉新滑雪板或靴子的好方式。我建议在试用一组新的滑雪板时，首先做这项练习。从山体倾斜的缓坡或公路开始出发，一旦开始移动，就在雪地上将你的滑雪板左右各180度旋转一次，不要让身体偏离下坡线。使用脚和膝盖来转动滑雪板，保持它们与雪面平滑接触。滑雪板应该轻轻擦过雪地，但不能抓握或卡住。你可能需要调整前后以及左右位置，以找到最适合的站姿。每双靴子在踝关节、膝关节和腰部弯曲的角度都略有不同，而每一组滑雪板都有其特定的前后中立点。

#### 0.1.4 Fore–Aft Balance 前后平衡

Because skis are so slippery fore and aft, balancing on them in the sagittal plane is much different than most other situations. You're in balance fore and aft when the balance axis, not the line of gravity, goes through your feet (figures 1.8, p. 9, and 4.3, p. 57). This is illustrated by the difference in the way beginners and experienced skiers get off a chair lift that has an unloading ramp they must ski down.

> 由于滑雪板前后都非常光滑，所以在矢状面的平衡与其他大多数情况下的平衡有很大的不同。当平衡轴而不是重力线通过你的脚（图1.8页9和4.3页57）时，你就是在前后的平衡中。这是通过新手和有经验的滑雪者在搭乘带有下坡道的吊椅缆车时下车的方式来进行说明的。

Most people taking up skiing are unaccustomed to standing on things as slippery as skis. Their bodies have learned to balance against the considerable friction that's been under their feet all their lives, and this is why so many beginners fall over the first time they get off a chair lift. Seeing that they are about to stand up on a slope (the unloading ramp), they assume a stance that anticipates a friction force pushing back up the slope on them. When that friction fails to materialize, they're left off balance.

> 大多数人开始滑雪时，对站在像滑雪板一样光滑的东西上还不习惯。他们的身体习惯了在一生中脚下摩擦力很大的平衡方式，这就是为什么很多初学者第一次下坐椅缆车时会摔倒的原因。当他们准备在坡道（卸载斜坡）站立时，采取的姿势预期的是一个向山坡推回的力量。但当这种摩擦力没有出现时，他们的身体失去了平衡。

For this person on a slope, the force that gravity exerts on her can be resolved into two components:  $G_{n'}$  which is perpendicular to the bottom of her skis and is therefore aligned with her balance axis, and  $G_{ij}$  which is trying to make her slide down the hill. In figure a, the surface on which she's standing can counterbalance both those components. Because the surface is firm, it exerts  $S_n$  on her, and because there is friction between it and her shoes, it exerts  $S_{\epsilon}$  on her. On skis, depicted in figures b and c, things are different.  $S_f$  is, for all intents and purposes, gone, and S can only oppose  $G_n$ . Because  $G_s$  is unopposed, it accelerates the skier down the hill. In figure b, the skier is in the same stance relative to her feet that she was in figure a. 1、垂直于她的滑雪底部，因此与她的平衡轴对齐的分量；

1、垂直于她的滑雪底部（即平行于平衡轴）的作用力F_g_perp，以支持她的平衡；

2、沿斜坡方向试图推动她滑下的摩擦力f。

We want to begin the control phase of most turns with at least a bit of extra tip pressure, because this makes the ski turn more sharply. In the completion phase, on the other hand, we generally want the ski to stop turning, so we shift pressure back to the middle or even to the tail of the ski. This is the typical flow of fore–aft pressure in turns (figure 5.11). In terms of where the balance axis goes through your feet, the range of movement in free skiing is usually small (perhaps an inch or two, and rarely more than from the ball of the foot to the back of the arch), but in aggressive turns the range might be larger.

> 我们希望在大多数轮次的控制阶段开始时，至少增加一些额外的小头压力，因为这会使滑雪转弯更加尖锐。相反，在完成阶段，我们通常想要滑雪停止转弯，所以我们把压力转移到滑雪板的中间甚至尾部。这就是转弯中前后压力的一般流动（图5.11）。在自由滑雪的情况下，平衡轴通过脚的动作范围通常很小（可能只有一英寸或两英寸，并且很少超过从脚掌到足弓背部），但在激烈的转弯中，这个范围可能会更大。

I've just made two statements that might appear to be at odds with each other. First, I said that you want to enter the control phase of the turn with pressure on the forebody of the ski. Then I said you want to finish the turn farther back on the ski. Because the end of one turn often melds seamlessly with the start of the next, wouldn't the finish of one turn leave you in the wrong place on the ski (back) as you enter the next turn? Not necessarily. In the transition, your center of gravity travels a shorter path between turns than your feet (see figure 9.10, p. 145). Then during this initiation phase, the skis are turned to their initial steering angle. This puts your center of gravity farther forward over the ski than it was at the end of the preceding turn. Just how far forward your center of gravity will be is determined by the angle of its trajectory to the path of your feet, your initial steering angle in your new turn, and how much you draw your feet back going into the turn (figures 5.12 and 11.4, p. 183).

> 我刚刚说了两个看似矛盾的陈述。首先，我说你希望在转弯结束时，在滑雪前体施加压力。然后我又说，你希望在下一个转弯开始时停留在滑雪板上更远的位置。因为一个转弯的结束往往与下一个转弯的开始无缝衔接，那么一个转弯结束时你在滑雪板上的位置（后部）是否会在进入下一个转弯时处于错误的位置呢？不一定。

在转换阶段中，你的身体重心在两轮之间的路径比你的脚短（见第9.10页的图9.10）。然后，在这个启动阶段，滑雪板被转向新的初始转向角度。这使得你的身体重心在滑雪板上的位置更向前方，与前一个转弯结束时的位置相比。具体到什么程度取决于你身体重心轨迹对脚部路径的角度、你在新转弯中最初的方向角以及进入转弯时将脚后拉的距离（见第183页的图5.12和图11.4）。

Only on smooth slopes and consistent snow do these cyclic fore and aft movements happen in isolation. In the real world of skiing, the arcs of your turns are folded over bumps, bent through dips, and banked off counterslopes, all of which demand fore–aft adjustments. The ability to combine these movements seamlessly is one thing that separates the best skiers from the rest. If they don't look as if they're making big movements, it's because they're making them with their feet.

> 只有在光滑的斜坡和稳定的雪地上，这种前后循环的动作才会孤立地发生。在滑雪的实际世界中，转弯的弧线会被小丘折叠、弯曲通过凹陷，并在外侧斜面处靠边滑行，所有这些都要求进行前后调整。能够无缝结合这些动作是将最好的滑雪者与其他区别开来的关键之一。如果他们看起来没有做大幅度的动作，那是因为他们是用脚在做。
