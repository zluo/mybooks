## 1 <span id="page-62-0"></span>Techniques: Controlling Your Interaction With the Snow
技巧：控制你的雪地互动

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_62_Picture_2.jpeg)

Too many skiers have the notion that there's some approved menu of correct turns that are to be executed using a narrowly choreographed sequence of movements. They are otherwise good skiers who know how to perform a variety of fundamental movements, but they combine them in rote patterns that impose the skiers' technique on the hill. They know the foxtrot, so that's the dance they do.

> 太多滑雪者认为存在一套被认可的标准转弯动作，需要通过严格编排的步骤来完成。他们其他方面技术娴熟，懂得多种基本动作，但将它们机械地组合成固定模式，将自己的技术强加于山地。他们懂得狐步舞，所以他们就跳这种舞。

Really good skiers mix, match, and vary their movements on the fly in response to the shape and texture of the snow, the pitch of the hill, the turns they want to make, and the effects they want to feel. We use words like *touch* and *feel* to describe the way these skiers come down the hill, and when we see them ski by, they communicate a pleasure that we want to feel ourselves.

> 真正优秀的滑雪者会根据雪的形状和质地、坡度、想要完成的转弯以及希望产生的效果，灵活变换动作，随时调整自己的滑行方式。我们用“触感”和“感觉”这样的词来描述这些滑雪者下滑山道的方式，当我们看到他们滑过时，他们所传达出的愉悦感，正是我们渴望体验的。

A good way to become a really good skier is to learn to make those fundamental movements in isolation. Once you can make them cleanly and independently of the other movements, you're prepared to produce the turn of the moment, rather than the turn of the textbook.

> 成为真正优秀滑雪者的好方法是学习单独练习这些基本动作。一旦你能干净利落地独立完成这些动作，不受其他动作干扰，你就能实现即时转弯，而非教科书式的转弯。

What makes these movements fundamental is that they control some basic interaction between the skis, the snow, and ourselves. We move forward and backward over the skis, for example, to maintain stability fore and aft and to control the distribution of force and pressure along the length of each ski. We edge or flatten the ski to control its grip on the snow. In technical terms, the fundamental movements of skiing are about controlling the magnitude of the force the snow exerts on us, the direction it pushes us, and how we arrange our bodies to balance against it.

> 这些动作之所以是基础性的，是因为它们控制着滑雪板、雪和我们身体之间的一些基本相互作用。例如，我们向前或向后移动滑雪板，以保持前后稳定性，并控制每块滑雪板长度上力量和压力的分布。我们通过刃边或压平滑雪板来控制其对雪的抓地力。从技术角度来说，滑雪的基本动作是关于控制雪对我们的作用力大小、方向，以及我们如何调整身体姿势以平衡这些力。

The chapters in this part of the book are about those fundamental movements. I divide them into five categories:
> 本书的这一部分章节主要介绍这些基本动作。我将它们分为五类。

- Fore and aft movements. These are movements that control the position of the skier's balance axis along the length of the skis, affecting the distribution of force forward and backward along them and hence the skis' turning behavior and the skier's stability.
> 前后运动。这些运动控制滑雪者平衡轴沿滑雪板长度的位置，影响力量在滑雪板前后方向的分布，从而影响滑雪板的转弯行为和滑雪者的稳定性。  

- Up and down movements. These are movements that control the distance between the skier's center of gravity and the skis along the skier's balance axis, affecting the total amount of force the snow exerts on the skier.
>上下运动。这些运动控制滑雪者重心与滑雪板沿平衡轴的距离，影响雪对滑雪者施加的总力量。  

- Movements that turn the skis. These are movements that control the direction the skis are pointed relative to the direction the skier is going—the skis' overall steering angle in the transverse plane.
> 转向运动。这些运动控制滑雪板指向方向相对于滑雪者行进方向的角度，即滑雪板在横断面上的整体转向角度。  

- Edging movements. These are movements that control the angle of the ski's edge relative to the force on it from the snow—the ski's platform angle, which determines how well the ski holds—and the alignment of the hips, knees, and ankles in relation to that force. These movements also fine-tune the ski's edge angle and thereby its carving radius.
> 边缘运动。这些运动控制滑雪板边缘相对于雪对其作用力的角度，即滑雪板的平台角度，这决定了滑雪板的抓地性能，以及髋、膝、踝关节相对于该力的对齐。这些运动还微调滑雪板的边缘角度，从而影响其刻滑半径。  
- 
- Lateral balancing movements. These are movements that control the skier's inclination into the turn, affecting balance against turning and slowing forces from the snow, and the ski's edge angle. These movements are also a critical ingredient in linking turns.
> 侧向平衡运动。这些运动控制滑雪者向转弯倾斜的程度，影响其抵抗转弯和减速力的平衡，以及滑雪板的边缘角度。这些运动也是连接转弯的关键要素。

The posture of your body when you're skiing—the relative arrangement of your limbs and joints and the muscles that drive them—determines the degree of ease or difficulty with which you can make these fundamental movements while balancing against the forces of skiing. This important topic is covered in chapter 4.

> 滑雪时的身体姿态——四肢、关节及驱动这些动作的肌肉的相对位置——决定了你在滑雪力量作用下保持平衡时，完成这些基本动作的难易程度。这一重要话题在第4章中有所阐述。

Because so much of ski technique is about manipulating and balancing against the skis, your skiing can be no better than the equipment that connects you with them: your boots. That connection is so important that we devote a chapter to the subtleties of boot fit and alignment.

> 因为滑雪技巧的很大一部分在于对雪板的操控与平衡，你的滑雪表现不可能超越连接你与雪板的装备：你的滑雪靴。这种连接如此重要，以至于我们专门用一章来探讨滑雪靴适配与对齐的细节。

There's a difference between *what* good coaches and instructors teach, and *how* they teach it. The first is technique; the second is teaching methodology. Suppose you want a skier to edge the skis early in the turn by rotating the outside knee inward, and at the same time put pressure on the outside ski's forebody by bending the outside ankle. These are techniques. You can simply say, "Drive your knee into the turn." This is a teaching method, which most skiers learn better from than an on-hill recitation of the technical details.

> 好教练和指导员所教授的内容与他们教授的方式是有区别的。前者是技术，后者是教学方法。假设你想让滑雪者在转弯初期通过将外侧膝盖向内旋转来实现正确刃边，同时通过弯曲外侧脚踝对滑雪板的前部施加压力。这些都属于技术。你只需简单地说：“把膝盖向转弯方向驱动。” 这是一种教学方法，大多数滑雪者通过这种方式学习比在雪道上对技术细节的讲解更容易掌握。

Good teaching and coaching depend on good methods of that kind. Understanding why and how the methods work depends on understanding technique. Understanding technique will help you apply the teaching methods you pick up from others, including the ones in this book, and help you develop your own. So that you know whether I'm presenting technique or methodology in the chapters that follow, look for these clues: When I talk about doing an exercise or what you might feel when you make a particular movement, you should generally take that as methodology. The detailed descriptions of movements are generally descriptions of technique.

> 良好的教学和指导依赖于良好的技巧方法。理解这些方法为何及如何起作用，取决于对技术的理解。理解技术将帮助你应用从他人那里学到的教学方法，包括本书中的方法，并帮助你发展自己的方法。因此，当你在后续章节中阅读时，若想判断我是阐述技术还是方法论，请留意以下线索：当我谈论进行某个练习或你在执行特定动作时可能感受到的感觉时，通常应将其视为方法论。而关于动作的详细描述通常是对技术的描述。

When you take the information and images from this part of the book out to the slopes and experiment with your technique, keep the following tips in mind:

> 当你将本书这一部分的信息和图片带到雪道上，并尝试你的技巧时，请记住以下提示：

-  Be willing to ski less than your best. You seldom do new things as well as the ones you've already got grooved, even if what you've got grooved aren't the best things you could be doing. Try new things, work at the things you don't do well, and accept that you'll feel uncoordinated and uncomfortable for a while.
>  愿意在不发挥最佳状态的情况下滑雪。你很少能像做那些已经习惯的动作一样做好新的事情，即使你已经习惯的那些动作并不是你所能做的最好的。尝试新事物，努力改进那些你不擅长的动作，并接受在一段时间内你会感到不协调和不自在。

-  Exaggerate. A new movement or change in stance will feel bigger than it is. And if there's something you haven't been doing enough of, you have to know what it feels like to do too much before you can know what's right.
 > 放大动作。新的动作或站姿改变会感觉比实际更大。如果你没有足够做某件事，你必须先知道做太多是什么感觉，才能知道什么是正确的。
 
-  Simplify the training environment. Work on skills in terrain and snow that are relatively easy so you can focus on the tasks at hand.
 简化训练环境。在相对容易的地形和雪况下练习技能，以便你能专注于手头的任务。
### 1.1 <span id="page-65-0"></span>Alignment and Stance 对齐和站姿

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_65_Picture_2.jpeg)

FIGURE 4.1 Each skier has a gestalt that an experienced instructor or coach will quickly pick up. This personal signature expresses how comfortable the skier is, how in tune with the snow and terrain, and how able to act at will or react to the unexpected. We rely on a single word to describe this collection of individual technical details and how they fit together: *stance*. What we look for when we assess a skier's stance is the alignment of the key parts of the skier's body, both with the forces of skiing and with each other. Skier: David N. Oliver.

> 图4.1 每位滑雪者都有一个整体印象，有经验的教练或指导者能迅速察觉到。这种个人特征表达了滑雪者在雪道上的自如程度、与雪和地形的契合度，以及其自主行动或应对意外情况的能力。我们用一个词来描述这些技术细节的集合及其相互配合：姿态。在评估滑雪者姿态时，我们关注其身体关键部位的对齐情况，包括与滑雪力量的协调以及身体各部分之间的协调。滑雪者：大卫·N·奥利弗

s skis have become more sophisticated, Athe forces skiers develop in turns have increased, which means proper alignment and stance are now more important than ever. Just as there are safe and unsafe ways to align your body when lifting heavy objects, there are safe and unsafe ways to align it when skiing. Whenever you're in a flexed, athletic stance, you're depending on certain muscles to prevent your body from collapsing. When bending to lift a heavy box, you bend at the knees rather than at the waist. That's because bending at the knees supports the load with your thigh and buttock muscles, whereas bending at the waist puts unsafe demands on the muscles of your lower back. In skiing, too, an effective stance is based on the best alignment of muscles and bones to balance the forces of skiing so that you can remain strong, supple, and efficient as you move through turns and variations in terrain and snow (figure 4.1).

> 滑雪板技术已经变得更加先进，滑雪者在转弯时所产生力量也大幅增加，这意味着正确的身体对齐和站姿比以往任何时候都更加重要。就像在提起重物时，有安全和不安全的身体对齐方式一样，在滑雪时也有安全和不安全的身体对齐方式。无论你采用的是屈膝的、运动型站姿，你都依赖某些肌肉来防止身体塌陷。当你弯腰提起重物时，是屈膝而不是弯腰。这是因为屈膝时，大腿和臀部肌肉可以支撑负荷，而弯腰则会对下背部肌肉造成不安全的负担。在滑雪中也是如此，有效的站姿基于肌肉和骨骼的最佳对齐，以平衡滑雪时的力量，使你在通过转弯和地形及雪况变化时保持强壮、灵活和高效（图4.1）。

The alignment of your body segments strongly influences your range of motion, too, and your ability to accurately balance and control the edges of your skis as you make all the movements described in the subsequent chapters of the book.

> 身体各部位的对齐显著影响你的运动范围，同时也影响你执行书中后续章节中描述的所有动作时准确平衡和控制滑雪板边刃的能力。

#### 1.1.1 General Alignment and Stance Principles 姿势对齐与站姿原则

A few biomechanical principles will help us understand what makes for good alignment and stance. One or more of them underpin every topic in this chapter and every facet of technique in this book. Taken on their own, they are basic principles of good skiing.

> 一些生物力学原理将帮助我们理解什么是良好的姿态和站位。这些原理中的一个或多个支撑着本章的每一个主题，以及本书中技术的每一个方面。单独来看，它们是良好滑雪技术的基本原则。

*The Balance Axis is the Reference for Alignment.* Balancing against the forces of skiing means balancing along the balance axis, defined in chapter 1. While gravity plays a part in where the balance axis is, it's only one of several factors, so a true vertical line is not of much use for us here.

> 平衡轴是保持对齐的参考线。在滑雪的受力下保持平衡意味着沿着第1章中定义的平衡轴进行平衡。虽然重力在确定平衡轴的位置中起作用，但它只是众多因素之一，因此在这里真正的垂直线对我们帮助不大。

*Each Segment of the Body Balances on the One Below It.* Your torso balances on the heads of your femurs. Your torso, hips, and upper legs balance on top of your knees. Your torso, hips, and legs balance on your ankles and feet. When you're in a turn on firm snow, most of your body's mass is balanced on one leg, knee, and ankle. Depending on your alignment, different muscles are called on at each of the major joints to do the work of supporting and balancing the body segments above it. Good alignment enlists the strongest and best muscles.

> 身体的每个部位都平衡在下方的部位之上。你的躯干平衡在股骨的顶部。你的躯干、髋部和大腿上半部分平衡在膝盖之上。你的躯干、髋部和腿平衡在脚踝和脚上。当你在硬雪上转弯时，大部分身体重量集中在一条腿、膝盖和脚踝上。根据你的身体姿势，每个主要关节处会调动不同的肌肉来支撑和平衡上方的身体部位。正确的姿势能调动最强大且高效的肌肉。

*The Upper and Lower Body Must Work Independently.* The lower body—the legs and feet—plays a distinctly different role from the upper body (that portion from the hips up). You ski with your legs. You balance with your upper body. On average, about 65 percent of a person's mass is contained in the upper body and about 35 percent in the legs and feet. Looking at a skier from the front, the center of gravity is located somewhere just above the hips. As a result, small movements of the upper body have a disproportionately large effect on a skier's balance and the distribution of force and pressure on the skis. That's why the best skiers keep their upper bodies quiet. They put their torso over the part of the ski they want to apply force to, and they don't move it much. If they want more force on the outside ski, the upper body goes over that ski. If they want even pressure between their skis, the upper body is in the middle.

> 上下身必须独立运作。下半身（即双腿和双脚）在滑雪中扮演着与上半身（从髋部以上部位）截然不同的作用。滑雪时，你依靠双腿发力，依靠上半身保持平衡。通常来说，人体的上半身约占体重的65%，而下半身（包括双腿和双脚）约占35%。从正面观察滑雪者，其重心一般位于髋部上方不远处。因此，上半身的微小动作会对滑雪者的平衡以及滑雪板上的受力和压力分布产生不成比例的显著影响。这就是为什么优秀的滑雪者会保持上半身的静止。他们会将躯干重心放在想要施力的滑雪板部分上，并且不会过多移动。如果他们希望让外侧滑雪板承受更多力量，上半身就会向该滑雪板倾斜；如果他们希望两滑雪板之间压力均匀，上半身就会位于中间位置。

The best skiers use their legs, whose joints have many degrees of freedom, to manipulate the skis underneath the massive, quiet upper body. The legs turn the skis, control their edges, and adjust their fore–aft position relative to the skier's center of gravity.

> 最佳滑雪者利用其多自由度的腿部关节，操控雪板，以保持沉重而稳定的上半身。腿部负责转动雪板，控制其边刃，并调整雪板相对于滑雪者重心的前后位置。

*The Legs Must Work Independently.* Racers have always known that the functionally best technique in skiing is based on using their legs independently. Like the independent suspension on each of the wheels of a high-performance sports car, each leg must respond in its own way to variations in the shape of the snow surface. When you're in a turn, you apply force to the inside edge of one foot and the outside edge of the other, and your inclination requires your inside leg to be bent more than your outside leg. Any element of stance that prevents them from being able to do their own thing hinders you.

> 腿部必须独立运作。竞速选手们一直知道，在滑雪技巧中，功能上最优秀的技巧是基于腿部独立运作的。就像高性能跑车的每个车轮都配有独立悬挂系统，每条腿也必须以各自的方式应对雪面形状的变化。当你在转弯时，你需要将力量施加在一只脚的内侧边和另一只脚的外侧边，而你的身体倾斜则要求你的内侧腿比外侧腿弯曲得更多。任何影响腿部独立运作的站姿要素都会阻碍你的表现。

*The Hips Must Be Stable and Well Controlled.* Some of the best coaching methods focus on the hips: "Move your hips forward into the turn." "Stabilize your hips." "Keep your hips over your feet." The hips play a central role in skiing because they are, literally, central to your body. The hips are where the upper and lower body meet, and if the upper and lower body are to work independently, the muscles around the hips must constantly contract and relax in a coordinated fashion to adjust your balance and manipulate your skis while adapting to changes in external forces. These muscles support and balance the mass of the upper body over the heads of the femurs, and good alignment in the midbody is largely a matter of arranging the hips in the sagittal, frontal, and transverse planes so that the strongest, most effective muscles are used.

>臀部必须稳定且受到良好控制. 一些最有效的教学方法都聚焦于臀部：“将你的臀部向前移入转弯。”“稳定你的臀部。”“让臀部保持在脚的正上方。” 臀部在滑雪中扮演核心角色，因为它的位置确实在身体的中心。臀部是上半身与下半身的连接处；如果上、下半身要实现相对独立的动作，那么臀部周围的肌肉就必须**不断地协同收缩与放松**，以调整平衡、操控雪板，并适应外部力量的变化。

这些肌肉将上半身的质量支撑并平衡在股骨头之上，而所谓的良好中段对齐（midbody alignment）在很大程度上就是要在**矢状面、额状面和横断面**上正确摆放臀部，使最强、最有效的肌肉群得到运用。
The hips are also near your center of gravity. So it's more than poetic license to say that your body revolves around them. If they move erratically, your entire body moves that way, and your center of gravity with it. Be careful, though, not to confuse your hips with your center of gravity, because it's the latter that determines your state of balance, not the former.

> 骨盆也位于你的重心附近。因此，说你的身体围绕它们运转，不只是诗意的表达。如果骨盆移动不规律，你的整个身体也会随之移动，重心也随之变化。不过，要注意不要将骨盆与重心混淆，因为真正决定你平衡状态的是重心，而不是骨盆。

A lot of skiing happens in the ball-and-socket joints where the femurs meet the pelvis. Some of their movements are controlled by the biggest muscles in the body, and their three degrees of freedom and range of articulation give them great versatility. Having so many options for movement provides you with many ways to accomplish any particular task, but only a few of them are optimal. Consequently, the alignment of the hips in relation to the legs and upper body, which is one of the main subjects in this chapter, plays a central role in ski technique.

> 滑雪时，很多动作发生在股骨与骨盆相接的球窝关节处。其中一些运动由人体最大的肌肉群控制，而这些关节具有三个自由度和广泛的活动范围，赋予了它们高度的灵活性。拥有如此多的运动选择，使你能够以多种方式完成特定动作，但其中只有少数几种是最优的。因此，髋关节相对于腿部和上半身的位置关系，这是本章的主要内容之一，在滑雪技术中起着核心作用。

*You Must Be Able to Act and React From a Neutral Stance.* Skiers can learn a lot about stance by watching athletes in other sports. A football linebacker defending against an oncoming opponent provides a good model for a skier's neutral, homebase stance. The linebacker needs to be in a posture from which he can move in any direction as quickly and powerfully as possible, and take a hit without getting knocked out of balance. This stance is flexed at all the major joints. The hands are at the same height, about halfway between the hips and shoulder. The athlete's balance is on the balls of his feet. With a couple of tweaks—the stance is generally narrower and the balance maybe a hair farther back—this is the basic neutral stance of an athletic skier.

> 你必须能够从中立姿势中迅速做出反应和行动。滑雪者可以通过观察其他运动项目的运动员来学习站姿。例如，美式足球中防守方的 linebacker（防守线卫）就为滑雪者的中立基准站姿提供了良好的参照。linebacker 需要保持一种能够迅速且强有力地向任何方向移动，并且在受到冲撞时不会失去平衡的姿势。这种姿势的特征是所有主要关节都略微弯曲。双手保持在同一高度，大约位于髋部和肩部之间的中点位置。运动员的重心应放在脚掌上。通过一些微调——通常站姿更窄，重心可能略微后移——这就是滑雪运动员基本的中立站姿。

Each of the movements of skiing has its own range. A skier's vertical movement, for example, can range from standing almost bolt upright to a low tuck or lower. There's a point within each of these ranges that we can think of as its neutral point: a place the skier will go to to be ready to move in one direction or another within that range. The collection of all those neutral points defines a skier's overall neutral stance or home base.

> 滑雪的每一个动作都有其自身的范围。例如，滑雪者的垂直移动可以从几乎直立站立到低姿或更低的位置。在每个这样的范围内，存在一个我们可以称之为中立点的位置：滑雪者会前往此位置，以便在该范围内准备向任一方向移动。所有这些中立点的集合定义了滑雪者的总体中立姿势或基础位置。

Good skiers continually pass through the neutral points of various movements when they're skiing, but they don't linger there, and there are few moments in dynamic skiing when all movement patterns are in their various neutral points at the same time.

> 优秀的滑雪者在滑雪过程中不断经过各种动作的中立点，但他们不会在此停留，而在动态滑行中，几乎很少有时刻所有动作模式同时处于各自的中立点。

#### 1.2.1 *Center of Gravity* 重心（滑雪术语）

We often talk about having your balance, or your center of gravity, "over your feet." Sounds simple enough, doesn't it? But it's not. At the heart of the issue is what "over" means. It doesn't mean that your center of gravity and feet must be aligned with respect to gravity. It means they must be aligned with respect to the balance axis, as defined in chapter 1 (figures 1.17 and 1.18 on p. 17). Recall that the balance axis goes through your center of gravity and is perpendicular to the bottom of your skis, and

> 我们经常谈到要把重心放在脚下。听起来很简单，对吧？但实际上并非如此。问题的核心在于“放在脚下”中的“放”具体指的是什么。它并不意味着你的重心和双脚必须相对于重力方向对齐，而是相对于平衡轴对齐，如第1章（第17页图1.17和图1.18）中定义的那样。请回忆一下，平衡轴穿过你的重心，并且垂直于滑雪板的底板，

is seldom aligned with gravity (figure 1.8 on p. 9). So when a coach says your balance is over your feet, that means the balance axis falls somewhere between the balls of your feet and the back of your arch when viewed in the sagittal plane.

> 很少与重力对齐（见图1.8，第9页）。因此，当教练说你的重心在双脚之上时，这意味着在矢状面观察时，平衡轴位于脚掌与脚弓后部之间。

That's the essential rule of fore–aft balance, and it implies some interesting things about your hips and shoulders. To be in a flexed, athletic stance, your shoulders must be ahead of your hips. Furthermore, your shoulders will be ahead of your center of gravity and your hips behind it. And unless you're standing pretty tall, your shoulders will be ahead of your feet and your hips will be behind them, with respect to the balance axis, which is the reference of alignment that really matters. There's a tried and true coaching method that says, "Keep your hips over your feet." It's an effective method, but it's technically inaccurate, as Lindsey Vonn shows in figure 4.3 and Aksel Lund Svindal shows in figure 4.4.

> 这就是前后平衡的基本规则，这意味着关于你的髋部和肩膀有一些有趣之处。为了保持屈膝的运动姿势，你的肩膀必须位于髋部的前方。此外，你的肩膀会位于你的重心前方，而髋部则位于重心后方。除非你站得非常挺拔，否则你的肩膀会位于双脚的前方，而髋部则位于双脚的后方，这种关系是以平衡轴为参考的，而平衡轴正是真正重要的对齐基准。有一种经过验证的教练方法说：“保持髋部在脚部正上方。”这是一种有效的方法，但技术上是不准确的，正如林赛·沃恩在图4.3中所展示的那样，以及阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔在图4.4中所展示的那样。

#### 1.2.2 *Lower-Leg Angle* 小腿角度

The angle of the skier's lower leg in the sagittal plane controls fore–aft balance more than that of any other part of the body, and the forward angle of the boot's cuff determines the lower-leg angle's neutral point. Every degree of change in the angle of the lower leg moves your center of gravity forward or backward about 10 times as much as a similar change in the angle at which you bend forward at the waist. Changing the leg's angle by just 5 degrees moves your center of gravity about 3 inches (7.6 cm)—about the distance from the middle of your arch to the ball of your foot.

> 滑雪者小腿在矢状面的角度对前后平衡的控制作用，比身体其他任何部位都更为重要。滑雪靴筒的前倾角度决定了小腿角度的中立位。小腿角度每改变一度，重心前后移动的距离是腰部弯曲角度改变相同度数时的十倍。仅将腿部角度改变5度，重心就会移动约3英寸（7.6厘米），也就是大约从脚弓中部到脚掌前部的距离。

Because ski boots don't allow the ankles much range of motion in the sagittal plane, the skier can't adjust that angle much while skiing. So finding a boot with the right neutral point, the right range, and the right stiffness of forward flex is fundamental to good balance fore and aft.

> 由于滑雪靴在矢状面限制了脚踝的活动范围，滑雪者在滑雪过程中无法大幅调整该角度。因此，找到具有正确中立点、合适活动范围和适当前倾刚度的滑雪靴，对于实现前后平衡至关重要。

The cuffs of ski boots are pitched forward at an angle, called *forward lean,* so that the skier's center of gravity can stay over the feet while flexing into a low stance (figure 4.5). Too little forward lean causes the skier to lose balance to the rear when flexing deeply (figure 4.6), which is a far more common problem than too much forward lean. Too much forward lean might require the skier to adopt a vertical neutral point that's too low for comfort, too upright at the waist for smooth, vertical motion, or too far forward on the skis. World-class racers and mogul skiers (conditioned skiers for whom the ability to ski effectively in a low stance is important) typically ski in boots with enough forward lean to allow them to flex very deeply. The subject of forward lean, including how to measure how much you need and how to adjust your boots to get it, is covered in depth in chapter 10.

> 滑雪靴的护踝部分呈前倾角度，这种角度称为“前倾”（前倾角度），这样滑雪者在屈膝进入低重心姿势时，身体重心仍能保持在双脚上方（见图4.5）。前倾不足会导致滑雪者在深屈膝时向后失去平衡（见图4.6），这种问题比前倾过度更为常见。前倾过度可能迫使滑雪者采用过低的垂直中立点，影响舒适度，或使腰部过于挺直，影响垂直运动的流畅性，甚至可能使滑雪者在滑雪板上过于前倾。世界级的竞速滑雪者和障碍滑雪者（即需要在低重心姿势下有效滑雪的专业滑雪者）通常使用前倾角度适中的滑雪靴，以便能深入屈膝。关于前倾角度的详细内容，包括如何测量所需的前倾角度以及如何调整滑雪靴以达到理想效果，将在第10章中深入探讨。

#### 1.2.3 Aksel Lund Svindal 阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔

Aksel Lund Svindal has continued a trend in World Cup athletes since the tour's inception in the late 1960s: The best overall skiers have gotten bigger. Both Svindal and Bode Miller, the last two skiers to have won the overall World Cup more than once, are almost 10 percent taller and fully a third heavier than Killy, Schranz, and Thoeni, the first three champions.

> 自上世纪60年代末期世界杯赛事创办以来，阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔延续了世界杯运动员的一个趋势：最佳的全能滑雪运动员普遍变得更高大。斯文达尔和博迪·米勒这两位迄今为止获得世界杯全能冠军次数最多的滑雪运动员，身高比基利、施拉恩茨和滑尼这三位早期冠军高出近10%，体重则重了三分之一。

Another more recent trend is that in the last decade, the overall title on the men's side has generally gone to competitors who have excelled in speed events, which favor bigger athletes. The World Cup has more downhill and super G events, and fewer slaloms and giant slaloms. Compounding this effect is the fact that slalom and giant slalom have, by their nature, more uncertainty and variability in their results. The best skiers in the technical events seldom get on the podium with the consistency of those in the speed disciplines.

> 近年来的一个趋势是，在过去十年中，男子项目的总冠军通常由在速度项目中表现出色的选手获得，而这类项目更倾向于高大强壮的运动员。世界杯赛程中，下坡和超级大回转项目占比更高，而回转和大回转项目则相对较少。加剧这一趋势的是，回转和大回转项目本身具有更高的不确定性和结果波动性。技术项目中的顶尖选手在领奖台上表现出的稳定性，往往不及速度项目选手。

In these respects, Svindal has a lot going for him. He's big, and he's very good at the speed events, as well as giant slalom. He is very controlled in the movements he makes to stay in contact with the snow through the transition, so that, in turns where others get jacked from the snow, Svindal's skis stay close to the ground, ready to engage the next turn and work its arc. His shoulders are always ahead of his hips, even when he moves pressure back to the heel of his foot, and he rarely gets stretched out with his feet ahead of him going into the turn.

> 在这方面，斯文达尔有很多优势。他体型高大，在速度赛和大回转项目中表现非常出色。他在过渡阶段的移动非常精准，能够保持与雪面的接触，因此在那些其他人会被甩离雪面的弯道中，斯文达尔的滑雪板始终紧贴地面，随时准备进入下一个弯道并完成其轨迹。他的肩膀始终位于髋部前方，即使在将压力转移到脚跟时也是如此。他很少在进入弯道时因双脚过于靠前而显得僵硬。

Svindal's personality is one of his biggest competitive assets. He just doesn't seem to get rattled. He steps up in big events, competes well in tough conditions, and can return from setbacks as well as anyone, if not better. In December 2006, in a training run for the Birds of Prey downhill at Beaver Creek, one of the most difficult courses in the world, Svindal uncharacteristically made a terrible error at the takeoff of the Golden Eagle jump, the biggest on the course. Flying through the air at around 70 mph, Svindal flipped over backward, landing 200 feet (60 m) down the hill on his shoulders and neck. He suffered broken bones in his face and a large gash in his groin, probably from his ski. After missing the entire remainder of the season, Aksel returned to Beaver Creek the next December for the first downhill of the year and won. He also won the super G contested the next day on the same hill, and placed third in the third race of the weekend, a giant slalom.

> 斯文达尔的个性是他最大的竞争优势之一。他似乎从未显得紧张不安。在大赛中他能表现出色，即使在恶劣的条件下也能发挥良好，而且在遭遇挫折后也能迅速恢复，甚至比其他人更出色。2006年12月，在贝弗利克举行的“猛禽速降赛”训练赛中，斯文达尔在金雕跳台上犯了一个不寻常的严重失误，这是赛道上最大的跳台。他以约70英里的时速在空中飞行时，突然翻转着倒退着坠落，最终落在山坡下方200英尺（60米）处的肩膀和颈部。他面部骨折，大腿根部还有一道较大的伤口，可能是被滑雪板划伤所致。在缺席了整个赛季之后，斯文达尔次年12月重返贝弗利克，赢得了当年的首场速降赛。他还在同一天的同一山坡上赢得了超级大回转赛，并在周末的第三场比赛——大回转项目中获得第三名。

That year Svindal went on to win the overall World Cup title, as he had the year before his injury, edging out Benjamin Raich of Austria with 1,009 points to Raich's 1,007, the smallest margin in World Cup history.

> 那一年，斯文达尔再次夺得世界杯总冠军，与他受伤前一年一样，以1009分力压奥地利选手贝内迪克特·莱希的1007分，创造了世界杯历史上最小的分差。

Continuing a long tradition of exceptional Norwegian skiers, Aksel Lund Svindal is a model of technique as well as competitive spirit, and we can expect to see him on the top step of many podiums in the future.

> 延续挪威滑雪运动员悠久的传统，阿克塞尔·伦德·斯文达尔不仅技术精湛，而且竞技精神十足，未来我们有望在众多领奖台的最高台阶上看到他的身影。

#### 1.2.4 *Lead* 领先

In a parallel turn, lead is the distance the inside ski is ahead of the outside when viewed in the sagittal plane. Back in the1950s and 1960s, skiers were often taught to deliberately shove the inside ski ahead of the other one when starting a turn, and many thought that the lead change a good skier made going from one turn to the next was a fundamental movement of good turns. This is no longer widely thought. The inside ski does lead the outside ski in virtually all good turns made on alpine ski equipment, but it's the result of other movements rather than a deliberate act of advancing the inside ski for its own sake.

> 在平行转弯中，“领先”指的是在矢状面上观察时，内侧雪板相对于外侧雪板所处的前方距离。20世纪50年代和60年代，滑雪者通常被教导在开始转弯时，故意将内侧雪板向前推，以带动外侧雪板。当时许多人认为，优秀滑雪者在从一个转弯过渡到下一个转弯时所表现出的“领先”变化，是良好转弯动作的基本要素。但如今这种观点已不再被广泛认同。实际上，几乎所有使用高山滑雪装备完成的良好转弯中，内侧雪板都会领先于外侧雪板，但这种领先是其他动作的自然结果，而非为了自身目的而有意将内侧雪板向前推进的刻意行为。

When you're inclined into a turn, your inside leg must be bent more than your outside leg, as if you were standing in a traverse (figure 4.7). In particular, the inside femur is articulated more in the hip than the outside femur, which brings your inside knee forward of the outside knee in the sagittal plane. Because the ski boot allows little flex of the inside ankle, your inside foot must come forward with the knee, establishing the inside ski's lead (figures 4.8 and 4.9).

> 当你进入转弯时倾斜，你的内侧腿必须比外侧腿弯曲得更多，就像你站在横移时一样（图4.7）。特别是，内侧大腿骨在髋关节处的活动范围比外侧大腿骨更大，这会使得你的内侧膝盖在矢状面上位于外侧膝盖的前方。由于滑雪靴对内侧脚踝的弯曲限制较小，你的内侧脚必须随着膝盖向前移动，从而确定内侧滑雪板的主导位置（图4.8和图4.9）。

The more inclination you have, the greater the difference in flex between your inside and outside femurs, and the more lead your inside ski will have. The lead is also increased by the degree to which your hips are rotated in the transverse plane (about the balance axis) toward the outside of the turn, a subject we'll address in our discussion of counter (see p. 73).

> 你的倾斜度越大，内侧和外侧大腿骨的弯曲度差异就越大，内滑雪板的领先性也就越强。此外，你的髋部在横断平面（围绕平衡轴）向转弯外侧旋转的程度也会影响内滑雪板的领先性，这将在我们讨论反向动作时详细探讨（详见第73页）。

For decades, instructors said that a line drawn between the tips of the skis should be parallel to a line drawn between the hip joints and another line drawn between the shoulders. This approximation turns out to be true only when skiers don't incline very far into their turns. Now that skis allow us to make turns with high inclination angles, this rule of thumb is no longer valid.

> 数十年来，教练们认为，滑雪板尖端之间的连线应与骨盆关节之间的连线以及肩膀之间的连线保持平行。然而，这种近似方法仅在滑雪者在转弯时没有大幅度倾斜的情况下成立。如今，由于滑雪板使我们能够进行高倾斜角度的转弯，这一经验法则已不再适用。

#### 1.2.5 *Hip Height*t  髋高

A lot has been said by coaches and instructors about keeping the hips up. This is a useful teaching method that addresses the important technical issue

> 教练和教员们经常谈到保持髋部高位的重要性。这是一种有效的教学方法，针对重要的技术要点。

of how high or low the skier's neutral stance is. Too high, and you'll be thrown around by changes in terrain, and if your skis suddenly slow down or speed up, you won't be able to react effectively. Too low, and you limit your range of motion to absorb bumps and place greater demands on your muscles.

> 滑雪者中立姿势的高低程度。太高的话，地形变化会让你被甩来甩去，如果滑雪板突然减速或加速，你将无法有效应对。太低的话，会限制你的活动范围以缓冲颠簸，同时对肌肉提出更高要求。

There's one particular danger in the hips themselves getting too low, and this has to do with exposing the knee's anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) to possible injury. When the hips are higher than the knees, the hamstring muscles can, by contracting, provide support to each knee similar to that of the ACL. When the hips get lower than the knees, however, the hamstrings can't provide that support, and the ACL is on its own. That's why the classic ACL injury occurs not just when the skier is leaning back but when the skier's hips are low. It makes sense, then, to adopt a vertical neutral point that keeps the hips from getting below the knees, especially when skiing through compressions.

> 髋部位置过低本身就是一个潜在的危险，这会导致膝关节前十字韧带（ACL）暴露在可能的伤害中。当髋部高于膝盖时，腘绳肌通过收缩可以为每个膝盖提供类似于前十字韧带的支撑。然而，当髋部低于膝盖时，腘绳肌无法提供这种支撑，前十字韧带将独自承担压力。这就是为什么经典的前十字韧带损伤不仅发生在滑雪者后仰时，更发生在髋部位置过低时。因此，在滑雪过程中，尤其是在面对压缩地形时，采用一个垂直中立点以防止髋部低于膝盖是有道理的。

#### 1.2.6 *Lower-Back Posture* 下背部姿势

Much of your ability to move smoothly through the turn and terrain comes from the compliance and strength of your midbody, often called the core. Your hips and lower back must be able to flex and twist easily while stabilizing everything above them. Otherwise, your legs and upper body can't act independently, your legs can't manipulate the skis with as much facility, and the steadiness of your upper body as the counterweight that applies force to the proper spot on the skis is jeopardized.

> 你滑行时在弯道和地形中流畅移动的能力，很大程度上取决于中段（通常称为核心）的柔韧性和力量。你的髋部和下背部必须能够轻松地弯曲和扭转，同时稳定住其上方的所有部位。否则，你的双腿和上半身将无法独立运作，双腿将难以有效地操控滑雪板，而上半身作为施加力量到滑雪板正确位置的配重作用也将受到威胁。

Good midbody posture has the lower back slightly rounded and the pelvis titled neither forward nor back, as shown in figure 4.10. This is accomplished with balanced contractions of the hip flexors (primarily the iliopsoas muscles), hip extensors (gluteus maximus), abdominals, and lower-back muscles. Any feeling of unbalanced static tension in any of these is a sign that something's not right.

> 良好的躯干中段姿势表现为下背部略微弯曲，骨盆既不前倾也不后倾，如图4.10所示。这种姿势是通过髋屈肌（主要为髂腰肌）、髋伸肌（臀大肌）、腹肌和下背肌的平衡收缩来实现的。如果在这些肌肉中感受到任何不平衡的静态张力，就表明存在某些问题。

Two types of unbalanced static contractions in this area are common. The first is tightening the hip flexors and muscles of the lower back, making it arch and tilting the hips forward, as shown in figure 4.11. The second, and opposite, problem is a static tightening of the abdominals and buttocks (gluteus maximus), tucking the butt in and tilting the hips backward, as shown in figure 4.12. Both of these contractions lock up the midbody. There's a good chance you won't feel either of these problems if you have them, so the best way to tell if you do is by watching video of yourself shot from the side. The solution to both problems is to relax the muscles that are being statically contracted and to actively roll the hips slightly in the opposite direction to put the pelvis in its optimal neutral posture.

> 在这一区域常见的两种不平衡静态收缩类型分别是：第一种是紧绷髋屈肌和下背部肌肉，导致脊柱前凸并使髋部前倾，如图4.11所示。第二种是相反的问题，即腹部和臀部（臀大肌）的静态紧绷，使臀部内收并使髋部后倾，如图4.12所示。这两种收缩都会使躯干中部僵硬。如果你有这些状况，很可能不会察觉到，因此最好的判断方法是观看从侧面拍摄的自己的视频。解决这两种问题的方法是放松被静态收缩的肌肉，并主动向相反方向轻微转动髋部，以将骨盆置于最佳中立位。

#### 1.2.7 *Steady Head* 头部稳定

All great skiers keep their heads quiet and steady while they ski. Periodically tipping the head forward in the sagittal plane to look at the skis is a bad habit that many skiers have but few are aware of. Some might do it to check on the snow directly in front of them, some to see if their skis are close together, and some because they're afraid their skis will cross. Whatever the cause, it's a habit that's hard, but very important, to break. And while it isn't an issue of alignment in the same sense as the other topics in this chapter, it's a common issue that gets in the way of good skiing.

> 所有优秀的滑雪者在滑行时都会保持头部安静且稳定。周期性地将头部前倾至矢状面以观察滑雪板是一种许多滑雪者都有的不良习惯，但很少有人意识到这一点。有些人这样做是为了查看前方的雪况，有些人是为了确认滑雪板是否靠拢，还有些人则是因为害怕滑雪板会交叉。无论出于何种原因，这都是一种难以改变但非常重要的习惯。虽然这种习惯与本章其他主题中的对齐问题在本质上有所不同，但它是一种常见的问题，会妨碍良好的滑雪表现。

There are two problems with tipping the head forward. The first is that looking directly in front of you while you ski is like looking at the hood of your car while you're driving. Your attention should be focused farther in front. The second is that moving your head disturbs your sense of balance. Your head contains two of the most important components contributing to your sense of balance and orientation in space: the eyes and the inner ears. When you ski, your brain is constantly integrating the information from your eyes, inner ears, and other senses to determine your body's orientation in space and in relation to the balance axis. Moving your head around jumbles the images from your eyes as well as the information from your inner ears, making your brain's job that much harder and its assessments less accurate.

> 低头前倾会有两个问题。第一个问题是，当你滑雪时直视前方，就像开车时盯着引擎盖看一样。你的注意力应该更专注于更前方。第二个问题是，移动头部会干扰你的平衡感。头部包含两个对平衡感和空间定位能力至关重要的组成部分：眼睛和内耳。当你滑雪时，大脑会持续整合来自眼睛、内耳以及其他感官的信息，以确定身体在空间中的方位以及相对于平衡轴的位置。移动头部会打乱眼睛所接收的影像以及内耳传来的信息，使大脑的工作更加困难，评估也变得不那么准确。

The best way to find out if you tip your head while skiing is, again, to watch video of yourself. If you can't break the habit by consciously making yourself look ahead at all times, I know a sure-fire, though somewhat embarrassing, cure: Find an old pair of goggles and cover the lower half of the lens with duct tape. Ski with these goggles and you'll know instantly when you tip your head. Do this for a few runs a day for a week, and you'll kick the habit.

> 要确定自己在滑雪时是否习惯性抬头，最好的方法还是观看自己的视频。如果你无法通过有意识地让自己始终向前看来改掉这个习惯，我知道一个绝对有效（尽管有些尴尬）的方法：找一副旧护目镜，用电工胶带将镜片下半部分完全覆盖。戴上这些护目镜滑雪时，你立刻就能察觉自己是否抬头。每天这样坚持滑几趟，持续一周，你就能彻底改掉这个习惯。

#### 1.2.11 *Torso, Hip, Knee, and Ankle Alignment* 躯干、髋部、膝盖和脚踝的对齐

Good skiing, especially for carved turns, depends on good lateral alignment of the ankle, knee, and hip joint of each leg. The platform angle is part of the equation, too, but that aspect of alignment is a matter of boot setup and is addressed in chapter 10.

> 良好的滑雪表现，尤其是切线转弯，依赖于每条腿的脚踝、膝盖和髋关节的良好横向对齐。平台角度也是影响因素之一，但这一对齐方面的因素属于靴子设置问题，将在第10章中讨论。

The commonly accepted neutral knee alignment for recreational skiers is defined by straight running. When you're going straight down the fall line on a gentle to moderate slope with your feet a comfortable, natural distance apart, your skis should run flat on the snow and your knees should be directly over your feet. This is a good starting point, but it begs a few questions: What is comfortable and natural? Does this work for people who are naturally bowlegged, knock-kneed, or pigeon toed? Most important, we spend most of our time turning, and that's when edge control is most critical. So should turning, rather than straight running, dictate proper lateral knee alignment?

> 休闲滑雪者普遍接受的中立膝位是以直线滑行定义的。当你在缓坡至中坡上，双脚以自然舒适的间距站立，沿落差线直线下滑时，滑雪板应平躺在雪面上，膝盖应正对双脚。这是一个良好的起点，但引发了一些问题：什么是舒适和自然的间距？对于天生O型腿、X型腿或内八字的滑雪者，这种姿势是否适用？更重要的是，我们大部分时间都在转弯，而这时边刃控制最为关键。因此，是否应该以转弯而不是直线滑行来决定正确的横向膝位？

The last question is key. When you're turning, you are balancing against a large force on an edged outside ski and against a considerably smaller force on the inside ski. Because of your inclination, your outside and inside legs are flexed differently, and your pelvis and torso are tipped outward in relation to the balance axis, as described later. This is a much different posture from what you use in a straight run.

> 最后一个问题至关重要。当你转弯时，你是在外侧滑雪板的边刃上承受较大的力量，而在内侧滑雪板上承受相对较小的力量之间保持平衡。由于你的身体倾斜，外侧和内侧腿的弯曲程度不同，骨盆和上半身相对于平衡轴向外倾斜，如后文所述。这是一种与直线滑行时使用的姿势截然不同的姿势。

Because the large majority of the load in carved turns is on the outside ski, that's the leg we'll focus on. In such a turn, a line drawn from the center of gravity to

This is shown in figure 4.20. The ankle also must be reasonably close to the line, a subject treated in more detail in chapter 8. If the boots are canted correctly, the ski's platform angle will also be right about 90 degrees. Most misalignments of the knee here are caused by improperly canted boots (covered in chapter 10).

> 在矢状面观察时，外侧滑雪板的内边缘应通过外侧大腿骨的头部。同时，外侧膝盖也应位于这条线上，或者稍微偏内一点。如图4.20所示。脚踝也应合理靠近这条线，相关内容将在第8章中更详细地讨论。如果滑雪靴的倾斜角度正确，滑雪板的平台角度也会大约为90度。此处大多数膝关节错位问题都是由于滑雪靴倾斜不当造成的（详见第10章）。

Proper alignment keeps the knee in a strong place while the leg flexes and extends, and balances the mass of the upper body on the head of the femur. From this alignment, the skier can turn the leg inward to increase the ski's edge and platform angles to tighten the turn or to rotate the leg outward to make the ski slip.

> 正确的姿势使膝关节在腿部屈伸时保持稳定位置，并将上半身的重心平衡在股骨头之上。基于这种姿势，滑雪者可通过内转腿部增大滑雪板的倾斜角和平台角度以收紧转弯，或通过外转腿部使滑雪板打滑。

#### 1.2.12 *Pelvis and Torso Tilt* 骨盆与躯干倾斜

#### 1.2.13 exercise 滑雪训练

When turning, a good skier has more weight on the outside ski than the inside ski sometimes all of it—virtually all of the time. For this to happen, the skier's balance axis must be closer to the outside foot than the inside foot. To put this in real-world terms, stand in front of a full-length mirror, balance on your right foot, and watch yourself as you touch the outside of your right knee with your right hand (see figure 8.6*a* on p. 127). What you've done is moved your balance axis closer to that foot. To accomplish this, you tilted your pelvis and torso in the frontal plane until your center of gravity was balanced over the head of your femur. If you look carefully in the mirror, you'll see that your shoulders are tilted more than your hips. The tilt of your hips is a rotation in the frontal plane of the pelvis itself on the head of the femur, and the tilt of your shoulders is the result of small rotations in each of the vertebrae of your spine.

> 在转弯时，优秀的滑雪者会将更多的体重放在外侧雪板上，而不是内侧雪板，有时甚至全部重量都集中在外侧雪板上——几乎总是如此。要实现这一点，滑雪者的重心轴必须更靠近外侧脚而非内侧脚。为了更直观地理解这一点，站在一面全身镜前，单脚站立在右脚上，用右手触碰右膝外侧（参见第127页图8.6a）。你所做的就是将重心轴更靠近那只脚。为了做到这一点，你将骨盆和躯干向冠状面倾斜，直到你的重心恰好位于股骨头上。如果你仔细观察镜中的自己，会发现肩部的倾斜程度大于髋部。髋部的倾斜是骨盆本身在股骨头上围绕冠状面的旋转，而肩部的倾斜则是脊椎各椎体的小幅度旋转所导致的。

Good skiers get most or all of their weight on the outside ski through a combination of what you just did in front of the mirror and countering, which is discussed later in this chapter (see Alignment in the Transverse Plane, p. 73). The best skiers in the world vary in how much they tilt in the frontal plane and how much they counter, but they all do some of each (figures 4.21 and 8.7, p. 128).

> 优秀的滑雪者通过你在镜子前所做的动作以及对抗（counter）的结合，将大部分或全部体重转移到外侧滑雪板上（详见本章后面讨论的内容，参见“横断面内的对齐”部分，第73页）。这些动作共同构成了骨盆倾斜（hip angulation），该内容在第8章中将进一步详细阐述。世界顶级滑雪者在冠状面内的倾斜程度和对抗动作的运用上各有不同，但他们都会进行一定程度的倾斜和对抗（参见图4.21和图8.7，第128页）。

Coaches and instructors have talked for decades about the importance of the hips and shoulders being "level," referring to the alignment of an imaginary line

> 教练和指导员们已经讨论了数十年，强调髋部和肩部保持“平齐”的重要性，指的是想象中的一条线的对齐。

passing through the heads of the femurs and another through the shoulders. When athletes were inclining 30 or 40 degrees into a turn, those lines were approximately level with respect to the snow. That is, the lines were approximately perpendicular to gravity. With current angles of inclination we can see that the hips and shoulders of the world's best skiers are seldom truly level. The important aspect of hip and shoulder alignment in the frontal plane is not how they line up in relation to the snow or to gravity, but how they line up relative to the balance axis: They need to be tipped toward the outside relative to that line. But emphasizing the importance of the hips and shoulders being "level" was, and still is, a good coaching method. What feels level to a skier generally turns out to be a good alignment, whether it is literally level or not.

> 通过股骨头部和另一条通过肩部。当运动员在转弯时倾斜30度或40度时，这些线大致与雪面水平，即这些线大致与重力垂直。在当前的倾斜角度下，我们发现世界顶级滑雪运动员的髋部和肩部很少真正水平。在冠状面中，髋部和肩部对齐的重要方面不在于它们相对于雪面或重力的对齐方式，而在于它们相对于平衡轴的对齐方式：它们需要相对于这条线向转弯外侧倾斜。但强调髋部和肩部“水平”这一点，过去是、现在仍然是一个良好的教学方法。滑雪者感觉“水平”的姿势通常是一种良好的对齐方式，无论它是否字面意义上的水平。

Attaining good hip alignment starts with an awareness of where the inside hip is during the turn's initiation phase. You develop that awareness by making lots and lots of turns while focusing on just that, starting in simplified environments—smooth snow, moderate pitch, and no distractions—and progressing to more complex situations. If your inside hip drops going into the turn, a common problem, your center of gravity will almost certainly fall toward the inside of the turn, and you won't enter the control phase with the right amount of hip angulation. As a result, you'll skid and slip. A good exercise to help you keep your hips aligned properly is shown in figure 4.22. At the very start of the turn, put your outside hand on your hip, and reach up and forward with your inside hand. Keep reaching up and forward with the inside hand all the way through a mediumsized round, clean turn, then switch hands as you go into the next.

> 实现良好的髋部对齐始于在转弯开始阶段对内侧髋部位置的意识。通过在平滑雪面、中等坡度和无干扰环境中进行大量转弯练习，并专注于这一点，逐步过渡到更复杂的场景，可以培养这种意识。如果在进入转弯时内侧髋部下沉（这是常见问题），你的重心将几乎必然向转弯内侧倾斜，从而无法在控制阶段实现正确的髋部倾斜角度。结果会导致你打滑和滑出。如图4.22所示的一种练习方法有助于你正确保持髋部对齐。在转弯的开始阶段，将外侧手放在髋部，同时将内侧手向上向前伸展。在整个中等大小的干净转弯过程中持续保持内侧手向上向前的姿势，然后进入下一个转弯时切换手的位置。

Tilting the shoulders into the turn, or simply not tilting the torso sufficiently outward relative to the balance axis, is one of the most common problems in skiing, and it's easy to spot. Developing good shoulder alignment starts with the hands, and exercises that emphasize keeping the hands "level," in the sense that a line between them is tilted outward with respect to the balance axis, usually produce good results. Another is skiing with the poles held crosswise in front of you, as shown in figure 4.23.

> 将肩部向转弯方向倾斜，或者更简单地说，相对于平衡轴，肩部没有充分地向外倾斜，是滑雪中最常见的问题之一，而且很容易察觉。良好的肩部对齐始于手部，强调保持手部“水平”的练习（即手部之间的连线相对于平衡轴向外倾斜），通常会产生良好的效果。如第9章所述（见图9.8，第143页），将一根绳子绕在手上是一种很好的练习方法。另一种练习方法是将滑雪杖横向握在身前进行滑雪，如图4.23所示。

### 1.3 Alignment in the Transverse Plane 横断平面的对齐

We don't often view skiers in the transverse plane—from directly above (figure 4.26)—but one of the most subtle and important facets of body alignment appears from this vantage: counter. Too much or too little of this stance element can compromise all the good movements you make. Just how you should arrange your body in this plane depends on many aspects of your particular body type, so alignment in this regard varies widely among the best of skiers.

> 我们很少从正上方（图4.26）的横断平面观察滑雪者，但从这个视角来看，身体姿势中最为微妙且重要的一个方面就是反向。过多或过少的这种姿势要素都会影响你所做出的所有良好动作。你在这个平面上应该如何安排身体位置，取决于你特定体型的许多方面，因此顶尖滑雪者在这一方面的对齐方式差异很大。


> 从横断面观察，一名优秀的滑雪者在转弯时，其骨盆和躯干通常不会完全朝向雪板指向的方向。相反，它们会围绕平衡轴向转弯的外侧旋转（图4.27）。这种姿态被称为反向，其运动过程是……

Viewed in the transverse plane, a good skier's pelvis and torso won't generally face exactly in the direction the skis are pointing when the skier is in a turn. Instead they'll be rotated about the balance axis toward the outside of the turn (figure 4.27). This alignment is called *counter,* and the movement of turning the hips and torso in that direction is called *countering.* (Don't confuse these terms with *counterrotation,* described in chapter 7. They're all standard terms in ski teaching and coaching, and unfortunately sound very similar. Counter and countering are very close in meaning, but counterrotation refers to a much different concept.) Counter helps the skier move the balance axis toward the outside ski (as described on page 70) without tilting the pelvis and torso excessively in the frontal plane. By first countering, the skier can move the balance axis toward the outside ski by bending forward at the waist in the sagittal plane, instead of just tilting the hips and torso sideways in the frontal plane. Both the pelvis and spine have limited flexibility in that direction, and stiffen up quickly when bent very far that way. Countering also shifts much of the load of supporting the upper body, which can be considerable, from weak muscles to stronger ones.

> 将骨盆和躯干向那个方向转动称为“反向转动”(countering)。(不要将这些术语与第七章中描述的“反向旋转”(counterrotation)混淆。这些术语都是滑雪教学和指导中的标准术语，不幸的是它们听起来非常相似。“反向”(counter)和“反向转动”(countering)意义非常接近，但“反向旋转”指的是完全不同的概念。) “反向转动”有助于滑雪者在不使骨盆和躯干在冠状面内过度向侧倾斜的情况下，将重心轴向外侧滑雪板方向移动(如第70页所述)。通过首先进行“反向转动”，滑雪者可以在矢状面内通过屈腰前倾来将重心轴向外侧滑雪板方向移动，而不是仅仅在冠状面内使骨盆和躯干向侧倾斜。骨盆和脊柱在该方向上的灵活性有限，当向该方向弯折得非常厉害时，它们会迅速变僵硬。此外，“反向转动”还能将支撑上半身的大部分负荷从较弱的肌肉转移到较强的肌肉上，而上半身的支撑负荷可能相当大。


从横断面来看，当滑雪者处于转弯中时，一名技术良好的滑雪者的骨盆和躯干通常不会完全面向双板所指的方向。相反，它们会围绕平衡轴向转弯外侧略微旋转（图 4.27）。这种对齐方式称为 **“反向”（counter）**，而将髋部和躯干向该方向转动的动作称为 **“反向动作”（countering）**。

（不要将这些术语与第 7 章所描述的 **“反向旋转”（counterrotation）** 混淆。它们都是滑雪教学与训练中的标准术语，但非常不幸的是，它们的发音非常相似。“counter” 和 “countering” 意义非常接近，但“counterrotation” 指的是完全不同的概念。）

**反向（counter）** 的作用是：帮助滑雪者将平衡轴移向外侧滑雪板（如第 70 页所述），而无需在额状面中过度倾斜骨盆和躯干。  
通过先做反向动作，滑雪者可以通过在矢状面上从腰部前屈，将平衡轴移向外侧雪板，而不只是简单地在额状面上将髋部和躯干向侧面倾斜。

无论是骨盆还是脊柱，在那个方向上的灵活性都有限，若过度弯曲会很快变得僵硬。  
**反向动作（countering）** 还有一个好处——它能将支撑上半身（重量可能相当大）的大部分负荷，从较弱的肌肉转移到较强的肌肉上。

> 图4.26 从横断面观察的滑雪者。滑雪者：杰里·伯格。

![](_page_84_Picture_8.jpeg)

Figure 4.27 Hermann Maier counters by turning his hips and shoulders slightly toward the outside of the turn.
> 图4.27 赫尔曼·马伊尔通过将臀部和肩膀略微向转弯的外侧转动来进行调整。

The strongest muscles can support the greatest loads with the most finesse. Ask a muscle to work against a load that taxes its limits, and it can't work with precision. The biggest, strongest muscles in your body are those of the thigh and buttocks. So when you're balancing against big forces in a turn, these are the muscles you want to have supporting and balancing your upper body. The closer the balance axis gets to the outside foot, the more the hips and torso need to counter to line up the load with those muscles of the outside leg. Most countering takes place in the ball-and-socket joint where the femur meets the pelvis. A smaller amount also occurs throughout the spinal column, so that the shoulders are countered slightly more than the hips. Figure 4.28 shows a good combination of lateral tilt and countering in a turn with a lot of force. In some turns, especially those of medium radius and larger, you should establish counter by the end of the transition so your body is well aligned to balance against the force that you'll encounter in the control phase (see figures 8.9, p. 130, and 9.9, p. 144). In other turns, such as short radius turns on steep slopes, the big force doesn't come until after the fall line (see figures 8.8, p. 129, and 14.4, p. 203), and your upper body won't turn toward the outside so much as your legs will turn under your body to put you in a countered stance. Look at the inset frames in figures 8.8 and 14.4 and imagine how contorted the skiers would be if they were still balanced over the downhill ski but their upper bodies weren't countered—that is, if their torsos were facing the camera. Figure 4.29 shows a skier doing just that.

> 最强壮的肌肉可以以最细腻的控制支持最大的负荷。如果要求肌肉对抗超出其极限的负荷，那么它就无法进行精准的控制。你身体中最强大、最有力的肌肉是大腿和臀部肌肉。因此，当你在转弯中对抗较大的力量时，这些肌肉就是支撑和平衡上半身的关键。平衡轴越靠近外侧脚，髋部和躯干就越需要进行反向动作，以使负荷与外侧腿的肌肉对齐。大部分的反向动作发生在股骨与骨盆连接的球窝关节处。也有一部分反向动作发生在脊柱的整个长度上，因此肩部的反向动作比髋部稍多一些。图4.28展示了一个在强大力量转弯中良好的侧倾和反向动作的组合。在某些转弯中，特别是中等半径和较大半径的转弯，你应该在过渡结束时建立反向动作，使身体良好对齐，以平衡控制阶段中将遇到的力（参见图8.9，第130页，以及图9.9，第144页）。在其他类型的转弯中，例如在陡坡上的短半径转弯，较大的力量通常出现在下滑线之后（参见图8.8，第129页，以及图14.4，第203页），此时你的上半身不会向外侧倾斜，而是腿部在身体下方转动，以使你进入一个对抗姿势。请查看图8.8和图14.4中的插图框，并想象如果滑雪者仍然保持在下滑滑雪板上平衡，但他们的上半身没有进行反向动作——也就是说，他们的躯干正对着相机——他们将会有多扭曲。图4.29展示了一位滑雪者正做这样的动作。

Because proper counter brings into play the best muscles for balancing against the force of the turn, you get the feeling of being supported by your bones, especially in medium to large radius turns. A less-than-optimal alignment, which enlists weaker muscles to the task, makes you feel like your muscles are doing more work.

> 因为正确的反向姿势会调动最佳肌肉群来对抗转弯的力，你会感受到骨骼的支撑，尤其是在中到大半径转弯时。而非最佳姿态会调动较弱的肌肉来完成任务，让你感觉肌肉需要做更多工作。

#### 1.3.1 Gender Differences in Countering and Hip Angulation

> 性别差异在反向与髋部角度中的表现

The distribution of mass in women's bodies is, in general, different from that of men. Women carry proportionately more mass in and around the hips and less in their upper torsos. How far into a turn a skier should put the hips depends on the location of the outside hip joint and the location of the ankle with respect to the balance axis. How far to the outside a skier must put the shoulders, on the other hand, is dictated by how much weight that aforementioned hip movement puts to the inside of the turn. In other words, the upper body is put to the outside of the turn to counterbalance the movement of the hip to the inside. And because most women's hips have relatively more mass than men's, and their shoulders less, women must usually counter and angulate at the hips more than men to stay in balance while getting the same edge control.

> 女性身体的质量分布总体上与男性不同。女性在骨盆及周围部位的质量比例更高，而上半身的质量则相对较少。滑雪者在转弯中将臀部向内移动多远，取决于外侧髋关节的位置以及脚踝相对于平衡轴的位置。另一方面，滑雪者必须将肩膀向转弯外侧移动多远，则取决于前述髋部动作将重量转移到转弯内侧的程度。换句话说，上半身被向转弯外侧倾斜，是为了抵消髋部向内移动的运动。由于大多数女性的臀部相比男性拥有相对更多的质量，而肩膀则较少，因此女性通常要比男性在臀部处做出更多的倾斜和调整，才能在保持平衡的同时获得相同的刃控制效果。

> Another good exercise for developing hip angulation and countering is the javelin turn, demonstrated in figure 4.31. The idea is simple: Make a turn and, when you get to the fall line, pick your inside ski up and point its tip toward the outside of the turn. As the turn progresses, point the ski more and more toward the outside. The more you can cross it over the outside ski, the better. This turns your hip to a countered position and puts it to the inside of the turn. Really good skiers can make javelin turns of just about any size on just about any terrain. This exercise dates back to the mid-1960s, when Art Furrer, a Swiss who was probably the first well-known trick skier, invented the maneuver for his repertoire. He named the move after the model of ski that his supplier, Hart, was promoting at the time.

> 另一种有助于发展骨盆角度和对抗姿势的练习是标枪式转弯，如图4.31所示。其原理很简单：进行一个转弯，当到达下滑线时，将内侧雪板向上抬起，并让其尖端指向转弯的外侧。随着转弯的进行，逐渐将雪板尖端指向更外侧。你越能将雪板跨过外侧雪板，效果越好。这样会将骨盆转向对抗姿势，并将骨盆置于转弯的内侧。真正优秀的滑雪者几乎可以在任何地形上完成各种大小的标枪式转弯。这项练习可以追溯到20世纪60年代中期，当时瑞士的阿尔特·弗勒尔（Art Furrer）可能是最早知名的技巧滑雪者之一，他为自己的滑雪技巧集发明了这一动作。他以当时供应商哈特（Hart）所推广的滑雪板型号命名了这一动作。
