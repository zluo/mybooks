## 1 <span id="page-98-0"></span>Up and Downt 上下

Moving up and down in concert with the snow and the dynamics of the turn is a mark of a fine skier. That doesn't necessarily mean moving your center of gravity up and down, but rather extending and flexing your entire body in that direction. In fact, a good skier makes many of these movements to moderate the effects of terrain and the forces of the turn, preventing those effects and forces from causing the skier's center of gravity to move up and down. Extending the range of motion and precision of your up-and-down movements is one of the simplest and biggest steps you can take toward becoming a better skier yourself. The more range you have, the wider variety of terrain and skiing situations you'll be able to deal with. And the more precision you have in these movements, the better you'll be able to flow through those situations without your balance being disturbed. You'll be able to control the force and pressure exerted by the snow on you and your skis so they will produce the exact effects you want—running smoothly through bumps and dips, setting your edges firmly and cleanly, and jumping gaps between moguls to make airplane turns—while you stay centered over your skis.

> 随着雪和转弯动力的变化上下移动，是优秀滑雪者的标志。这并不一定意味着改变你的重心上下移动，而是整个身体在那个方向上的伸展和弯曲。事实上，好的滑雪者会做出许多这样的动作来调节地形和转弯的力量影响，防止这些影响和力量导致滑雪者重心的上下移动。增加上下动作的范围和精确度是你向成为更好的滑雪者迈出的最简单也是最大的一步。你拥有越多的范围，就能应对更多样化的地形和滑雪情况。而你在这些动作中的精确度越高，你就越能在那些情况下流畅地通过，而不被平衡打扰。你可以控制雪对你和滑板施加的力量和压力，以便产生你想要的确切效果——在颠簸和平坦之间平稳穿行，坚定干净地设定边缘，并从莫格之间的缝隙跳跃来进行飞机转弯，同时保持在你的滑雪板上居中。

### 1.1 What Do Up, Down, and Vertical Mean? 上、下和垂直是什么意思？

The words up, down, and vertical are so basic to our vocabularies that we assume they mean the same things to all people. But what do *up*, *down*, and *vertical* really mean in your frame of reference as a skier, as discussed in chapter 1? Assuming you're in balance, *up* is the direction your supporting surface pushes on you. *Down*, obviously, is the other direction: the direction in which you push on that surface. And *vertical* is the line that up and down share. In terms of the skier's frame of reference, vertical is the balance axis.

> “上”、“下”和“垂直”的词汇如此基础，以至于我们假定所有人都理解这些词的意思是一样的。但是，在滑雪者的第一章中讨论的上下左右相对于你自身来说又意味着什么呢？假设你是平衡的，“上”是支撑面推你的方向。“下”显然是另一个相反的方向：也就是你在支撑面上推动的那个方向。垂直就是“上”和“下”共享的一条线。就滑雪者的参考系而言，垂直是其身体保持平衡的轴。

When you're standing still at the side of a ski trail, up, down, and vertical are defined only by gravity: For you, up is the direction the trees point. When you're skiing, however, you sense a different up and down, set within your own personal frame of reference. As you ski through terrain variations and turns, more forces come into play, and the balance axis isn't usually parallel with the trees, as shown in figure 6.1. Whenever the terms up, down, and vertical appear in this book, we'll be using them from the skier's frame of reference. It's the best thing you have to go by when you're skiing, and it defines the category of fundamental movements we address in this chapter.

> 当你站在滑雪道的一侧时，上下和垂直都是由重力定义的：对你来说，向上是树木所指的方向。然而，在滑雪的时候，你会感觉到一个不同的上和下，这基于你自己的个人参考框架。随着你在地形变化和转弯中滑行，更多的力量开始发挥作用，并且平衡轴通常不会平行于树木（如图6.1所示）。本书中出现的“上”、“下”和“垂直”等术语都将从滑雪者的视角出发进行解释。这是当你滑雪时最好的参照物，它定义了本章将要讨论的基本运动类别。

#### 1.1.1 Why Move Up and Down? 为什么上下移动？

Skiers move up and down to control the magnitude of the force exerted on them by the snow. The total amount of force acting between you and the snow determines how much of an effect the snow has on your motion. If you have just passed over a bump and your skis are light, you won't be able to turn or slow down because the snow can't push on you. You'll be able to pivot your skis easily, on the other hand, for the same reason.

> 滑雪者上下移动以控制他们对雪的施力大小。作用在你和雪之间的总力量决定了雪对你运动的影响程度。如果你刚刚越过了一个凸起，而你的滑雪板很轻，你就无法转弯或减速，因为雪无法推你。另一方面，出于相同的原因，你可以轻松地将滑雪板转换方向。

At any point in a turn, traverse, or straight run, you want the snow to exert a particular amount of force on you. Sometimes you want a lot, and sometimes you want a little, depending on the particular place you are at in the turn you're making. If you don't move up and down at all, the contour of the snow and the virtual bump, described in chapter 3, will combine to exert changing forces on you throughout the turn that often won't be optimal for what you want to do. By extending or flexing, you augment or reduce these forces to produce the amount of overall force you want.

> 在任何转弯、穿越或直行的时刻，你希望雪对你产生特定的力量。有时候需要大力度，有时候则只需要轻微的作用力，这取决于你在正在进行的那个转弯的具体位置。如果你完全不上下移动，那么雪面轮廓和第三章中描述的虚拟颠簸相结合，在整个转弯过程中对你的作用力往往不会达到最优状态。通过伸展或弯曲身体来增强或减少这些力量的影响，以产生你想要的整体作用力。

When going straight down the hill, you're usually interested in keeping the total force from the snow constant. If there are bumps in your path, you flex and extend to negate their effects, keeping your center of gravity moving in something close to a straight line when viewed in the sagittal plane, as Donna Weinbrecht does in figure 6.2. When making short turns through big moguls, you usually flex at the end of the turn to reduce the upward force the mogul would otherwise exert on you. Once having crossed the crest of the bump, you extend to maintain some ski–snow pressure. The larger the bumps, the more amplitude and precision you need. In both situations, you move up and down in synch with the snow to keep your center of gravity from being displaced up and down.

> 当你直接下山时，你通常对保持雪的总力量感兴趣。如果路径中有颠簸，你会弯曲和伸展以抵消它们的影响，在视图中在矢状面附近保持你的重心移动为一条直线，就像Donna Weinbrecht在图6.2中所做的那样。当通过大莫尔格进行短转弯时，你通常会在转弯结束时弯曲，以减少莫尔格原本会对你的推力。一旦越过颠簸的顶点，你就伸展以保持一些滑雪板-雪压力。颠簸越大，你需要的振幅和精确度就越多。在两种情况下，你会与雪同步上下移动，以防止重心上下位移。

Beginning skiers make movements that create only small changes in ski–snow pressure. They ski in simplified environments where only small pressure changes are created by the terrain, and they make turns that generate only small forces and no discernable virtual bump. As they progress through intermediate ability stages, skiers keep the environment simple but learn to deliberately change pressure from ski to ski, modulate the overall pressure on their skis, and make turns that generate greater forces.

> 初学者滑雪者所做的动作只引起了微小的滑雪板-雪压变化。他们在简化环境中滑雪，地形只会产生微小的压力变化，并且他们会做转弯来产生很小的力量和看不出的虚拟颠簸。随着他们通过中级能力阶段，滑雪者保持环境简单但学会了故意在滑雪板之间改变压力，调节其滑雪板的整体压力，并做出能产生更大力量的转弯。

By the time skiers have reached an advanced level, they have learned to make many movements to deal with anticipated and potentially disruptive pressure changes caused by terrain and the dynamics of the turn. It's fair to say that good skiers make more movements to prevent or reduce changes in the force from the snow than they make to create them.

> 当滑雪者达到高级水平时，他们已经学会了许多动作来应对由地形和转弯动态引发的预知或潜在破坏性压力变化。可以说，优秀的滑雪者为了防止或减少雪地力量的变化所进行的动作比创造这些变化要多。

#### 1.1.2 Accounting for the Virtual Bump 虚拟凸度的处理

Intermediates first feel the virtual bump, described in chapter 3, when they start to complete turns with significant pressure and link them together. They quickly learn to like the lift and bounce they get when they merge the completion of one turn with the initiation of the next in a seamless transition. At this point, they learn to time their own up and down movements to coincide with the ups and downs of the virtual bump. Their up movements and the rise of the virtual bump complement each other, making it easier to initiate the turn.

> 中介首先在第三章中描述的虚拟颠簸开始感觉到，当他们开始用显著的压力完成转弯并将其连接在一起时。他们很快学会了喜欢在平滑过渡中将一个转弯的完成与下一个转弯的启动相结合时所获得的提升和弹跳。到这个阶段，他们会学习调整自己的上下运动以与虚拟颠簸的起落相吻合。他们的上升运动与虚拟颠簸的上升相补充，使得开始转弯变得更加容易。

This synergy works well up to a point. But once you start linking tightly carved turns, you have to be prepared for virtual bumps that might launch you off the snow if you extend at the end of the turn. To maintain contact with the snow in these circumstances, you have to time your flexions and extensions as if your were skiing through bumps: You flex in the completion phase of one turn and don't extend until you start to turn down into the fall line in the next (figures 6.3 and 6.4).

> 这种协同效应工作得很好，直到一定点。但是当你开始连接紧密雕刻的转弯时，你必须准备好应对虚拟的颠簸，如果你在转弯末尾伸展的话，这可能会把你从雪上弹开。在这种情况下保持与雪地接触，你需要像滑雪穿过波浪一样调整你的弯曲和延展：在完成一个转弯的过程中弯曲，并且直到开始向下进入下一个坡面线时才开始延伸（图6.3和图6.4）。

#### 1.1.3 Uncoupling Up and Down From Fore and Aft 解耦上 下 和 前后

We move up and down on skis for different reasons than we move fore and aft. Up and down movements regulate the total amount of force the snow exerts on us, whereas fore and aft movements control the skis' behavior in response to that force and help us maintain balance. Yet most skiers can't move independently in these two directions. They have deep-rooted couplings between their vertical and fore–aft movements. Some move forward with every downward flexion and backward with every upward extension. Every possible combination of entrenched patterns can be seen out on the mountain.

> 我们滑雪时上下移动的原因与前后移动大不相同。向上和向下的运动调节了雪对我们的总作用力，而前后来回的运动则控制着在受到这种力的作用下滑雪板的行为，并帮助我们保持平衡。然而大多数滑雪者在这两个方向上无法独立行动。他们在垂直和前后运动之间存在着固有的耦合关系。一些人在向下弯曲时向前移动，在向上伸展时向后移动。在山地上可以看到所有可能的模式组合。

To ski with real versatility, you must be able to control independently these two interactions with the snow. You need to control overall force through long vertical movements without disturbing its distribution fore and aft. Likewise, you have to move forward or backward while keeping the total force against the snow constant. See figure 6.5.

> 为了实现真正的滑雪技巧，你必须能够独立控制与雪的这两种互动。你需要通过长时间垂直移动来整体控制力量而不干扰其前后分布。同样地，在保持对雪的总力不变的情况下前进或后退。请看图6.5。

Nothing demonstrates this particular ability better than making large turns through moguls, as shown in figure 6.6, because it requires a skier to move up and down several times in a single turn while independently controlling fore–aft and lateral balance. It's as good a test as any to separate the best skiers from the rest.

> 没有什么比在如图6.6所示的 mogul群中做大幅转弯更能体现这种能力了，因为这需要滑雪者在一记滑行中上下移动数次，并独立控制前后和横向平衡。这是将顶尖滑雪者与其他人区分开来的一个很好的测试。

If you can isolate the vertical movement of your center of gravity, you can control the overall force on it and the force's fore–aft distribution independently. Your body is composed of segments that pivot around joints, however, and articulating at any single joint moves your center of gravity in an arc. Consider the piston in figure 6.7. It's driven by links that move in circular arcs around pivot points, like your center of gravity is driven by your skeleton. Your task as a skier is to coordinate those rotary movements so that, like the piston, your center of gravity moves straight up and down.

> 如果你能孤立地控制你的重心的垂直运动，你就能独立地控制作用在它身上的总力以及力沿前后方向的分布。然而，你的身体是由围绕关节旋转的段落组成的，并且，在任何单一关节处活动都会导致重心沿着弧线移动。考虑一下图6.7中的活塞。它是通过连接在枢轴点上并以圆周轨迹移动的连杆驱动的，就像你的重心是被骨骼所推动的一样。作为一个滑雪者，你的任务就是协调这些旋转运动，使得你的重心像活塞一样，垂直地上下移动。

The major joints we have to work with for moving vertically and fore and aft are the ankles, knees, hips, lower back, and shoulders. None of these joints, by itself, moves your center of gravity purely up and down or forward and backward. Rather, each joint moves your center of gravity in an arc. As we saw in chapter 5, the ankles are useful for making fore-aft adjustments, but as figure 6.8 shows, they have little effect on your center of gravity's vertical position. So the knees, hips, lower back, and shoulders must work together like the links driving the piston to move your center of gravity straight up and down (figure 6.9).

> 我们需要工作以垂直和前后移动的主要关节包括踝关节、膝关节、髋关节、下背部和肩膀。这五个关节中的任何一个，单独使用，都不能仅通过上下或前后移动来改变你的重心。相反，每个关节都只是将你的重心在一个弧线上移动。正如我们在第5章中看到的那样，踝关节对于调整前后位置非常有用，但是如图6.8所示，它们对你的重心垂直位置的影响很小。因此，膝盖、髋关节、下背部和肩膀必须像驱动活塞的连杆一样协同工作，以使你的重心上下直线移动（如图6.9所示）。

Here, then, is a good rule of thumb for uncoupling up and down movements of the center of gravity from those that move it fore and aft. Use your knees, hips, and shoulders for up and down, and use your ankles for fore and aft. As with all rules of thumb, it's a bit simplistic, but nonetheless is a good place to start. In the next section we'll look at combining articulations at the knees, hips, and shoulders to produce pure vertical motion.

> 以下是一個很好的標準，用於將重心的上下運動與使其前後運動分離。使用膝蓋、臀部和肩膀進行上下運動，並使用踝关节进行前后运动。就像所有經驗法則一樣，這有些簡單化，但仍然是一個很好的開始點。在下一節中，我們將研究如何結合膝蓋、臀部和肩部的運動以產生純垂直運動。

#### 1.1.4 Learning to Move Straight Up and Down 学习上下直行

Everyone can move straight up and down when they're wearing sneakers. But put them in ski boots, and it's a different story. That's because ski boots, unlike sneakers, are designed to restrict ankle movement. When you're not wearing ski boots, you can move up and down using coordinated movements at the ankles, knees, hips, and lower back that your body has learned from years of experience. When you put on ski boots and step into your skis, you lose the free use of your ankles. Try to bend your ankle, and the boot stops you. Try harder, and it puts leverage on the ski's forebody, shifting pressure forward. Straighten the ankle past a certain point, and the back of the boot shifts pressure toward the tail. Try to flex using the same combination of articulations as you would in street shoes, and you'll wind up on your heels.

>每个人穿着运动鞋时，都能轻松地上下移动。但一旦换上滑雪靴，情况就完全不同了。   这是因为滑雪靴与运动鞋不同，它们的设计目的就是**限制脚踝的活动**。 当你不穿滑雪靴时，你可以通过脚踝、膝盖、髋部和下背部的协同动作来完成上下移动——这些都是你多年自然形成的身体习惯。但当你穿上滑雪靴并卡进滑雪板后，你就失去了对脚踝的自由使用。 尝试弯曲脚踝时，靴子会阻止你；如果继续加力，它会向滑雪板的前部施加杠杆作用，把压力转移到板头。反过来，如果你把脚踝伸直超过一定程度，靴子的后侧又会把压力推向板尾。 如果你仍然试图用穿街鞋时那套由多个关节配合而成的动作去弯曲身体，你最终只会**后仰站在脚跟上**。

To move straight up and down in ski boots to accurately control the overall force from the snow, you must flex and extend with very little ankle movement. You use a different combination of articulations at the knees, hips, lower back, and shoulders than you use when you're wearing sneakers. But finding the right combination can be tricky. Bend too little at the waist, and your center of gravity moves down and back (figure 6.10*a*). Bend too much at the waist for the amount of bend at your knees, and your center of gravity moves down and forward (figure 6.10*c*). Drop your arms straight down while flexing, and your center of gravity moves back. Straighten too fast at the waist for the amount of straightening at the knees, and your center of gravity moves up and back (figure 6.10*d*). And so on.

> 要准确控制从雪中施加的总体力，只需直上直下地在滑雪靴中移动，你需要非常小的踝关节运动就能进行屈伸。当你穿着慢跑鞋时所用的不同组合的动作，你在膝盖、髋部、下背部和肩膀使用的是不同的动作。但是找到正确的组合并不容易。腰部弯曲得不够多，你的重心会向后和向下移动（图6.10a）。如果你的腰部弯曲度超过了膝盖弯曲的程度，那么你的重心也会向后和向前移动（图6.10c）。在弯曲时让双臂垂下，那么你的重心就会向后移动。腰部直起来的速度太快于膝盖伸展的速度，那么你的重心会向上并朝后移动（图6.10d）。等等。

The combination of articulations that work for you are specific to your body, depending on the length and weight of your lower legs, upper legs, hips, torso, and arms. Other important factors are your ski boots, because of their forward lean, and your bindings, which can affect the forward lean of your boots. (Forward lean is discussed in chapter 10.) The expert skier's body has learned the right articulations by making hundreds of thousands of turns. Your body can learn them that way, too, but there's no guarantee it will unless you train it properly.

> 你身上的动作组合对你来说是特定的，这取决于你的小腿、大腿、臀部、躯干和手臂的长度和重量。其他重要的因素包括你的滑雪靴，尤其是它们向前倾斜的角度，以及你的固定装置，这些都可能影响到靴子的前倾角。（前倾角在第10章中有所讨论）专家级滑雪者的身体通过数以十万计的转弯学会了正确的动作组合。你的身体也能学会这些，但如果不进行适当的训练，就无法确保你能够做到这一点。

People watching a world-class mogul skier often remark that the skier's knees and back must take a pounding, because they see a sharp compression of the body as the skier encounters each bump. Viewed from the side, you can see that the skier's feet move up and down, but his center of gravity follows a much straighter line.

> 观看世界级雪上技巧滑雪者的人们常常会评论说，滑雪者的膝盖和背部一定承受着巨大的冲击，因为他们看到滑雪者遇到每一个颠簸时身体会出现明显的压缩。但其实这些滑雪者所受到的打击并没有你想象中的那么严重，因为他们的这些身体动作通常是故意而主动的。滑雪者会在遭遇颠簸前积极地调整身体姿态进入弯腰状态，以减少颠簸对他施加的力量。从侧面观看可以发现，滑雪者的脚在上下移动，但他的重心轨迹则遵循一条相对平直的线。

#### 1.1.8 Unweighting

> 0.1.1.9 脱重力

The terms commonly used to describe increasing and decreasing the overall force between the snow and the skier are *weighting* and *unweighting,* respectively. The words make sense subjectively, but technically they're misnomers. Your weight might change when you eat a big lunch, but it doesn't change when you fly off a bump into the air. What changes is the magnitude of the force the snow exerts on you. You feel light when you lift off a bump because that force goes away. Conversely, you feel heavier when you ski into a dip because the reaction force from the snow increases. These sensations are produced by vertical accelerations and decelerations of your center of gravity relative to the snow. Technical inaccuracies notwithstanding, we'll use the terms weighting and unweighting here because they make sense to skiers.

> 用来描述雪与滑雪者之间总体力增加或减少的常用术语是“加权”和“减载”。这些词汇在主观上说得通，但在技术层面上却是错误的说法。你的体重可能因为吃了大餐而变化，但当你从一个凸起点跳跃起来飞向空中时，并不会因此改变。发生变化的是雪对你施加的力量大小。当你从一个凸起点起飞时会感到轻盈，因为这时那股力量就消失了；相反的，当你滑雪滑入凹坑时会有加重的感觉，因为来自雪面的反作用力增加了。这些感觉是由你的重心相对于雪面在垂直方向上的加速和减速产生的。尽管这样的描述技术上不完全准确，但我们仍在这里使用“加权”和“减载”这两个术语，因为它们更容易让滑雪者理解。

Because the initiation phase of many turns is facilitated by unweighting, most systems of ski instruction once emphasized unweighting skills. As the slopes we ski on have become more manicured and our skis easier to turn, focus has shifted to other skills. This is unfortunate for two reasons. First, to ski well in many situations still requires unweighting. Second, it was in learning to unweight that skiers learned to move up and down properly—a skill all good skiers still need. Skiers are, in general, more static on their skis than they were 20 years ago, and the reduced instructional emphasis on unweighting could be a primary reason.

> 由于减轻重量阶段通常可以促进轮换，因此许多滑雪教学体系曾经强调过减重技巧。随着我们滑雪场地的修剪越来越精细，以及滑雪板转向变得更加容易，重点已经转移到了其他技能上。这种转变有两个不幸的原因：第一，在很多情况下，想要滑好仍然需要减重；第二，在学习减重的过程中，滑雪者学会了上下移动的正确方式——这一技能对所有好的滑雪者来说仍然是必不可少的。总的来说，现在滑雪者的体态在滑雪板上的稳定性较20年前有所下降，而减少对减重要点的教学可能就是主要原因之一。

Unweighting comes in two main types: *up-unweighting* and *down-unweighting*. Both can be produced by the skier, variations in terrain, the virtual bump, or combinations of the three. How and when the force changes between the skier and the snow depends on the direction and timing of vertical accelerations of the skier's center of gravity. See figure 6.16.

> 去重力主要分为两种类型：上部去重力和下部去重力。这两种都可以由滑雪者、地形变化、虚拟凸起或三者的组合产生。重力在滑雪者与雪地之间的变化，取决于滑雪者重心的垂直加速度的方向和时间。参见图6.16。

There are two pairs of definitions for these terms (up-unweighting and downunweighting) in common use today. One of them says that any unweighting that the skier initiates by extending at the knees and hips is an up-unweighting, and any unweighting in which the skier starts by flexing at the knees and hips is a down-unweighting. The other pair of definitions, and the one I prefer and use in this book, bases the definitions on the direction in which the skier's center of gravity moves.

> 今天的通用定义中有两组对于这些术语（向上卸重和向下卸重）的描述。其中一组认为，滑雪者通过膝盖和髋部伸展所引发的任何卸重行为都是向上卸重；而从膝盖和髋部弯曲开始的任何卸重行为则是向下卸重。另一组定义，也是我在这本书中偏好的以及采用的定义，将卸重行为与滑雪者的体重中心移动方向联系起来。

这两组定义都关注了滑雪者在进行卸重心的动作时所处的状态，但后一组则从一个更具物理力学视角的角度出发来描述这些动作。这种观点强调在特定阶段（即伸展或弯曲）后，滑雪者的重心如何改变，并将这个过程视为“向上”或“向下”的卸重行为。

In this book's terminology, up-unweighting always starts with the skier's center of gravity being pushed and accelerated upward, away from the snow. This is common, for example, in mogul skiing, where the skier flexes to absorb a bump that's still big enough to project the body upward. During this time the skier feels heavier. When the upward force decreases or disappears, the skier's upward motion is slowed by gravity, and the force between the skis and the snow is reduced. As the skier's center of gravity subsequently falls back toward the snow, the force remains reduced until the center of gravity's downward motion is slowed and stopped.

> 在这本书的术语中，上提总是始于滑雪者的重心被推离雪面并加速向上。这种情况发生时，滑雪者通过伸展身体来推动自身向上，就像第101页6.17图中的那样。它也可能在滑雪者同时通过屈曲动作减少推力的情况下发生——这是第104页6.20图和90页6.3a所示的情况。比如，在大回转滑雪中较为常见，滑雪者会屈身来吸收足够大的颠簸，并将其向上推出人体。在这个过程中，滑雪者感觉体重增加。当向上的力量减少或消失时，由于重力的作用，滑雪者的上升运动速度减慢，雪板与地面之间的力也会降低。随后，在滑雪者重心下降回雪面的过程中，这种力会持续降低，直到其向下运动的速度放缓直至停止。

#### 1.1.9 *Terrain Unweighting*

> 0.1.1.10 地形去重量化

*Terrain unweighting* is a term used to describe unweighting produced by bumps or drops rather than the skier's muscles (figure 6.19). Terrain unweighting can be either up-unweighting or down-unweighting.

> 地形脱重是一种描述由颠簸或下降而不是滑雪者肌肉产生的脱重现象的术语（图6.19）。地形脱重可以分为向上脱重和向下脱重两种情况。

When the slope suddenly falls away, you experience a down-unweighting, even though you might extend to maintain some pressure on the snow. Conversely, skiers skiing into bumps will experience an up-unweighting if they don't flex quickly and deeply enough to prevent their centers of gravity from being projected upward.

> 当斜坡突然下降时，你会体验到体重的减轻，即使你可能需要伸展以保持对雪的压力。相反，滑向凸起物的滑雪者如果不能快速而深入地弯曲以防止他们的重心被推向上方，就会经历上升的卸载。

As soon as they get comfortable skiing in small bumps, skiers learn to start turns with the help of the terrain unweighting the bumps provide. From then on, all skiers start a large percentage of their turns on bumps, from tiny lumps just a few inches high to mammoth moguls. Somewhere along the way, they learn when and how to flex to reduce terrain unweighting. It's one of the essential skills that enables them to ski expert terrain.

> 一旦他们适应了在小起伏中滑雪，滑雪者就学会了利用地形减轻颠簸来开始转弯。从那以后，所有滑雪者都会从大约百分之几十的转弯开始在颠簸上进行，从小小的几英寸高的隆起体到巨大的莫格洛夫山。在某个过程中，他们学会了何时以及如何弯曲以减少地形的减轻作用。这是让他们能够在专家级地形中滑雪的一项必备技能。

#### 1.1.11 *Rebound*

> 0.1.1.12 反弹

Rebound is a type of up-unweighting that occurs in short radius, checked turns and is commonly used on steeper slopes where speed control is important. As with moguls and sharply carved turns that produce sizable virtual bumps, the unweighting effect of rebound is not produced by a deliberate action on the part of the skier but rather is the result of the dynamics of the turn. And, as with moguls and significant virtual bumps, the skier usually mitigates its unweighting effect by flexing a bit at the knee and hip. Modern shaped skis have reduced the need for rebound, but it remains an important skill for well-rounded skiers.

> 反弹是一种在短半径，检查转弯时发生的减轻重量现象，在坡度较陡、速度控制重要的情况下常用。与制造大量虚拟隆起的跳台和锐利雕刻的转弯相似，滑雪者并非故意通过行为产生反弹的卸载效果，而是由转弯的动力学产生的结果。同样地，与跳台和显著的虚拟隆起类似，滑雪者通常通过膝盖和髋部轻微弯曲来减轻其卸载效果。现代形状的滑雪板减少了对反弹的需求，但对于全面发展的滑雪者来说，这是一个仍然重要的技能。

#### 1.1.12 *Choosing the Technique for the Task*

> 0.1.1.13 选择适合任务的技术

With so many techniques available to control the force on you in the transition, it's natural to wonder which ones to use and when. This is where considerations of terrain, snow, and tactics come in. If your line and the dynamics of the turn create a big virtual bump, you'll probably want to flex to absorb it. If you're in inconsistent, unskied snow, you might very well want to up-unweight strongly in the transition between turns to get your skis out of it. The length of the transition between two turns is also a big factor, as described in Estimating the Transition in chapter 9. A long transition favors up-unweighting. If you need to get across your skis and into the next turn as quickly as possible, down-unweighting is your best choice (figure 6.22).

> 在转换过程中有如此多的技巧来控制作用于您的力量，自然会想知道何时使用哪种方法。这就是地形、雪和战术考虑的地方。如果你的线路和转弯动力产生了一个大虚拟颠簸，你可能会想要弯曲以吸收它。如果是在不稳定的未滑雪雪地上，你很可能需要在转弯之间强烈地向上减轻重量，让你的滑雪板摆脱出来。两个转弯之间的转换长度也是一个重要因素，如第9章中估算转换时所描述的那样。长时间的转换有利于向上减轻重量。如果你需要尽快穿过滑雪板进入下一个转弯，向下减轻重量是最佳选择（图6.22）。

Practice all the techniques until they become second nature. Then, when you go out and ski, focus not on your technique but on your tactics, the snow, and the mountain. Your body will learn to pick the right moves on its own.

> 将所有技巧练习到自然而然的地步。然后，当你出去滑雪时，不要专注于你的技术，而是关注战术、雪况和山体。你的身体会学会自行挑选合适的动作。
