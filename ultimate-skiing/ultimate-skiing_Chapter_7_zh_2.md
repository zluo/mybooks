Expert skiers on ultrasteep and narrow pitches often pivot their skis more than 120 degrees at the start of the turn. #### 0.1.1.1 Leg Rotation

> 0.1.1.1 腿部旋转

*Leg rotation* is the most important technique used today to turn the skis, and the preferred technique for most situations is twisting one or both legs in the hips (figures 7.1, 7.2, and 7.3). When instructors talk about "turning your feet," this is what they mean. This technique is used in skiing maneuvers at all levels, from gliding wedge turns to dynamic parallel turns.

> 腿部旋转是当今用于转弯的最重要技术，大多数情况下首选的技术是通过髋部扭转一条或两条腿（见图7.1、7.2和7.3）。当教练们谈到“转动双脚”时，指的是这种技术。这一技术被用于所有级别的滑雪动作中，从滑行楔形转弯到动态平行转弯。

Ski instructors and coaches commonly call this technique *braquage* (pronounced *bra-'kazh*), a French term introduced by Georges Joubert that literally means "steering." If you turn both legs at the same time, or if there's weight on both your feet when you twist one leg inward, your upper body will remain motionless, and you can turn your skis without disturbing your balance.

> 滑雪教练和教练通常称这种技术为braquage（发音为bra-'kazh），这是由Georges Joubert引入的法语术语，字面意思是“转向”。如果你同时转动双腿，或者在将一条腿向内扭转时双脚同时承受重量，你的上半身将保持静止，从而可以在不破坏平衡的情况下转动滑雪板。

If, on the other hand, you twist only your outside leg inward and lift your inside foot off the snow, your upper body reacts by turning a bit toward the outside. This sets your upper body into a countered position (described in chapter 4), ready for hip angulation early in the turn. This is a technique racers have used for decades. Rainer Schoenfelder executes such a movement in the fourth and fifth frames of figure 4.14 on page 65. Because his right ski is off the snow, the muscular effort that rotates his left leg inward also causes his upper body to turn slightly toward the outside of the turn.

> 另一方面，如果你仅将外侧腿向内扭转，并将内侧脚抬起离开雪面，你的上半身便会随之向外侧略微转动。这种转动使你的上半身进入一种反向姿势（详见第4章），为转弯初期的髋部倾斜做好准备。这种技术竞速选手已经使用了数十年。如图4.14第65页的第四和第五帧所示，Rainer Schoenfelder就执行了这样的动作。由于他的右雪板已离地，将左腿向内旋转所需的肌肉力量同时也使他的上半身向转弯的外侧轻微转动。

With it a skier can produce large torques, and produce them through an entire turn, much longer than any other rotary technique. Leg rotation also allows you to manipulate your skis with your legs alone, leaving your upper body to balance against whatever forces are acting on you. Leg rotation is one of those key techniques of skiing that is, unfortunately, less than obvious to many self-taught skiers. The intuitive movement for most beginners is to twist the upper body and hips in the direction they want their skis to turn movements that usually have counterproductive side effects. Turning the leg in the hip to the degree that a good skier turns it isn't an essential movement in many sports, which might be why it doesn't come naturally for many beginning skiers.

> 腿部旋转是滑雪技巧中的一项关键技术，但不幸的是，对许多自学滑雪者来说，这项技术并不明显。大多数初学者的直觉动作是将上半身和骨盆向想要滑雪板转向的方向扭转，这种动作通常会产生反效果。将腿部在髋部转动到优秀滑雪者所做的程度，并不是许多运动中必不可少的动作，这或许就是为什么许多初学者难以自然掌握这一动作的原因。

A straight leg can't exert as much turning force on the ski as a bent one, especially when it's the outside leg. The interior rotators of the femur, which are used when the leg is extended, aren't nearly as strong in this regard as the adductors, which come into play when the leg is bent. As a result, this technique is most powerful when performed from a flexed, athletic stance.

> 直腿无法像弯曲的腿那样对滑雪板施加足够的转向力，尤其是在外侧腿的情况下。当腿部伸展时使用的股骨内旋肌，在这方面远不如在腿部弯曲时起作用的内收肌强壮。因此，这种技术在采用屈膝、运动姿势时效果最佳。

#### 0.1.1.2 exercise

> 0.1.1.2 练习

To feel the movement for yourself, sit on the edge of a chair with your feet and knees about 6 inches (15 cm) apart. Now, without moving your heels, twist your right leg so that your right big toe touches your left foot, and your right knee touches your left knee. This movement is the essence of leg rotation. You might feel as if you're twisting your knee, but you're actually turning your femur in your hip.

> 要亲自感受这个动作，坐在椅子的边缘，双脚和膝盖大约相距6英寸（15厘米）。现在，不移动脚跟，将右腿扭转，使右脚的大脚趾触碰左脚，右膝触碰左膝。这个动作就是腿部旋转的核心。你可能会感觉像是在扭动膝盖，但实际上是在髋部转动股骨。

#### 0.1.1.4 Anticipation

> 0.1.1.4 预判

*Anticipation*, also known as *windup-release*, is a technique used mostly in linked, short radius turns. Although inklings of the movement pattern can be seen in pictures of skiers from the 1950s and earlier, anticipation became commonly visible in the skiing of world-class ski racers in the mid-1960s. To this day, it remains a fundamental element of advanced short-turn technique.

> 预兆，又称蓄力-释放，是一种主要用于连贯的短半径转弯的技术。尽管早在1950年代及更早的滑雪者照片中就能看到这种动作模式的初步迹象，但到了1960年代中期，这种技术在世界级滑雪赛手的滑雪动作中变得普遍可见。时至今日，它仍然是高级短半径转弯技术中的基本要素。

From the fall line to the end of the turn, your legs wind up under your torso, which continues to face more or less down the fall line. The resulting twist in your body through the hips and lower back stretches several muscles, some of them large, including those used for leg rotation. When the skis disengage from the snow, either via unweighting or flattening as they change edges, those stretched muscles seek to shorten, realigning your legs with your upper body (figure 7.6). A blocking pole plant, described in the next section, is usually used to stabilize the upper body so that all the realignment occurs in the legs.

> 从下滑线至转弯结束时，你的双腿会向身体下方卷起，此时躯干仍持续朝向大致下滑线方向。这种通过骨盆和下背部的扭转所导致的身体旋转，会拉伸多个肌肉群，其中包括一些较大的肌肉，如用于腿部旋转的肌肉。当滑雪板因减重或在改变边时变平而脱离雪面时，这些被拉伸的肌肉会试图缩短，从而使双腿重新与上半身对齐（图7.6）。下一段中将描述的“阻拦杆插入动作”，通常用于稳定上半身，使所有重新对齐的动作都发生在腿部。

The term *anticipation* refers to the entire windup and release mechanism and can be powered by tension in parts of the body other than those already mentioned. For example, a windup in the knees and lower legs, common in short turns, can be seen in the middle frame of figure 3.7 on page 39. Telemark skiers develop windup tension in the abdominals and lower-back muscles, as shown in figure 7.7.

> “预兆”一词指的是整个蓄力和释放机制，可以由除已提及部位之外的身体其他部位的紧张来驱动。例如，膝盖和小腿的蓄力，常见于短半径转弯，可在第39页图3.7的中帧中看到。提尔马克滑雪者在腹部和下背部肌肉中发展蓄力，如图7.7所示。

The muscle tension created by the windup isn't a big source of torque for turning the skis, but it supplies enough to be useful in turns in which the skis are unweighted, or the tips and tails are free of the snow, as is common in moguls, especially when accompanied by a well-planted pole (figures 7.8 and 7.9).

> 风车动作产生的肌肉张力并不是转弯时滑雪板旋转的主要扭矩来源，但它提供的张力足以在滑雪板未受重力作用，或尖端和尾部脱离雪面（如雪块地形）的情况下发挥作用，尤其是在配合稳固插地的杖时（见图7.8和图7.9）。

One of anticipation's greatest benefits is that it enhances leg rotation. It places the hips and legs in a relationship that provides the maximum range and power for leg rotation. A muscle will develop its maximum contracting force when it's first stretched to about 120 percent of its resting length, and some of the muscles stretched in the windup phase of anticipation are the same ones used for leg rotation. Thus, the windup enables the skier to perform a more powerful leg rotation. Yet another advantage of anticipation is that it coordinates well with the increased hip angulation and countering that are often effective in the completion phase of short, linked turns. Figure 8.8 on page 129 illustrates this well.

> 预期的最显著优势之一是它能增强腿部旋转。它使髋部和腿部处于一种关系中，从而为腿部旋转提供最大的活动范围和力量。当肌肉被拉伸至其静止长度的120%时，其收缩力达到最大值。在预期的预摆阶段中，一些被拉伸的肌肉正是用于腿部旋转的肌肉。因此，预摆使滑雪者能够完成更有力的腿部旋转。预期的另一个优势是它能很好地与髋部角度增大和对抗动作相协调，而这些动作在完成短而连贯的转弯时往往非常有效。第129页的图8.8很好地说明了这一点。

#### 0.1.1.5 Torque From the Pole Plant 磁极产生的扭矩

> 0.1.1.5 扭矩 从 极杆插入

Planted properly, your pole will do something you might not have thought it could: It will make you turn. You must plant the pole at an angle: When the tip of the pole goes in the snow, it must be ahead of the hand that planted it. This is the critical element that enables the snow to exert a turning force (figures 7.10 and 7.11). All great skiers, from Hannes Schneider to Doug Coombs, have always planted their poles at this angle. With the pole planted obliquely, the snow can push backward on your hand, creating a torque on your entire body about the balance axis. This is called a *blocking pole plant*. If the pole is planted straight up and down, the snow can't do this. Figures 7.12 and 7.13 on page 114 show the physical mechanics involved.

> 正确地插入你的杖，可以做到你可能没想到它能做到的事：它会使你转弯。你必须将杖以倾斜的角度插入：当杖尖插入雪中时，它必须位于插入手的前方。这就是使雪能够产生转向力的关键要素（见图7.10和图7.11）。从汉内斯·施耐德到道格·库姆斯，所有伟大的滑雪者一直都在以这个角度插入杖。当杖以倾斜角度插入时，雪可以向你的手施加向后的力，从而在你的身体重心轴上产生扭矩。这种插入方式称为“阻拦式插杖”（blocking pole plant）。如果杖是直立插入的，雪就无法产生这种作用。第114页的图7.12和图7.13展示了其中的物理力学原理。

The ability of the snow to apply a torque to the skier is important in short radius turns. It becomes critical on very steep slopes where significant rotary force must be applied to the skis to achieve big initial steering angles.

> 雪对滑雪者施加扭矩的能力在小半径转弯中非常重要。在非常陡峭的坡面中，这种能力变得至关重要，因为必须对滑雪板施加显著的旋转力以实现较大的初始转向角度。

You can precisely and instantaneously control the amount of torque the pole plant produces by controlling the strength and duration of the contraction of your arm and shoulder muscles. The more firmly they contract, the greater the torque you'll get. The longer the muscles are contracted, the longer that torque is exerted on you. The farther away from the balance axis you plant the pole, the greater the torque will be. You should plant the pole at least a foot and a half (half a meter) away from your body.

> 你可以通过控制手臂和肩部肌肉的收缩强度和持续时间，精确且即时地控制杆植（pole plant）所产生的扭矩。肌肉收缩越有力，产生的扭矩就越大；肌肉收缩时间越长，扭矩作用在你身上的时间也越长。杆植点距离平衡轴越远，产生的扭矩也越大。你应该将杆植点至少距离身体1.5英尺（0.5米）远。

#### 0.1.1.6 Upper-Body Rotation

> 0.1.1.6 上半身旋转

*Upper-body rotation* is a classic technique for pivoting the skis and creating a steering angle. It is intuitive and powerful. Many books on skiing from the 1940s, 1950s, and earlier have beautiful black and white photographs of expert skiers rotating their way through billows of untracked powder. This was the technique taught in Hannes Schneider's Arlberg Ski School, founded in 1921 and generally considered the first ski school with a systematic method of instruction.

> 上半身旋转是一种经典的滑雪技巧，用于转动滑雪板并形成转向角度。这种技巧直觉且富有力量。许多20世纪40年代、50年代及更早时期的滑雪书籍中，都配有精美的黑白照片，展示了专家滑雪者如何通过未踩过的粉雪形成的浪花进行旋转。这种技巧是汉斯·施耐德（Hannes Schneider）于1921年创立的阿尔贝格滑雪学校所教授的，该校通常被认为是世界上第一所采用系统教学方法的滑雪学校。

These skiers swung the whole upper body—arms, shoulders, and all—into the turn. It worked well at powering their long, stiff wooden skis through the ungroomed and often unpacked snow. All well-rounded skiers still use this technique at one time or another. Often, an expert powder skier will throw off a full-on shoulder rotation to deal with a situation that calls for a lot of torque, as shown in figure 7.16. But

> 这些滑雪者将整个上半身——包括手臂、肩膀等——转动进入转弯。这种方法能有效驱动他们长而硬的木制滑雪板通过未修整且常常松散的积雪。所有技术全面的滑雪者偶尔都会使用这种技巧。通常，专家级的粉雪滑雪者会在需要大量扭矩的情况下，进行全身肩部旋转，如图7.16所示。但

This transfers the momentum of the projected body segments to the rest of the body and the skis as a torque.

> 上半身旋转涉及将外侧手臂、肩部或两者同时向预期转弯的方向投掷（图7.18）。此动作始于准备阶段，此时手臂和肩部向后拉。随后进入投掷阶段，手臂（可能随后肩部甚至髋部）被抛向前方并绕向新的转弯方向。一旦被投掷的上半身部分获得一定动量，它们便在阻挡阶段被适当肌肉的收缩所捕捉，从而与身体其余部分锁定。这将被投掷的身体部分的动量传递至身体其余部分及滑雪板，形成力矩。

#### 0.1.1.7 Hip Rotation 髋关节旋转
> 0.1.1.7 髋部转动

In hip rotation (or hip projection, as it's sometimes called), the skier initiates the turn by pushing the hips to the outside of the new turn. This flattens the skis, making it easier to slip the tails out to a good-sized steering angle, even if the skis are pressed against the snow. The movement of the hips can also produce a twisting force on the skis, like upper-body rotation. This is an effective and perfectly acceptable technique in loose snow, particularly at slower speeds or on flatter terrain, where flattening the skis helps them move laterally through the snow. An example can be seen in figure 13.4 on page 196.

> 在腰部旋转（有时也称为腰部前倾）中，滑雪者通过将腰部推向新转弯的外侧来开始转弯。这种动作会将滑雪板压平，即使滑雪板被压在雪面上，也能更容易地让尾部滑出到适当的转向角度。腰部的移动还可以在滑雪板上产生扭转力，就像上半身旋转一样。这种技术在松软雪地上特别有效，尤其是在较低速度或平坦地形上，因为压平滑雪板有助于它们横向穿过雪地。图13.4（第196页）中给出了一个例子。

To begin using hip rotation effectively in powder, imagine your body as a big corkscrew: The point of the corkscrew is between your feet, and your entire torso, from the hips up, is the handle. Remaining flexed at the knees and waist if possible, twist the handle of the corkscrew with your hips in the direction you want to turn. Avoid tipping your body or shoulders into the turn. Just make big torques by cranking the corkscrew straight down into the snow. Remember that this is a specialized technique used in special situations. When you leave the powder and crud behind, leave the big hip rotation there, too.

> 要有效运用髋部旋转技巧在粉雪中滑行，可以想象自己的身体像一个大螺旋：螺旋的尖端位于双脚之间，而从髋部向上整个躯干则是螺旋的柄。尽可能保持膝盖和腰部弯曲，用髋部扭转螺旋的柄，朝你想要转弯的方向转动。避免身体或肩膀向转弯方向倾斜。只需将螺旋垂直压入雪中，产生大的扭矩。请记住，这是一种专门用于特殊雪况的技巧。当你离开粉雪和雪块时，也把大髋部旋转留在那里。

Hip rotation is useful in small doses to start turns on packed snow, too. You can use it to deliberately make your skis slip or to help get them to your desired initial steering angle if you don't have enough leg rotation, anticipation, or torque from a blocking pole plant. But you must bring your hips back to the inside of the turn to counter and angulate properly before you reach the fall line or need the skis to grip. Skiers whose boots are overcanted (discussed in chapter 10) sometimes use this technique to get their turns going. Unfortunately, most skiers who start their turns with hip rotation never make the proper midturn adjustment and are left facing their tips with no hip angulation or counter, and underedged skis. This leads to an overreliance on knee angulation and difficulty making short, quick turns because the upper body is turning with the skis. (Hip and knee angulation are discussed in chapter 8, p. 124.)

> 适量的髋部旋转在开始在压实雪面上转弯时也很有用。你可以用它故意让滑雪板打滑，或者在缺乏足够的腿部旋转、预判或阻拦杆插入所产生的扭矩时，帮助滑雪板达到所需的初始转向角度。但在到达雪道中心线或需要滑雪板抓雪之前，你必须将髋部重新带回转弯的内侧，以正确地进行反向和倾斜。一些靴子过度倾斜的滑雪者（第10章讨论过）有时会使用这种技术来启动转弯。不幸的是，大多数以髋部旋转开始转弯的滑雪者从未正确调整转弯中的中间阶段，导致他们面对滑雪板的尖端，没有髋部倾斜或反向，且滑雪板处于不充分倾斜的状态。这会导致过度依赖膝盖倾斜，并在转弯时难以做出短而快速的转弯，因为上半身随着滑雪板一起转动。（髋部和膝盖倾斜在第8章第124页有讨论。）

Short turns are difficult using hip or upper-body rotation because the rotation gives your whole body angular momentum. This takes some time to get going, and also some time to stop, which works against the rhythm of short turns. Also, both types of rotation make your upper body follow your skis through the turn, making short turns that much harder. Great skiers can start a turn with an emergency rotation, then quickly bring everything back together for the rest of the turn, but most skiers suffer through the whole turn with the ill effects of the initiation.

> 短弯的难点在于使用髋部或上半身的旋转，因为这种旋转会使整个身体产生角动量。这种角动量需要一定时间才能启动，也需要一定时间才能停止，这与短弯所需的节奏相冲突。此外，这两种旋转方式都会导致上半身跟随滑雪板完成弯道，从而使短弯更加困难。优秀的滑雪者可以在开始转弯时进行一次应急旋转，然后迅速将身体各部分重新整合，以完成后续的转弯动作；但大多数滑雪者则会在整个转弯过程中承受启动阶段带来的不良影响。

#### 0.1.1.8 Counterrotation 反向旋转

Counterrotation is a mechanism for turning the skis when they're free of the snow, or nearly flat on it. By rotating the upper body in one direction, the legs and skis naturally turn in the other direction—a technique that animals use intuitively when they're airborne. The technique comes naturally for skiers, too, often showing up in moguls, terrain parks, and other situations in which the skis are off the snow (figures 7.19 and 7.20).

> 反向旋转是一种在滑雪板脱离雪面或几乎贴着雪面时用于转动滑雪板的机制。通过向一个方向转动上半身，腿部和滑雪板会自然地向相反方向转动——这种技巧是动物在空中时本能地使用的。滑雪者也会自然而然地掌握这一技巧，常在雪包、地形公园以及其他滑雪板脱离雪面的情况下使用（图7.19和7.20）。

![](books/ultimate-skiing/image/_page_130_Picture_4.jpeg)

In terms of physical mechanics, this technique works because of the principle of conservation of angular momentum.

#### 0.1.1.9 Anja Paerson 安佳·帕森

Through most of this decade, fans of ski racing were treated to a great rivalry between two great skiers: Anja Paerson of Sweden and Janica Kostelic of Croatia. In the six years from 2001 to 2006, Kostelic's last season, between them they won the overall World Cup five times, the slalom title five times, the giant slalom cup three times, and the combined crown four times. But while the two of them are great technicians, they have distinctly different styles. Where Janica was a technical perfectionist who rarely pushed her line to the point that she needed to make recoveries or adjustments, Anja was, and still is, a fiercely aggressive skier who pushes her line to the limit and who knows how to stay fast while recovering from a bobble. > 在整个十年中，滑雪赛的粉丝们一直欣赏着两位优秀滑雪选手之间的精彩对决：来自瑞典的安妮亚·帕森和来自克罗地亚的珍妮卡·科斯特利奇。从2001年到2006年这六年期间，也就是科斯特利奇的最后一季，两人共获得了世界杯总积分五次冠军、速降赛冠军五次、大回转杯三次冠军以及全能冠军四次。然而，尽管两人都是技术高手，但她们的滑雪风格却截然不同。珍妮卡是一位追求技术完美的选手，很少将滑行路线推至需要调整或修正的地步；而安妮亚则是一位充满攻击性的选手，她总是将滑行路线推向极限，并且懂得在出现小失误后如何保持高速度。这并不是说她是个鲁莽的选手。

Anja comes from Tarnaby, a small town in the north of Sweden within one degree of latitude of the Arctic Circle, also the hometown of Ingemar Stenmark. As Paerson tells it, skiing was what there was to do, so that's what she did. She won her first World Cup race at the age of 17. And considering that Tarnaby's skiing facilities are small, served by just two T-bars, it's no surprise that the win was a slalom. She soon found success in giant slalom, too. In 2005, like many of the great skiers, she began to find herself on the downhill and super G podiums.
