#### 4.1.4 ANATOMY AND STRUCTURE. 解剖学与结构

A TREE is <sup>a</sup> living being, feeding, digesting, breathing, transpiring, reproducing its kind, having power of holding itself erect and resisting the wind, of repairing injuries, and even, in a small degree, of moving certain of its parts.

> 一棵树是一种活着的生物，它进食、消化、呼吸、蒸腾、繁衍后代，能够挺直自身、抵御风力，修复创伤，甚至在某种程度上，还能移动其部分器官。

It feeds by its roots and leaves, digests in its wonderful internal system, breathes through its bark, transpires by its leaves, and by flowering produces seeds, that scattered, often by elaborate means, become in time trees like itself that shall perpetuate the processes.

> 它通过根和叶吸收养分，借助奇妙的内部系统进行消化，通过树皮进行呼吸，通过叶子散发水汽，并通过开花产生种子。这些种子往往以复杂的方式传播，最终长成与自己相似的树木，从而延续这一循环过程。

Trees always fighting against adverse conditions, suffering by many foes, have great powers of repairing injuries and of adapting themselves to circumstances. They can within limits largely alter their character and growth to enable them to live under conditions which are unfavourable to them, so much so indeed that though they still retain the essentials of their kind, they are without careful examination unrecognizable, as when the Spruce, so well known to us in the form of the Christmas tree, becomes in more northern latitudes a mere creeping plant, upon which one can walk as upon turf.

> 树木总是与恶劣的环境抗争，经受着众多敌手的侵害，却拥有强大的自我修复能力和适应环境的能力。它们在一定范围内可以大幅改变自身的特性与生长方式，以适应不利的生存条件。以至于尽管它们仍然保留着自身种类的本质特征，却在未经仔细观察的情况下难以被识别。例如，我们熟悉的圣诞树——云杉，在更北的纬度地区，却会变成一种匍匐生长的植物，人们甚至可以像踩草地一样在上面行走。[!note] 译者注：此处通过云杉在不同环境下的形态变化，比喻人类在逆境中适应与改变的能力。

2.—Stems are "True" or "False." In the Conifers they are "True"—that is to say, the terminal shoot, often growing upwards during the summer season, rests during the winter, and resumes its upward growth again in the spring. In the dicotyledonous trees (including practically all our outdoor trees except the Conifers) the stems are "False"—the terminal bud after growing for a season dies, and the stem is continued the following spring by the highest lateral bud. The "True" stem, therefore, is one continuous piece of timber, while the "False" stem is a succession of branches strung end to end; and it will be found that in some trees, more especially when young, the effect of this difference is quite noticeable in the superior straightness of a "True" stem over the "False" stem, which shows, as it were, a slight hesitation in its growth, or which gets divided and lost.

> 2.—枝干是“真”还是“假”。在松柏类植物中，枝干是“真”的，也就是说，顶端枝条通常在夏季向上生长，冬季则停止生长，到了春天又重新开始向上生长。而在双子叶植物树（包括几乎所有我们户外见到的树木，除了松柏类）中，枝干则是“假”的——顶端芽在生长一个季节后便会死亡，而次年春天则由最高处的侧芽继续生长。因此，“真”枝干是一整段木材，而“假”枝干则是一系列枝条首尾相连；人们会发现，在一些树木中，尤其是幼树时，这种差异对枝干笔直程度的影响非常明显，“真”枝干比“假”枝干更笔直，而“假”枝，似乎在生长过程中略显犹豫，或者出现分叉和断裂的现象。

3.—Branches are " True " or " False " in the same manner that has just been described in regard to stems ; they grow from the tree at varying angles and in varying numbers. In the younger trees the method of branching is, of course, more regular and in keeping with the habit of the tree, they not having had so much time to suffer adversities ; so it is well for the student to study young trees, as from them the true methods of growth can be better learnt.

#### 4.1.6 TREE DRAWING.

> 4.1.6 树枝画

THE best medium for tree drawing, whether it be for studies only or for producing drawings to be kept as pictures, is undoubtedly the lead pencil. Some French masters have produced very beautiful pictures of trees in charcoal, but they are rather elaborated indoor productions than outdoor studies, and are, moreover, complete landscape compositions rather than drawings of trees for the tree's sake. Charcoal is at best a troublesome medium out of doors, being so easily damaged and so difficult to carry. Some beautiful drawings of trees have also been produced with pen and ink-by Alfred Parsons, for example, that master of plant form and of the pen—but, as a rule, they are hardly satisfactory as representing trees. The use of the pen necessitates too many conventions for the true rendering of stem and branch and foliage, and the line the pen makes is a line with sharp, hard edges: whereas the pencil line is far more sympathetic by reason of its soft edges, which make it more resemble the etched line. Chalk is out of the question for most tree work, as its point wears down so rapidly, causing a wide touch, and, also, it cannot be properly rubbed out.

> 树木素描的最佳媒介，无论是用于练习还是制作可作为图画保存的作品，无疑是铅笔。一些法国大师曾用炭笔创作出非常优美的树木画面，但这些作品更多是室内创作的精致作品，而非户外写生，而且它们更像是完整的风景画，而非为了树木本身而绘制的树木画。在户外使用炭笔是一种颇为麻烦的媒介，因为其极易损坏，而且携带不便。也有一些树木画是用钢笔和墨水完成的，例如阿尔弗雷德·帕森斯的作品，他是一位植物形态和笔法的大师。然而，通常而言，这些作品在表现树木方面并不令人满意。使用钢笔需要遵循太多约定俗成的规则，才能真实地表现树干、枝条和树叶。钢笔画出的线条具有尖锐而硬朗的边缘，而铅笔线条则因边缘柔和而更具表现力，更接近刻线的效果。对于大多数树木绘画来说，使用粉笔是不可行的，因为粉笔笔尖磨损得非常快，导致笔触过于宽泛，而且也无法很好地擦除。
