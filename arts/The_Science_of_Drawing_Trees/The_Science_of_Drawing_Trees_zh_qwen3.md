## 4 ADVERTISEMENT 广告

In the Sketcher's Manual, has been given the Science of Picture-making, in black and white, or, as it is technically termed, in Chiar Oscuro : the end to be obtained, and the means of attaining it. In Colour, the means of producing pictures in colour, have been afforded. The present series of works has been undertaken with the view of providing materials for those who wish to add to pictorial qualities, the individuality of Natural Character, and with the view of affording to those who desire the power of delineating objects, without attempting to convert the representation into a picture, a sound and simple method of instruction in the art of Drawing, upon the only solid basis of Science.

> 在《速写手册》中，已经阐述了绘画的科学，即黑白画法，或按技术术语称为“明暗对照法”（Chiaroscuro），其目的是获得最终效果，并阐述实现这一目的的方法。至于彩色绘画，也已经提供了如何创作彩色图像的方法。本系列作品的编写目的，是为那些希望在绘画中增添自然个性的人提供素材，同时为那些希望掌握描绘物体的能力，而不必将其转化为完整绘画的人，提供一种简明易懂的素描艺术教学方法，其唯一坚实的基础就是科学。

## 5 PRELIMINARY REMARKS. 初步说明

It is unnecessary to enlarge upon the importance of Drawing as a means of communicating ideas . It has been tacitly admitted, if not openly asserted, in every age, and in none more generally than in the present.

> 没有必要过多强调“绘图”作为一种传达思想的手段的重要性。在每一个时代，这一点都已被默认承认，甚至公开宣称，而在当今时代尤为普遍。

In Egypt and in Mexico, Drawing was a language. There can be little doubt that it preceded the written alphabets, as being the readiest means of transmitting or recording ideas. And even now, amidst all the extension and refinements of modern tongues, there are few persons who do not have recourse to signs, or such drawings as they are capable of executing, in explanation of their words. The finger dipped in wine has described the battle and the siege. Launce cannot recount the hardheartedness of his dog to his own satisfaction till he has personified his mother by his shoe.

> 在埃及和墨西哥，绘画是一种语言。几乎可以肯定，它早于文字字母出现，因为它是传递或记录思想最便捷的方式。即使在今天，尽管现代语言已经发展得如此丰富和完善，仍然很少有人在解释自己的话时，不借助一些符号或自己所能画出的简笔画。手指蘸着酒水，便能描绘出战斗与围攻的场景。拉恩斯直到用鞋子象征他的母亲，才能让自己满意地讲述他对那只冷血狗的无情。[!note] 这里引用了莎士比亚戏剧《驯悍记》中的情节，用以说明通过象征性动作来表达情感和思想的表达方式。

It tells a chapter at a glance, and throws whole pages of a narrative into a single view. Forms, situations, proportions, and character, actions and passions, are alike by Drawing condensed, distinguished, and made intelligible in a moment. Ideas, experience, facts , may all be described ; but scarcely more intelligibly by words than by forms, in many instances decidedly less so. Machinery, whether new inventions, or such as have been long established, cannot be fully explained without Drawing, in addition to description. But in every thing that regards form, as of animals, plants, &c. or the general character of a country, or its inhabitants, Drawing is indispensable to writing, whilst it is sufficiently explanatory alone. Nothing but abstract reasoning is confined to writing .

> 这段文字以一目了然的方式讲述一个章节，将整个叙事的若干页浓缩呈现在一个视界中。形式、情境、比例、人物、行为与激情，皆通过绘图被浓缩、区分并瞬间变得清晰可懂。思想、经验与事实都可以被描述，但在许多情况下，用文字表达并不比用形式来得更清晰，有时甚至更逊一筹。无论是新发明的机械，还是早已存在的机械，若不辅以绘图，单靠描述难以充分解释。然而，在涉及形式的任何方面——如动物、植物等，或一个国家的整体风貌及其居民——绘图对于写作而言是不可或缺的，而它本身也足以解释清楚。唯有抽象推理可以完全依赖文字表达。

Hitherto Drawing has been looked upon as a fine art, attainable only by those whom Nature has endowed with what is designated taste and genius . > 4.1.4 第二部分

#### 4.1.5 Branches.

> 4.1.5 分支

THERE is undoubtedly, a very decided difference between the forms and directions of the principal branches, in various species of trees, as they may be distinguished from one another in the winter, when deprived of leaves ; and the character of the tree is materially affected thereby, when the foliage is sufficiently dense to conceal the branches altogether, as is evident from the first classification adopted.

> 无疑，在不同树种中，主干的形态和方向存在明显的差异，这种差异在冬季树叶脱落时尤为明显，此时可以通过枝干的形态来区分不同的树种。当树叶足够茂密，完全遮盖了枝干时，这种差异对树木的整体特征产生了实质性的影响，这一点从最初的分类方法就可以看出。

Some are straight, as in the Fir, Beech, and Willow.

> 有些树是笔直的，比如枫树、山毛榉和柳树。

Some bend with their own weight, as the slender branches of the Weeping Willow, and Birch .

> 有些则因自身重量而弯曲，如同垂柳那纤细的枝条，或是白桦树的枝桠。

Some are of serpentine form, as in the Horsechesnut, Ash, and sometimesthe Beech.

> 有些则呈蛇形，如山毛榉、梣树，有时则是鹅掌楸。[!note] 译者注：此处“serpentine form”指树皮的纹理呈蛇形，中文中无直接对应表达，故采用意译。

Some are gnarled, (very much twisted) as in the Oak, and the Acacia.

> 有些树则扭曲得非常厉害，比如橡树和金合欢树。[!note] 译者注：此处“gnarled”原意为“扭曲的”，根据上下文，译为“扭曲得非常厉害”更符合中文表达习惯。

Others are less regular, as the Elm, but nearer straight.

> 其他人则不那么规律，像榆树一样，但更接近笔直。

Some break or shoot out at right angles from the stem, as the Oak, and sometimes the Elm, at the lower part of the tree ; the Fir and Cedar.

> 一些枝条或枝干会以直角从主干分叉而出，例如橡树，有时山毛榉也会在树的下部出现这种情况；还有冷杉和雪松。

Others form angles of various degrees of acuteness, from the Poplar to the Acacia and the Beech.

> 其他人则以各种不同程度的锐度形成角度，从白杨树到刺槐，再到鹅掌楸。

THE Trunk of a tree, in general, diminishes more or less from the root, to a certain height: it then continues much the same thickness to the first branches, where it again spreads in various degrees in different species.

It is generally cylindrical ; but is sometimes ribbed, as in the Acacia, and Horsechesnut; and more or less knotty, or bossy, as in the Lombardy Poplar, the Oak, Old Lime, and sometimes the Elm.

> 有时表面略显颗粒感，比如阿贝勒（Abele），其表面带有黑色条纹。

#### 4.1.8 SECTION IV.

> 4.1.8 第四部分

#### 4.1.9 Forms of the Leaves.

> 4.1.9 叶子的形态

ALTHOUGH upon close inspection, the forms of the leaves of different trees afford a ready means of determining their various species, and would be commonly referred to as the most conclusive testimony by general observers ; it is not requisite, except for botanical purposes, and in very elaborate drawings, to represent the actual form of any one individual leaf when drawing a tree. But the attention must be given to the small masses, and the shape they assume, which sometimes are the only means of distinguishing one species of tree from another.

> 尽管从近处观察，不同树木的叶子形态可以作为判断其种类的可靠依据，通常也会被普通观察者视为最确凿的证据；但除了在植物学研究或非常精细的插图中，我们并不需要在画树时描绘某一片叶子的实际形态。不过，我们仍需注意那些小块的形态，以及它们所呈现出的形状，因为有时候，这些正是区分一种树木与另一种树木的唯一手段。

to lie like flakes upon the branches, as in the Beech and Cedar : and sometimes they grow all round the branches, as in the Oak .

> 像雪花般落在枝头，如同在山毛榉和冷杉上所见的那样；有时它们则环绕着枝干生长，如同在橡树上所见的那样。

The Horsechesnut is remarkable for small oval masses of large leaves.

> 马栗树最引人注目的是其小而椭圆形的大叶片。

The Walnut has leaves of similar character, but in larger masses and more detached.

> walnut 的叶子特征相似，但体积更大，且更加分散。

The Spanish Chesnut is of the same class, but the leaves are longer in proportion.

> 西班牙栗树属于同一类，但其叶子的比例更长。

Similar forms, but less distinct, being smaller and closer together, will be found in the Elm ; more open and with longer leaves in the Ash and Willow ; and more pointed in the Beech .

> 相似的形态，但轮廓不那么分明，体积更小且彼此更接近，可在榆树中找到；在橡树和柳树中则更为舒展，叶子也更长；而在山毛榉中则更加尖锐。

There are some exceptions to this general form, as the broad and palmated Plane, and Sycamore, the former slightly curling up, the latter drooping and more closely arranged ; the minute irregular ragged forms in the Oak ; and the minute regular form of the Pine, Cedar, and Fir, Cypress, and Yew.

> 不过，这种形态也有一些例外情况。例如，广而扁平的叶片，如悬铃木，其叶缘略微卷曲；而枫树的叶子则下垂且排列更为紧密。此外，橡树的叶片还有一种细小而不规则的锯齿状形态；而松树、雪松、云杉、柏树和山毛榉则呈现出细小而规则的形态。
